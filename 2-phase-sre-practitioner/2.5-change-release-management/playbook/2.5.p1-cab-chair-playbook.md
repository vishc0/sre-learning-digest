# CAB Chair Playbook

The CAB Chair is the single accountable owner for change governance. Not a committee chair — an accountable decision-maker who can and does say no, defer, and reject.

---

## Pre-Meeting (3 business days before CAB)

1. Confirm all RFCs submitted on time (5-business-day deadline). Return any late submissions without review — enforce the deadline to protect the team's preparation time.

2. Distribute all RFCs to CAB members via email with 48-hour pre-read expectation. Include: RFC ID, change title, risk tier, proposed window.

3. Review each RFC yourself (not just the summary). Questions to ask while reading:
   - Does the blast radius match the technical scope?
   - Is the rollback trigger observable?
   - Is the test evidence sufficient?

4. Prepare clarifying questions for each RFC. Send async before the meeting — gives the change owner time to prepare answers.

5. Flag any RFC that has a critical gap: incomplete rollback plan, missing blast radius, no test evidence. Email the change owner:

   > "Your RFC [ID] has the following gap that will result in deferral unless addressed before the meeting: [specific gap]."

   This gives them a chance to fix it before CAB. This is a coaching action, not a punitive one.

---

## During the Meeting

Structure each RFC review: max 10 minutes. Role of Chair: timekeeper, decision driver, recorder.

1. **Change owner presents: 3 minutes.** What is changing, why, and what is the risk? Not a re-read of the RFC.
2. **CAB questions: 5 minutes.** Chair priority-queues the async questions that were not resolved.
3. **Decision: 2 minutes.** Chair calls the decision: Approve / Approve with conditions / Defer / Reject. No decision-by-consensus — the Chair decides, members advise.
4. **Decision recorded immediately** in meeting notes (Jira/ServiceNow): RFC ID, decision, rationale, conditions if applicable, any open items.

---

## Decision Recording Standard

**Approved:**
> "RFC-1042 approved. Risk P2/Medium. Rollback plan reviewed and validated. Window: Saturday 02:00–04:00 ET. Owner: [name]."

**Deferred:**
> "RFC-1043 deferred. Missing rollback trigger criteria. Owner to resubmit by [date] with specific rollback decision point defined."

**Rejected:**
> "RFC-1044 rejected. Proposed approach affects 3 shared services without coordination plan. Recommend: break into phased approach with one service per change. Owner to submit revised RFC."

---

## Emergency CAB Convening Procedure

1. CAB Chair receives emergency convening request from IC or CISO.
2. Within 15 minutes: confirm emergency criteria are met (see `ch06`). If criteria not met, decline and redirect to Normal process with priority scheduling.
3. Notify minimum CAB quorum (Chair + one domain SME). Use direct communication — call or IM, not email.
4. 15-minute emergency CAB: the five questions only (what, why, blast radius, rollback trigger, rollback owner). Record verbal approvals.
5. Notify remaining CAB members within 1 hour. 2-hour window for objections.
6. Document in change management tool within 4 hours.

---

## Post-Meeting

1. Send meeting minutes within 2 hours: approved changes with windows, deferred changes with reasons, rejected changes with guidance.
2. Update change calendar: approved changes added, windows blocked.
3. Notify change owners of their outcomes directly (not just via ticket).
4. Schedule follow-up for any "approve with conditions" — track that the condition is met before the change window opens.
5. Ensure PIR scheduling for all Normal changes: each approved change should have a PIR scheduled within 5 business days of the change window.

---

## Monthly CAB Chair Retrospective

Pull and review: change volume by type, approval/defer/reject rates, emergency change frequency, PIR completion rate.

Write a 3-bullet retrospective:
- What went well
- What needs improvement
- One action item for next month
