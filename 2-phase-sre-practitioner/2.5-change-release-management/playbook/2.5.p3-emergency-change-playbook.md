# Emergency Change Playbook

Emergency changes bypass standard CAB process. This playbook is the compressed process that maintains governance while enabling speed. It is not a permission slip to skip governance — it is a faster path through governance with mandatory post-hoc accountability.

---

## Step 1: Declare an Emergency Change (0–5 minutes)

**Who can declare:**
- Incident Commander — Category A
- CISO or Security Lead — Category B
- Director/VP + Legal sign-off — Category C

**What declaration means:** you are asserting that one of the three emergency criteria is met. You are accepting personal accountability for the post-hoc PIR.

**Criteria check — answer YES to at least ONE:**
- [ ] Is there an active production incident where this change is the required mitigation?
- [ ] Is there a confirmed security vulnerability with evidence of active exploitation?
- [ ] Is there a regulatory deadline with documented legal consequence?

If NO to all three: do not declare emergency. Submit Normal change with priority scheduling request.

---

## Step 2: Notify CAB Chair (0–5 minutes)

Call or IM the CAB Chair directly. State:

> "I am declaring an emergency change. Category: [A/B/C]. Service: [service name]. What is changing: [one sentence]. ETA to implement: [X minutes/hours]."

Do NOT wait for email response. The CAB Chair has 15 minutes to respond before you proceed.

---

## Step 3: Complete Abbreviated RFC (5–15 minutes)

Open the emergency change template (`templates/emergency-change-template.md`). Complete all mandatory fields:

- What is changing (specific, not vague)
- Why it cannot wait for Normal process
- Blast radius (which services and customers are affected)
- Implementation steps (numbered, owner per step)
- Rollback trigger (specific observable condition)
- Rollback steps (numbered)
- Who is authorizing (by name)

---

## Step 4: Emergency CAB Review (15 minutes)

CAB Chair convenes emergency CAB (minimum: Chair + one domain SME).

**The five questions (15-minute timebox):**
1. What is changing and why is this an emergency?
2. What is the blast radius if the change fails?
3. What is the rollback trigger (specific, observable)?
4. Who is executing the rollback and are they available?
5. What is the implementation duration estimate?

CAB Chair calls the decision. Record all approvals verbally — names and time.

---

## Step 5: Implement the Change

- Assign a scribe to document every action taken during implementation with timestamp and actor. This is the audit trail.
- Communicate the change to affected team leads before implementation starts.
- Execute implementation steps in order. Do not deviate from RFC steps without notifying CAB Chair.

---

## Step 6: Verify and Close (immediately post-implementation)

**Verify:** confirm success criteria are met (as defined in RFC). Confirm monitoring shows expected behavior.

**If rollback is triggered:** execute rollback plan exactly as written. Notify CAB Chair. Document in change ticket.

**Close the change ticket:** record actual implementation duration, any deviations, final status (success / rollback / partial).

---

## Step 7: Post-Hoc PIR (within 24 hours)

- **Category A:** combined with incident postmortem if possible — see Incident Management playbook.
- **Category B and C:** standalone emergency change PIR.

**PIR questions:**
- Was the declaration justified?
- Did the change work?
- What deviated?
- Was rollback needed?
- What process change prevents an emergency next time?

**Output:** completed PIR in change management tool, action items with owners.
