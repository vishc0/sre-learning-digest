# SLA Breach Playbook

## Purpose and Scope

This playbook governs the response to a confirmed or imminent SLA breach. It covers every step from breach declaration through credit issuance and remediation commitment delivery. It applies to all external SLAs (customer-facing) and may be adapted for significant internal SLA violations.

**Owner**: VP of Operations
**Activator**: VP of Operations or designated Director delegate
**Prerequisites**: Confirmed breach calculation (actual availability vs. SLA commitment); breach record created in ServiceNow

---

## Phase 1: Breach Declaration (T+0 to T+30 minutes)

### Step 1.1: Confirm the Breach Calculation

Before declaring, verify the numbers are correct. Breach declaration is a commercial event — a false declaration creates more damage than a delayed accurate one.

Checklist:
- [ ] Pull availability data from the authoritative SLI measurement system (Datadog/Grafana)
- [ ] Confirm measurement methodology matches the SLA definition (calendar month, specific vantage point)
- [ ] Confirm which incident records contributed to the downtime calculation
- [ ] Verify exclusion windows have been correctly applied (scheduled maintenance, force majeure)
- [ ] Calculate actual availability percentage: `(Total Minutes − Unavailable Minutes) / Total Minutes × 100`
- [ ] Compare actual availability to SLA commitment
- [ ] If breach is imminent (not yet confirmed at month-end): verify the math shows breach is certain regardless of remaining month performance

**Declare breach when**: Actual availability is confirmed below SLA threshold, OR when accumulated downtime mathematically guarantees breach before month-end.

### Step 1.2: Create the Breach Record

Create a breach record in ServiceNow (or equivalent SLM system):

Required fields:
- Service name and SLA reference number
- Measurement period (calendar month)
- SLA commitment (%)
- Actual availability (%)
- Downtime in minutes (total and per contributing incident)
- Declaration date and time
- Declaring officer (VP name)
- Notification deadline (from SLA notification clause)

### Step 1.3: Alert the Response Team

Within 30 minutes of declaration:
- [ ] Notify Account Manager for the affected customer account
- [ ] Notify Legal (for enterprise accounts or accounts with credit dispute history)
- [ ] Notify Customer Success Manager
- [ ] Brief the Director of SRE on breach status and contributing incident records
- [ ] Notify the SLM Manager (initiate credit calculation)

---

## Phase 2: Customer Notification (T+30 minutes to T+4 hours)

### Step 2.1: Prepare the Notification

Use the [breach-notification-template.md]. Before sending, verify:
- [ ] Factual accuracy: availability number, incident dates, duration
- [ ] Credit calculation: pre-calculate and include in notification
- [ ] Remediation statement: specific (not vague), time-bound, VP-committed
- [ ] Legal review: for any notification to a top-10 revenue account or accounts with escalated history

### Step 2.2: Send the Notification

**Who sends it**: VP of Operations or designated Director delegate — not Account Manager, not SRE lead.

**Channel**: Email to the customer's designated technical and operational contacts per SLA notification clause. CC Account Manager and Customer Success Manager.

**Timing**: Within the notification window specified in the SLA (typically 2–4 hours for an active-incident breach; 5 business days for month-end retrospective breach).

**What the email must include**:
1. Plain statement of breach (no softening language before the facts)
2. Measurement period, actual availability, committed availability
3. Total downtime in minutes
4. Brief summary of contributing incidents (factual, non-technical)
5. Credit amount (calculated and stated, not "will be determined")
6. Remediation commitment summary (specific actions with dates)
7. Follow-up schedule ("I will contact you by [date] to discuss root cause and improvement plan")
8. VP's direct contact information

### Step 2.3: Document the Notification

- [ ] Log notification send time and recipient list in the breach record
- [ ] Save a copy of the sent notification in the SLA library record for this customer
- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for the executive briefing call within 48 hours

---

## Phase 3: Credit Calculation and Issuance (T+1 to T+5 business days)

### Step 3.1: Calculate the Credit

Use the credit schedule from the applicable SLA:

```
Credit Amount = Monthly Service Fee × Credit Percentage (from credit schedule tier)
```

Determine which credit tier applies:
- Identify the actual availability percentage
- Map to the credit schedule in the SLA contract
- Apply any repeat breach multiplier if applicable (check breach history for this customer)
- Apply credit cap if calculated credit exceeds cap

Document the calculation:
- Monthly service fee (from contract)
- Actual availability (%)
- Applicable credit tier
- Credit percentage
- Credit amount
- Credit cap check
- Repeat breach multiplier (if applicable)
- Final credit amount

### Step 3.2: Issue the Credit

- [ ] Submit credit memo to Finance/Billing with: customer name, contract number, credit amount, applicable period, breach record reference number
- [ ] Confirm credit will appear on next invoice (or current invoice if billing has not closed)
- [ ] Confirm credit processing timeline with Finance
- [ ] Send written confirmation to customer: "Your credit of $[X] has been processed and will appear on your [month] invoice."

### Step 3.3: Update the Breach Record

- [ ] Record credit amount and issuance date in breach record
- [ ] Record Finance ticket reference number
- [ ] Update breach status to "Credit Issued"

## Phase 4: Executive Escalation (T+24 to T+48 hours)

### Step 4.1: Determine Escalation Level

| Customer Profile | Escalation Level |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Top 10 revenue or strategic account | VP-to-VP phone call within 24 hours |
| Mid-tier enterprise account | VP or Director call within 48 hours |
| Standard enterprise account | Written follow-up within 5 business days |

### Step 4.2: Prepare for the Call

Before calling the customer executive:
- Review incident timeline and root cause findings (even if preliminary)
- Know the credit amount and issuance timeline
- Know the remediation commitment (specific, dated)
- Know any open action items from prior SLM interactions with this customer
- Brief Account Manager — they should be aware a VP call is being made
- Do not call without a named commitment to share

### Step 4.3: Execute the Call

Follow the executive escalation call structure from [ch07 — Customer Communication]:
1. Personal acknowledgment of the impact
2. Status summary (what happened, where things stand)
3. Accountability statement
4. Specific remediation commitments
5. Customer's turn to share their experience
6. Agreed next steps and follow-up timing

### Step 4.4: Send Call Summary

Within 24 hours of the call, email a call summary to the customer executive:
- Participants and date
- Key points discussed
- Commitments made (with owner and due date)
- Next contact date

## Phase 5: Remediation Commitment (T+10 to T+15 business days)

### Step 5.1: Confirm Root Cause

Before delivering the remediation commitment letter, confirm root cause from the Problem record:
- [ ] Problem record created for this breach? (Required if repeat failure mode or systemic weakness)
- [ ] Root cause confirmed by SRE lead?
- [ ] Engineering fix identified and resourced?
- [ ] Timeline for fix delivery confirmed with engineering management?

Do not issue a remediation commitment letter with speculative root cause or an unresourced fix timeline. The letter is a VP commitment — it must reflect achievable promises.

### Step 5.2: Draft the Remediation Commitment Letter

The letter is VP-signed, Legal-reviewed (for major accounts), and contains:
1. Reference to the breach (date, service, availability delta)
2. Credit confirmation
3. Root cause findings (confirmed, not speculative)
4. Specific remediation actions (not "improve monitoring" — specific engineering changes)
5. Timeline for each action with responsible owner
6. Reporting commitment (how customer will receive evidence of improvement)
7. Escalation path if customer has concerns about progress

### Step 5.3: Deliver and Document

- [ ] Send letter to customer (VP email, with Account Manager CC)
- [ ] File letter in SLA library under this customer's breach record
- [ ] Create tracking items for each remediation commitment
- [ ] Schedule follow-up service report that will include evidence of remediation

---

## Phase 6: Breach Record Close-Out (T+30 to T+90 days)

### Step 6.1: Track Remediation Completion

Each remediation commitment from the letter must have a tracked completion date. The SLM Manager tracks these monthly. VP reviews at the SLM Review Board quarterly.

### Step 6.2: Provide Follow-Up Evidence to Customer

The first monthly service report after the breach should include:
- Reference to the breach and remediation commitment
- Evidence that the committed changes were implemented (deployment date, architecture change description)
- Availability data showing post-remediation performance

If availability has improved: share proactively and with specificity.
If availability has not improved: notify the VP before the report goes out. This is an emerging repeat breach risk.

### Step 6.3: Close the Breach Record

The breach record is closed when:
- Credit has been issued and confirmed
- Remediation commitment letter has been delivered
- All remediation commitments have been completed
- One full measurement period has passed without a breach on the same service

Breach records are never deleted — they are closed and retained in the SLA library.

## Escalation Paths

| Situation | Escalate To |
|-----------|-------------|
| Customer disputes the breach calculation | VP of Operations + Legal |
| Customer invokes contract termination rights | VP of Operations + Legal + Account Executive |
| Third breach in 12-month period | VP of Operations + VP of Engineering + CTO |
| Regulatory reporting obligation triggered | Legal + Regulatory Affairs |
| Customer threatens public disclosure | VP of Operations + Legal + Communications |

---

## Playbook Anti-Patterns

These failures have been observed in SLM programs and must be avoided:

**Delay the notification to "confirm the calculation"**: The SLA has a notification window. Exceeding it creates a secondary breach. Calculate quickly — precision can follow.

**Send the notification from the Account Manager**: The customer signed with an executive. They expect to hear from an executive. Account Manager-initiated breach communication signals the organization is not taking it seriously.

**Issue a vague remediation commitment**: "We will improve our monitoring" is not a commitment. "We will implement automated circuit breaking on the notification-dispatch to auth-service dependency by July 31, 2026, and confirm via a follow-up service report" is a commitment.

**Close the breach record before remediation is complete**: The breach record tracks the full lifecycle. Closing it early creates false closure and loses the remediation accountability thread.
