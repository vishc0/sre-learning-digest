# SLA Credit Calculation Guide

## Overview

SLA credits are the commercial consequence of a breach. Calculating them correctly — quickly, and before the customer asks — is a VP-level operational discipline. This guide covers the standard credit formula, common variations, worked examples for multiple breach scenarios, and the edge cases that create disputes.

---

## The Standard Credit Formula

```
Credit Amount = Monthly Service Fee × Credit Percentage
```

Where:
- **Monthly Service Fee**: The fees paid or payable by the customer for the affected service in the breached calendar month
- **Credit Percentage**: The percentage from the credit schedule tier that corresponds to the actual availability achieved

---

## Standard Credit Schedule (Reference)

This is the baseline. Actual schedules vary by contract — always reference the specific SLA document.

| Actual Availability Achieved | Credit % of Monthly Service Fee |
|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Met SLA commitment | 0% (no credit) |
| < SLA but ≥ (SLA − 1.0%) | 10% |
| < (SLA − 1.0%) but ≥ (SLA − 5.0%) | 25% |
| < (SLA − 5.0%) | 50% |

### Example: 99.9% SLA Commitment

| Actual Availability | Tier | Credit % |
|--------------------|------|---------|
| ≥ 99.9% | No breach | 0% |
| 99.0%–99.89% | Tier 1 | 10% |
| 95.0%–98.99% | Tier 2 | 25% |
| < 95.0% | Tier 3 | 50% |

### Example: 99.99% SLA Commitment (Gold Tier)

| Actual Availability | Tier | Credit % |
|--------------------|------|---------|
| ≥ 99.99% | No breach | 0% |
| 99.90%–99.989% | Tier 1 | 10% |
| 95.0%–99.89% | Tier 2 | 25% |
| < 95.0% | Tier 3 | 50% |

## Credit Cap Calculation

Most SLAs cap total monthly credits at 50% of the monthly service fee. This prevents an extended outage from generating unlimited credit obligations.

```
Credit Cap = Monthly Service Fee × Cap Percentage (typically 50%)
```

If the calculated credit exceeds the cap, the credit issued equals the cap.

### Cap Example

Monthly service fee: $100,000
Calculated credit: $50,000 (50% credit tier)
Credit cap: $100,000 × 50% = $50,000
Credit issued: $50,000 (at the cap)

If the same month had two separate breaches, each generating $30,000:
Total calculated: $60,000
Credit cap: $50,000
Credit issued: $50,000 (cap applies to the month total, not per-breach)

---

## Repeat Breach Multiplier

Contracts with repeat breach escalation provisions apply a multiplier based on breach history in the trailing 12 months.

```
Credit Amount = Monthly Service Fee × Credit Percentage × Multiplier
```

Always check: is the Credit Cap applied before or after the multiplier? Typically: apply the multiplier to the credit percentage, then apply the cap to the total.

| Breach Occurrence | Multiplier | Example Credit (base 10%, $100K fee) |
|-----------------|-----------|--------------------------------------|
| First in 12 months | 1.0× | $10,000 |
| Second in 12 months | 1.5× | $15,000 |
| Third or more | 2.0× | $20,000 |

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Simple Tier 1 Breach

**Contract**: 99.9% SLA, $50,000/month service fee, standard credit schedule
**Breach**: October 2026, actual availability = 99.82%

Step 1: Identify the applicable tier
- SLA commitment: 99.9%
- Actual: 99.82%
- Delta: −0.08%
- Tier mapping: 99.0%–99.89% → Tier 1 → 10%

Step 2: Calculate credit
- Credit = $50,000 × 10% = **$5,000**

Step 3: Apply cap check
- Cap = $50,000 × 50% = $25,000
- $5,000 < $25,000 → **Cap not triggered**

**Credit issued: $5,000**

---

### Example 2: Tier 2 Breach with Repeat Multiplier

**Contract**: 99.9% SLA, $200,000/month service fee, repeat breach multiplier provision
**Breach history**: First breach was August 2026 (this is the second breach in 12 months)
**Breach**: October 2026, actual availability = 98.50%

Step 1: Identify tier
- 98.50% falls in: 95.0%–98.99% → Tier 2 → 25%

Step 2: Apply repeat breach multiplier
- Second breach in 12 months → 1.5× multiplier
- Adjusted credit % = 25% × 1.5 = 37.5%

Step 3: Calculate credit
- Credit = $200,000 × 37.5% = $75,000

Step 4: Apply cap check
- Cap = $200,000 × 50% = $100,000
- $75,000 < $100,000 → **Cap not triggered**

**Credit issued: $75,000**

---

### Example 3: Multiple Breaches in One Month (Two Services)

**Contract**: Two services covered by same SLA; separate credits per service
- Service A: 99.9% SLA, $60,000/month
- Service B: 99.9% SLA, $40,000/month
**Breach**: October 2026
- Service A: actual availability = 99.75% → Tier 1 → 10%
- Service B: actual availability = 97.50% → Tier 2 → 25%

Service A credit: $60,000 × 10% = $6,000
Service B credit: $40,000 × 25% = $10,000

Cap check per service:
- Service A cap: $60,000 × 50% = $30,000 → $6,000 not capped
- Service B cap: $40,000 × 50% = $20,000 → $10,000 not capped

**Total credit issued: $16,000 ($6,000 + $10,000)**

Note: Check your SLA — some SLAs apply a single cap to the total contract value, not per-service caps.

---

### Example 4: Breach at the Credit Cap

**Contract**: 99.9% SLA, $500,000/month service fee, 50% credit cap
**Breach**: November 2026, actual availability = 85.0% → Tier 3 (< 95%) → 50% credit

Credit calculation: $500,000 × 50% = $250,000
Cap: $500,000 × 50% = $250,000

**Credit issued: $250,000 (at the cap — cap equals the tier 3 credit)**

In this scenario, the cap and the maximum credit tier happen to equal the same amount. Any additional breaches in the same month would not generate additional credit (cap already reached).

---

### Example 5: Accumulated Breach (Multiple Small Incidents)

**Situation**: Five separate incidents in the month, each individually small, accumulating to breach

| Incident | Date | Duration |
|----------|------|---------|
| INC-001 | Jul 3 | 12 min |
| INC-002 | Jul 9 | 8 min |
| INC-003 | Jul 15 | 11 min |
| INC-004 | Jul 22 | 7 min |
| INC-005 | Jul 28 | 9 min |
| **Total** | | **47 min** |

**Contract**: 99.9% SLA (43.2-minute allowance for 30-day month), $75,000/month
- Total downtime: 47 minutes
- Allowable: 43.2 minutes
- Actual availability: (43,200 − 47) / 43,200 × 100 = 43,153 / 43,200 × 100 = 99.891%
- Delta from SLA: 99.891% − 99.9% = −0.009%
- Tier: 99.0%–99.89% → Tier 1 → 10%

Credit: $75,000 × 10% = **$7,500**

This illustrates that five small incidents can generate the same tier-1 credit as one large incident. The accumulated breach scenario is commercially identical to the single-event breach under most SLA credit schedules.

## Common Credit Calculation Disputes and Resolutions

### Dispute: "Our monitoring shows more downtime than yours"

Resolution path:
1. Request the customer's monitoring data (timestamps, measurement methodology)
2. Compare measurement vantage points: the SLA specifies which vantage point governs
3. Explain any legitimate differences (customer-side network issues, CDN propagation delay vs. origin service downtime)
4. If discrepancy is material and the customer's measurement shows breach while vendor's does not: escalate to VP — do not dismiss the customer's data

**Key principle**: The SLA document specifies the authoritative measurement. But a VP who mechanically defends their measurement while ignoring obvious customer-experienced unavailability will damage the relationship more than the credit cost.

### Dispute: "The credit doesn't cover our business losses"

Resolution path:
- Acknowledge the business impact without accepting legal liability beyond the credit
- Reference the "credits as sole remedy" clause if present in the SLA
- Offer non-credit remediation value (SLA tier upgrade, dedicated support resources, architecture review)
- Escalate to Legal if the customer is making formal consequential damage claims

### Dispute: "You haven't issued the credit yet"

Resolution path:
- Confirm the credit processing ticket reference with Finance
- Provide the expected invoice date where the credit will appear
- If the credit has been delayed beyond the promised timeline: escalate to Finance and communicate the revised date to the customer before they ask again

## Credit Request Window Enforcement

Most SLAs require customers to request credits within 30 calendar days of the month-end in which the breach occurred. This is a contractual protection for the vendor.

**However**: Do not use the credit request window to avoid issuing a credit that is clearly owed. The VP who refuses a credit on a technicality (customer submitted 35 days after month-end instead of 30) will pay for that decision in relationship capital many times over. The credit request window protects against retroactive claims months later — not against a customer who was two weeks late because they were dealing with the operational impact of the breach.

The rule: for recent breaches (< 60 days), issue the credit regardless of the window. For breaches raised > 90 days after month-end, review with Legal before making a determination.

## Credit Calculation Quick Reference Card

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Confirm actual availability % (from SLI measurement system) |
| 2 | Confirm measurement period (calendar month) and exclusions applied |
| 3 | Look up the monthly service fee from the contract |
| 4 | Look up the credit schedule tier in the applicable SLA |
| 5 | Check breach history: is a repeat breach multiplier applicable? |
| 6 | Calculate: Fee × Credit% × Multiplier |
| 7 | Apply credit cap: if calculated > cap, issue cap amount |
| 8 | Submit credit memo to Finance |
| 9 | Communicate credit amount to customer in breach notification |
| 10 | Confirm credit appears on next invoice |
