## 1. Purpose

This Service Level Agreement ("SLA") defines the performance standards that [Vendor] ("Vendor") commits to maintain for the services listed in Exhibit A, as provided to [Customer] ("Customer") under the Master Services Agreement ("MSA") dated [MSA Date], Contract Number [Contract #].

This SLA governs the measurement of service availability, the consequences of performance shortfalls, and the processes for breach notification and remediation.

## 3. Availability Commitment

### 3.1 Availability Target

Vendor will maintain **Service Availability of [X.X]% or greater** during each calendar month for each service listed in Exhibit A.

| Service | Availability Target | Tier |
|---------|--------------------|----|
| [Service Name 1] | [X.X]% | [Gold/Silver/Bronze] |
| [Service Name 2] | [X.X]% | [Gold/Silver/Bronze] |

### 3.2 Availability Definition

**"Service Availability"** for each calendar month is calculated as:

```
Service Availability = 
    (Total Minutes in Calendar Month − Unavailable Minutes) 
    ÷ Total Minutes in Calendar Month × 100
```

**"Unavailable Minutes"** are calendar minutes during which more than [5]% of synthetic monitoring probes to the primary [API endpoint / service interface] return HTTP 5xx responses or fail to respond within [30] seconds. Probes are executed from [monitoring provider] at [1-minute] intervals.

**"Total Minutes in Calendar Month"** is the total number of calendar minutes in the measurement period, excluding Excluded Periods as defined in Section 4.

### 3.3 Measurement Period

Availability is calculated on a **calendar month** basis. The measurement period begins at 00:00:00 UTC on the first day of each calendar month and ends at 23:59:59 UTC on the last day of the calendar month.

### 3.4 Measurement Authority

Vendor's monitoring system [specify: Datadog / Grafana / Statuspage] is the authoritative measurement system for purposes of availability calculation under this SLA. In the event of a dispute, Customer may request access to Vendor's monitoring data for the disputed period.

## 4. Excluded Periods

The following periods are excluded from availability calculation. Downtime occurring during Excluded Periods does not count as Unavailable Minutes.

### 4.1 Scheduled Maintenance

Vendor will provide [72-hour] advance written notice for planned maintenance. Maintenance is excluded from availability calculation up to a maximum of [4] hours per calendar month. Maintenance exceeding [4] hours per month will count against the availability commitment for hours in excess of the allowance.

### 4.2 Force Majeure

Events outside Vendor's reasonable control, including natural disasters, acts of government, large-scale internet infrastructure failures, or utility provider failures.

### 4.3 Customer-Caused Failures

Service unavailability caused by: Customer's actions or omissions; Customer's failure to follow Vendor's documented integration requirements; Customer exceeding agreed rate limits; Customer-side network or infrastructure failures.

### 4.4 Third-Party Failures (Customer-Directed)

Unavailability caused by a third-party provider or service designated by Customer for use in the service delivery.

## 5. Breach and Service Credits

### 5.1 Breach Definition

A breach of this SLA occurs when actual Service Availability for a calendar month falls below the target specified in Section 3.1.

### 5.2 Service Credit Schedule

If a breach occurs, Customer is entitled to a service credit calculated as follows:

| Service Availability Achieved | Service Credit (% of Monthly Service Fee) |
|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| < [X.X]% but ≥ [Y.Y]% | [10]% |
| < [Y.Y]% but ≥ [Z.Z]% | [25]% |
| < [Z.Z]% | [50]% |

**"Monthly Service Fee"** means the fees paid or payable by Customer to Vendor for the affected service in the calendar month in which the breach occurred.

### 5.3 Repeat Breach Escalation

If Customer experiences breaches in [two or more] calendar months within any [12]-month rolling period:

| Breach Occurrence | Credit Multiplier |
|-----------------|------------------|
| First breach in 12-month period | Standard credit (as above) |
| Second breach in 12-month period | [1.5]× standard credit |
| Third or subsequent breach | [2.0]× standard credit |

### 5.4 Credit Cap

Total service credits in any single calendar month shall not exceed **[50]% of the Monthly Service Fee** for the affected service. Credits are Customer's sole and exclusive financial remedy for SLA breaches and do not create liability for consequential, indirect, or special damages.

### 5.5 Credit Issuance

Credits will be applied to Customer's next invoice. Credits are not payable as cash. Credits expire if not used within [12] months of issuance.

### 5.6 Credit Request Requirement

Customer must request service credits within **[30] calendar days** of the end of the calendar month in which the breach occurred. Credits will not be issued for breach periods not reported within this window.

## 6. Breach Notification

### 6.1 Vendor Notification Obligation

Vendor will notify Customer of a confirmed SLA breach within:
- **[4] business hours** of breach declaration for breaches occurring during an active incident
- **[5] business days** of calendar month close for month-end retrospective breach confirmations

Notification will be sent to Customer's designated contact(s) listed in Exhibit B.

### 6.2 Notification Content

Breach notifications will include: service name, measurement period, actual availability percentage, committed availability percentage, total unavailable minutes, and contributing incident summary.

---

## 7. Escalation Rights

### 7.1 Remediation Plan

Customer may request a written remediation plan within [5] business days of a breach notification. Vendor will provide the remediation plan within [10] business days of receipt of the request.

### 7.2 Executive Escalation

If a breach recurs within [90] days of a prior breach for the same service, Customer may request an executive-level meeting between Customer's and Vendor's senior operational leadership within [5] business days.

### 7.3 Termination Rights (Chronic Breach)

If Vendor fails to meet the availability commitment for [three or more] calendar months in any [12]-month rolling period for the same service, Customer may terminate the MSA with respect to the affected service upon [30] days written notice, without early termination penalties, subject to the credit cap in Section 5.4 being Customer's sole financial remedy.

---

## 8. Service Reporting

### 8.1 Monthly Service Report

Vendor will provide a monthly service report to Customer's designated contacts by the [10th] business day of the following month. The report will include:

- Actual availability vs. SLA commitment for each service
- Incident summary (customer-impacting incidents)
- Breach status and credit calculation (if applicable)
- Prior month trend comparison

### 8.2 Real-Time Status

Vendor will maintain a customer-accessible status page at [URL] showing current service status and incident history. Customers may subscribe to notifications via [email / webhook].

## 9. SLA Review and Amendment

### 9.1 Annual Review

This SLA will be reviewed annually in conjunction with MSA renewal. Either party may request adjustments to the availability commitments, credit schedule, or excluded periods based on demonstrated service performance.

### 9.2 Mid-Term Adjustment Triggers

Either party may request an SLA review if:
- Actual service availability has consistently exceeded the availability commitment by > [0.5]% for [3] consecutive months
- A significant service architecture change materially affects the basis for the current commitment
- A regulatory change creates new performance requirements
