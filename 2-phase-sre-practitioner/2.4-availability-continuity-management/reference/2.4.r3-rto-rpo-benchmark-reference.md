# RTO/RPO Benchmark Reference

Sources: Gartner IT Key Metrics Data 2023, IDC Disaster Recovery Survey 2023, Uptime Institute Annual Global Data Center Survey 2023, IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2023, Ponemon Institute.

Use these benchmarks for board presentations, BIA negotiation, and investment cases. Always state the source and date when presenting.

**Methodology**: These figures represent direct costs only (lost revenue, emergency labor, SLA credits). Indirect costs (brand damage, customer churn, regulatory penalties, engineering distraction) typically add 2x–4x. Use 2x multiplier in conservative board presentations.

## Ransomware Recovery Benchmarks

IBM Security X-Force Threat Intelligence Index 2023 and Sophos State of Ransomware 2023:

| Metric | Without Air-Gapped Backup | With Air-Gapped Backup |
|--------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Average recovery time | 47 days | 23 days |
| % organizations that paid ransom | 46% | 28% |
| Average ransom paid | $812K | $812K (same; payment doesn't guarantee recovery) |
| % who recovered all data after payment | 8% | N/A |
| Average total recovery cost (including lost business) | $4.7M | $2.1M |

**Implication for BCP**: If ransomware is a credible threat (it is, for all organizations), the cost of air-gapped, immutable backup infrastructure is justified by the recovery cost differential alone.

## DR Strategy Cost vs. RTO: Reference Comparison

| Strategy | Infrastructure Cost Multiplier | Expected RTO Range | Expected RPO Range |
|----------|-------------------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Backup & Restore | 1.0x | 8–72 hours | 1–24 hours |
| Pilot Light | 1.1x–1.2x | 1–4 hours | 15–60 minutes |
| Warm Standby | 1.25x–1.5x | 15–60 minutes | 5–30 minutes |
| Hot Standby | 1.5x–2.0x | 5–15 minutes | 0–5 minutes |
| Multi-Site Active-Active | 2.0x–3.0x | < 60 seconds | 0–60 seconds |

*Multipliers relative to a single-environment infrastructure cost baseline. Add 10–20% for operational overhead (additional monitoring, DR testing, configuration management).*

## Regulatory Downtime Reporting Thresholds

| Regulation | Reporting Trigger | Timeline | Notes |
|-----------|------------------|----------|-------|
| FCC Part 4 (Telecom) | 900K+ user-minutes or special facility affected | 24 hours (initial); 30 days (final) | User-minutes = affected users × minutes of outage |
| FFIEC (Financial) | Material IT disruption affecting core banking | 2 business days | Board must be notified within 1 day |
| HIPAA (Healthcare) | PHI unavailability that constitutes a breach | 60 days from discovery | <500 individuals: annual HHS filing; >500: immediate |
| SEC (Public Companies) | Material cybersecurity incident | 4 business days from determination of materiality | Form 8-K required |
| DORA (EU Financial, effective 2025) | Major ICT incident | 4 hours (initial notification); 72 hours (intermediate); 30 days (final) | Applies to EU-regulated entities |
| NIS2 (EU, effective 2024) | Significant incident affecting essential services | 24 hours (early warning); 72 hours (incident notification) | Broader scope than original NIS |

## Industry Availability Commitments: Customer-Facing SLA Benchmarks

| Vendor / Sector | Typical Customer-Facing SLA | SLA Credit at Breach | Notes |
|----------------|---------------------------|---------------------|-------|
| AWS (compute) | 99.99% per service | 10%–30% of monthly fee | Regional availability, not global |
| Azure | 99.99% (VMs with AZ) | 25%–100% of monthly fee | Tiered credit structure |
| Salesforce | 99.9% | Per contract | Enterprise contracts often negotiate higher |
| T-Mobile Enterprise | 99.9%–99.99% | Per contract | Network SLA; specific services vary |
| Stripe (payments) | 99.99% | Per contract | Payment networks have high bar |
| Major US Banks (online banking) | 99.9%–99.99% | Varies; often reputational only | Regulated; regulatory scrutiny if missed |

**Use case**: When negotiating customer SLAs, these benchmarks establish industry-normal commitments. Committing to above-industry SLAs requires architecture to match.
