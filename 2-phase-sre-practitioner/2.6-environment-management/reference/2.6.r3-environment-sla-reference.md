## SLA Definition Clarifications

### Uptime Measurement

**Uptime %** is calculated as:

```
Uptime % = (Total minutes in window - Total downtime minutes) / Total minutes in window × 100
```

**Downtime** is any period where one or more services in the environment are unavailable for their intended purpose — not just complete environment failure. A QA environment where the database is down but the application is running is considered down for QA purposes.

**Planned maintenance windows** are excluded from downtime calculation if:
- Communicated to all environment users with ≥ 4 hours notice (< 2 hours for emergency maintenance)
- Executed within the communicated window
- Environment restored within the communicated duration

### Supported Hours

**Supported hours** = hours during which the platform SRE team provides active monitoring and incident response. Outside supported hours, environments are expected to self-recover from transient failures via Kubernetes self-healing. Human response is not guaranteed.

**Exception**: P1 environments (Staging, Performance during test, UAT during UAT window) receive 24×7 on-call coverage regardless of standard supported hours.

## Data Refresh SLA Details

| Tier | Automated Refresh Cadence | On-Demand Refresh SLA | Max Data Age Before Governance Flag |
|------|--------------------------|----------------------|------------------------------------|
| Shared Dev | Weekly (Sunday 6am UTC) | < 2 hours | 14 days |
| Integration | Per sprint start (automated) | < 1 hour | 14 days |
| QA | Per sprint start + on-demand | < 2 hours (masked snapshot pre-staged) | 30 days |
| Performance | Per test cycle (manual trigger) | < 4 hours (data volume dependent) | 30 days |
| UAT | Per UAT cycle (manual trigger) | < 4 hours | 7 days during active UAT |
| Staging | Per release (automated with pipeline) | < 2 hours | 7 days |

**Data age governance**: Any environment with data older than the "Max Data Age" threshold in the table above is flagged in the weekly environment health dashboard as a governance issue. Environment owner must trigger refresh within 2 business days.

## SLA Exception Process

When an environment cannot meet its SLA target due to known constraints (capacity, scheduled maintenance, deliberate configuration change):

1. **Request**: Environment owner or platform team submits SLA exception request via IDP
2. **Justification required**: Why SLA cannot be met; estimated duration; impact on consuming teams
3. **Approval**: Engineering Director for exceptions > 1 week; VP SRE for exceptions > 1 month
4. **Communication**: Exception communicated to all environment users before effective date
5. **Recording**: Exception recorded in environment inventory and SLA report for the period
6. **Expiry**: Exceptions have a hard expiry date; renewal requires re-approval
