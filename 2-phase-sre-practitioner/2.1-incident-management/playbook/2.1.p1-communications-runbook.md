# Communications Runbook
**Audience**: Comms Lead, IC, and anyone who touches external or executive communication during an incident  
**Purpose**: Exactly what to say, when to say it, and what not to say — for every communication channel  
**Usage**: The Comms Lead owns execution. The IC approves content for SEV1/2. Neither improvises.

---

## Status Page Protocol

### First Update: 10-Minute Hard Deadline

For SEV1 or SEV2: a status page update must be posted within 10 minutes of incident declaration. No exceptions.

You do not need the root cause. You do not need an ETA. You do not need a full picture. The rule is: customers who are experiencing an issue should not discover it themselves before we acknowledge it. Silence reads as incompetence. A brief holding message reads as competence.

Minimum acceptable first update:
> "We are investigating reports of elevated error rates affecting [service/feature]. We will provide an update within 30 minutes."

This takes 45 seconds to write. Write it.

### Status Page Levels — When to Use Each

| Status | Use When |
|--------|----------|
| **Operational** | All systems functioning within SLO. No customer impact. |
| **Degraded Performance** | Service is functional but slower than normal. Some users may notice latency. SLO at risk but not breached. |
| **Partial Outage** | A portion of customers cannot complete a specific action. Workaround may exist. |
| **Major Outage** | A significant customer segment cannot complete a core journey (checkout, login, payment). No workaround. |
| **Under Maintenance** | Planned work in progress. Only use for pre-announced windows. Never use this for an unplanned incident — it reads as dishonest when customers are experiencing unplanned failures. |

**Escalation path**: as the incident scope expands, update the status level upward immediately. Do not hold at "Degraded" because it feels less alarming when the reality is "Major Outage" — customers who are blocked know the difference.

### Language Standards

**What to say:**

- "We are investigating elevated error rates affecting [feature] for some users."
- "We have identified the issue and are actively working to restore service."
- "The issue has been mitigated. We are monitoring to confirm stability."
- "This incident has been resolved. Impact duration was approximately [X] minutes. We will publish a post-incident summary within 24 hours."

**What not to say:**

| Phrase | Why It Fails |
|--------|-------------|
| "Everything is fine, we're monitoring." | False reassurance when customers are experiencing failures. Destroys trust when they discover the gap. |
| "We have identified the root cause as [specific technical component]." | Don't commit to root cause during an active incident. You may be wrong. Correcting it mid-incident compounds the communication failure. |
| "This will be resolved by [specific time]." | ETA misses compound the damage. Only commit to an ETA when you have >80% confidence. When you don't have that confidence, say "We will provide an update by [time]" instead. |
| "A small number of users are affected." | Quantify or don't characterize. "Small" to you may be thousands of customers. Say "some users" or give an actual estimate if you have one. |
| "Due to unforeseen circumstances..." | Every incident involves unforeseen circumstances. This phrase adds nothing. |
| "Our engineers are working hard to..." | Implied. Don't say it. It wastes words and can read as defensive. |

### Update Cadence

| Severity | Active Incident | At Mitigation | At Resolution |
|----------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| SEV1 | Every 30 minutes | Immediate update | Immediate update |
| SEV2 | Every 60 minutes | Immediate update | Immediate update |
| SEV3 | At resolution only | N/A | Post resolution note |
| SEV4 | Not required | N/A | Optional internal note |

If you miss a cadence update because the incident is moving fast: post a placeholder ("Update in progress — next post within 15 minutes") rather than skipping. The worst customer experience is a long period of silence following an acknowledged incident.

## Internal Communication Structure

### Incident Channel Naming

Format: `#incident-YYYY-MM-DD-service-name`

Examples:
- `#incident-2026-06-30-payment-api`
- `#incident-2026-06-30-auth-service`

Why this format: sortable by date, searchable by service name, archivable for PIR reference. Do not use abbreviations that are not universally understood ("PMT" vs "payment"). Spell out the service name.

### Required Posts in Incident Channel (In Order)

The Scribe owns this log. Every item below must appear in the channel with a timestamp:

1. Incident declaration (IC posts or confirms)
2. Role assignments (IC, TL, Comms Lead, Scribe)
3. Every hypothesis proposed — and its outcome when tested (confirmed / ruled out)
4. Every major action authorized — with the authorizing IC's name
5. Every external status page update — copy the text posted externally
6. Every executive communication sent
7. Mitigation declaration
8. Stability watch status (every 10 minutes during 30-min post-mitigation watch)
9. Resolution declaration
10. PIR scheduled confirmation (date, facilitator, attendees)

If it happened and it's not in the channel, it didn't happen for PIR purposes.

### Bridge Call vs. Async Channel

| Condition | Use Bridge (Voice) | Use Async Channel |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| SEV1, active customer impact | Yes — always | Supplement bridge; do not replace |
| SEV2, multiple teams involved | Yes — for coordination | For detailed findings/logs |
| SEV2, single team, clear hypothesis | Optional | Sufficient |
| SEV3/4 | Not required | Primary communication |
| Post-mitigation stability watch | Not required | Primary communication |

Bridge calls are for real-time coordination when communication lag creates risk. Don't maintain a bridge call after the situation stabilizes into a monitoring pattern — it exhausts responders and produces no value.

## Executive Communication

### What Executives Need

Executives do not need technical detail during an incident. They need:
1. Scope: how many customers are affected and which ones?
2. Business impact: revenue, SLA breach exposure, named enterprise accounts?
3. Status: are we making progress, and how confident are we?
4. ETA: when can they expect the next update (or a resolution)?

Everything else is noise. Three sentences. No jargon.

### Executive Update Template

```
[HH:MM] INCIDENT UPDATE — [Service] — SEV[X]
Impact: [Number of customers/transactions affected] — [which segments or named accounts]
Status: [Current action — e.g., "Rollback in progress, 70% confidence this resolves the issue"]
ETA: [Estimated resolution OR "Next update at HH:MM — no resolution ETA yet"]
IC: [Name] | Bridge: [Link if they need to join]
```

### When to Send Executive Updates

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| SEV1 declared | Immediate notification to VP Engineering |
| 30 minutes into SEV1 | First structured update (use template above) |
| Every 30 minutes until mitigation | Recurring update — do not wait for VP to ask |
| Mitigation confirmed | Update immediately — even if it's 2 AM |
| Resolution confirmed | Final update — close the loop |
| Any time a business decision is needed | Escalate regardless of time elapsed |
| Any confirmed data exposure | Notify VP and Legal simultaneously — do not sequence |

**Who sends it**: IC or a designated executive communication owner. Not the Comms Lead (their lane is external/status page). Clarity on this before an incident starts prevents gaps.

### Escalation to C-Suite

When to go above VP:
- SEV1 with confirmed revenue impact >$100K
- Any customer data breach
- Any incident requiring a public statement beyond the status page
- Any incident with media attention or social media amplification
- Any incident that triggers a regulatory reporting obligation

The VP makes the call to escalate to C-suite. IC notifies VP; VP decides. The IC should not skip the VP layer unilaterally unless the VP is unreachable after two attempts.

## Customer Communication

### When Direct Customer Communication Is Required

The status page is your default external channel. Direct customer communication is a higher bar:

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| SEV1 with confirmed duration >30 minutes | Status page required; consider direct for enterprise accounts |
| Any customer data exposure | Direct notification required regardless of severity or duration |
| Any SLA breach (per contract terms) | Direct notification required within the SLA notification window |
| Named enterprise account confirmed affected | Customer Success direct outreach within 30 minutes of SEV1 declaration |

### Customer Communication Principles

**Own it.** "We experienced an issue" — not "users experienced issues." Your system failed. You own the failure.

**Be specific about impact.** "Checkout was unavailable for approximately 40 minutes" is more credible than "some users experienced difficulty completing purchases."

**Provide a timeline for the next update.** If you can't give a resolution time, give a time for the next status update. Customers can tolerate uncertainty; they cannot tolerate silence.

**No technical jargon.** "A memory exhaustion event in our Redis cluster" means nothing to a retail customer. "An infrastructure issue caused our checkout service to be unavailable" is accurate and accessible.

**Do not minimize.** "Minor disruption" for a 40-minute checkout outage will generate more complaints than an honest description. Customers know what they experienced.

### Post-Incident Customer Communication

For SEV1 incidents: publish a post-incident summary within 24 hours. For named enterprise customers with SLAs: deliver it directly, not only on the status page.

Post-incident summary must include:
- What happened (plain language, no jargon)
- What was affected (specific features or journeys, duration)
- What was done to resolve it
- What is being done to prevent recurrence (specific — not "we are improving our monitoring")

### Enterprise Account Direct Notification Protocol

For accounts with named SLA commitments:
1. Customer Success is notified by IC at the time of SEV1 declaration
2. Customer Success makes direct contact (phone or email) within 30 minutes — not through the status page
3. Customer Success provides the standard executive update content in their communication
4. Customer Success logs the outreach time in the incident channel
5. Customer Success provides post-incident follow-up within 24 hours with the written summary

Customer Success does not investigate the technical issue. Their lane is relationship and communication. They relay IC content. They do not improvise technical explanations.

## Regulatory Communication

### Know the Clocks — They Start at Discovery, Not Reporting Decision

Regulatory reporting timelines are not negotiable and are measured from when you knew (or should have known), not from when you decide to report.

| Regulation | Trigger | Reporting Target | Timeline |
|------------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| PCI-DSS | Cardholder data confirmed exposed or at risk | Acquiring bank | Within 24 hours of discovery |
| GDPR | Personal data of EU individuals breached with risk to individuals | Supervisory authority (Data Protection Authority) | Within 72 hours of discovery |
| HIPAA | PHI affected by a security incident | HHS (Office for Civil Rights) | Within 60 days of discovery |
| SOX | Incident affecting integrity of financial reporting systems | Internal Compliance team | Immediately upon discovery |

**These are minimums for internal escalation. Legal makes the final call on external reporting.**

### How to Involve Legal and Compliance

At SEV1 declaration: if data exposure is possible (not confirmed — possible), loop in Legal on-call. You are not reporting a breach; you are giving them early warning to evaluate.

```
[HH:MM] — Looping in Legal/Compliance per protocol.
Incident: [Name]
Data exposure risk: [Yes/Possible/Unknown]
Affected data types: [Cardholder data / PII / PHI / Financial records — or Unknown]
Legal on-call notified: [Name, method, time]
```

Do not make the regulatory reporting call yourself. Do not delay looping in Legal to "get more information first" — they need to start their assessment now, not after you've investigated.

**IC responsibility**: confirm Legal has been notified and log the notification in the incident channel. After that, Legal drives the regulatory decision tree. Do not insert yourself into their process.

### What Not to Do in Regulatory Situations

- Do not confirm or deny data exposure in public communications before Legal has reviewed the statement
- Do not provide timelines or scope estimates to regulatory bodies without Legal approval
- Do not communicate directly with regulators — route through Legal
- Do not document speculative breach scope in systems that may be subject to legal discovery without Legal guidance on phrasing
