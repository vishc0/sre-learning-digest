# Incident Command Playbook
**Audience**: Incident Commander (IC) on rotation  
**Purpose**: Phase-by-phase operating rules for the IC role — from pre-incident readiness to bridge closure  
**Usage**: This is your live reference during an incident. Print it. Bookmark it. Know Phase 1 cold before you're on rotation.

---

## Phase 0: Pre-Incident Preparation

### What the IC Must Know Before the Pager Fires

**Service catalog essentials** (know these, don't look them up during an incident):
- Top 10 customer-facing services, their tier, and their on-call team
- Critical dependencies: payment processor, auth service, CDN, primary DB cluster
- Which services share infrastructure (a K8s node issue can cascade across three teams)
- SLA thresholds per service tier — what "customer impact" means in numbers

**Escalation contacts** (verify these are current every rotation):
- Your TL candidates for each service domain
- Comms Lead on call
- VP of Engineering direct line (for SEV1 executive loop)
- Legal/Compliance on-call contact (for data exposure)
- Customer Success lead (for enterprise account notification)

**Severity criteria** — internalize this, don't recalculate it under pressure:

| SEV | Customer Impact | Revenue Impact | Response Time |
|-----|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| SEV1 | >10% users, complete journey failure, or data exposure | >$10K/min or SLA breach | Bridge in 5 min |
| SEV2 | Partial degradation, workaround exists | $1K–$10K/min | Bridge in 15 min |
| SEV3 | Single component, no customer journey broken | Minimal | Async response |
| SEV4 | Cosmetic / performance / monitoring only | None | Next business day |

**Authorization limits you hold as IC**:
- Authorize rollbacks without VP approval (document after)
- Authorize traffic rerouting or load shedder activation
- Authorize bringing in additional on-call responders from other teams
- Authorize status page updates
- You do NOT authorize: database schema changes in production, external vendor escalations that carry contractual cost, public communications not through Comms Lead

### IC Rotation Readiness Checklist (Run Weekly)

- [ ] Escalation contact list verified — no stale numbers
- [ ] PagerDuty rotation confirmed — you are listed correctly for your on-call window
- [ ] Incident bridge tool accessible from home network and mobile
- [ ] Status page admin access verified (test a draft update — don't publish)
- [ ] Monitoring dashboards load correctly: APM, infrastructure, error rate, SLI/SLO boards
- [ ] Runbook repo access confirmed — can you reach it from outside VPN?
- [ ] You know who the backup IC is for your rotation window
- [ ] Laptop charged, charger accessible, phone hotspot tested if home network is unreliable

### Pre-Incident Tooling Access Check

Before your rotation starts, confirm you can reach all of these right now:

| Tool | What to Check | Fallback if Down |
|------|--------------|------------------|
| Incident bridge (Zoom/Meet/Teams) | Can you start a room and invite by email? | Phone conference bridge number |
| Status page admin | Can you post a draft update? | Designate Comms Lead as publisher |
| PagerDuty | Can you acknowledge and reassign alerts? | Direct Slack/phone to on-call |
| APM/Monitoring (Datadog, NewRelic, etc.) | Main dashboards load, no auth errors | Splunk as backup observability |
| Splunk | Can you run a basic `index=prod error` search? | Raw log SSH access via ops team |
| Runbook repo | Can you access incident runbooks without VPN? | Local copy of top 5 runbooks |

## Phase 1: Detection and Declaration (0–5 Minutes)

### Step 1 — Acknowledge the Alert
- Acknowledge in PagerDuty immediately. This stops the MTTA clock.
- If you are driving, on a call, or otherwise occupied: acknowledge, then find 60 seconds of focus before doing anything else. Acknowledging without acting is still better than not acknowledging.

### Step 2 — 30-Second Reality Check
This is NOT an investigation. Answer three questions with what you can see in 30 seconds:

1. Is the alert firing for the first time or is this a flapping alert with no customer correlation?
2. Are correlated signals present? (Error rate spike + latency spike + PagerDuty from two different teams = real)
3. Is this time-of-day explainable? (Marketing campaign launch, batch job, known maintenance window?)

**Decision**: Real incident → proceed to Step 3. Alert noise → acknowledge, note in PagerDuty, add to weekly noise review queue.

### Step 3 — Assess Scope (Still Under 2 Minutes)

Check three things only — don't go deep yet:
- Which services are showing symptoms? (APM service map, not individual logs)
- Which user segments or regions? (Geography filter on error rate dashboard)
- Is a customer journey broken end-to-end, or is one component degraded?

### Step 4 — Make the Severity Call

Use the matrix in Phase 0. Do not negotiate with yourself. When in doubt, declare higher and downgrade — a false SEV2 costs you 30 minutes; a missed SEV1 costs you customers and SLA credits.

**When to skip severity and go straight to SEV1**: any confirmed customer data exposure, any complete checkout/auth failure, any signal from a major enterprise customer that their service is down.

### Step 5 — Declare the Incident

1. Create the incident Slack channel: `#incident-YYYY-MM-DD-service-name`
2. Open the bridge call
3. Post the declaration message in the incident channel:

```
[SEVERITY LEVEL] INCIDENT DECLARED — [Service Name]
Symptom: [One sentence — what is broken, not why]
Affected: [User segment, region, or journey]
IC: [Your name]
TL: [Assigned or TBD]
Comms Lead: [Assigned or TBD]
Scribe: [Assigned or TBD]
Bridge: [Link]
First status update: T+20 minutes
```

4. Page the TL for the affected service if not already on the call.

### Step 6 — Assign Roles Explicitly

Say it out loud on the bridge:
- "TL for this incident is [Name]. You own the technical investigation."
- "Comms Lead is [Name]. You own status page updates — next update in 10 minutes."
- "Scribe is [Name]. Log every action and hypothesis with timestamp in the incident channel."
- "Everyone else: post findings to the channel, not to me directly."

Set the first check-in: "TL, give me a status in 10 minutes. Go."

## Phase 2: Triage and Mitigation (5–30 Minutes)

### Bridge Opening Protocol (First 2 Minutes on the Call)

When everyone is assembled, run this sequence — takes 2 minutes, saves 10:
1. Symptom recap: restate what is broken for anyone who just joined
2. Role confirmation: confirm TL, Comms Lead, Scribe are all present and acknowledged
3. Working hypothesis statement: "Current leading hypothesis is [X]. TL, is that what you're investigating?"
4. Set next check-in: "I want a status from TL in 10 minutes. Time is now [HH:MM]."

### IC Operating Rules During Mitigation

**Hands off keyboard.** If you are typing commands or pulling logs, you are not commanding. Give the investigation to the TL and SMEs. Your job is to watch the clock, manage communication, and make authorization calls.

**Drive toward mitigation, not root cause.** The question is: "What can we do right now to reduce customer impact?" Root cause is for the PIR.

**Authorize explicitly.** When a responder proposes an action:
- State your authorization out loud and in the channel: `Authorized: rollback API gateway to v2.3.1 — [HH:MM]`
- Never assume implicit authority. If a responder acts without asking, ask them to narrate future actions before executing.

**Status update cadence** — Comms Lead drives this, but IC approves content:
- Every 20 minutes: internal status update in incident channel
- Every 30 minutes: external status page update (SEV1)

### Hypothesis Management

The IC is not investigating — but the IC tracks what is being investigated and when to call a pivot.

**Pivot signal**: TL has been investigating a hypothesis for 15-20 minutes with no confirmation. Say:
> "We've been on this hypothesis for [N] minutes with no confirmation. Are we gaining signal or spinning? TL, what is hypothesis B and what would it take to test it?"

**Escalation signal**: Three hypotheses ruled out with no mitigation path identified. Actions:
1. Escalate severity if not already at SEV1
2. Page additional SMEs from adjacent teams
3. Brief the VP (see Phase 3)

### Decision Log Requirements (Scribe Owns, IC Verifies Every 20 Minutes)

Every entry must have: timestamp, who made the decision, what was decided, what outcome resulted.

Required entries:
- Incident declaration and initial severity
- Every hypothesis tested (and whether confirmed or ruled out)
- Every mitigation action authorized
- Every escalation (severity change, additional responders, executive loop)
- Every status update sent externally

## Phase 3: Sustained Response (30–120 Minutes)

### Bridge Management at Scale

At 30 minutes, your bridge has more people, more hypotheses, and more noise. IC discipline:

- **Silence management**: if no one has spoken for 5 minutes, check in — either something good is happening or something is stuck. Don't let silent bridges run.
- **Parallel track discipline**: explicitly name the two tracks running simultaneously:
  - Track 1 (Mitigation): what can we turn off, roll back, or reroute to stop customer impact?
  - Track 2 (Diagnosis): what is the root cause so we can fix permanently?
  - Make clear which responders are on which track. Prevent everyone collapsing onto one track.
- **Contributor management**: more than 8 active voices on a bridge is noise. Ask non-essential participants to go async and watch the channel.

### Severity Escalation to SEV1

Escalate from SEV2 to SEV1 if any of the following are true:
- Impact has spread beyond initial assessment (additional services, regions, user segments confirmed)
- MTTR is tracking >30 minutes with no mitigation path identified
- C-suite or major enterprise customer impact confirmed
- Any regulatory exposure confirmed (data breach, financial reporting system)
- Customer Success has received inbound complaint from a named enterprise account

When escalating: update the channel topic, post escalation notice, page VP per executive communication protocol below.

### Executive Communication Protocol

When to brief VP/C-suite:
- SEV1 at the 30-minute mark, no exceptions
- Any time a business decision is needed (e.g., take the service down deliberately, trigger a DR failover, initiate a customer-facing SLA credit process)
- Any confirmed customer data exposure, immediately

Executive update format (three sentences max — they don't need more):
```
[HH:MM] SEV[X] — [Service] — [IC Name]
Impact: [Number of customers/transactions affected] — [which journeys broken]
Status: [What is being done and confidence in the approach]
ETA: [Estimate or "Next update in 30 min — no ETA yet"]
```

### Staffing Decisions

**Bring in additional responders when**:
- All three working hypotheses have been ruled out and you need domain expertise from another team
- A responder asks for help and can articulate what specific expertise they need
- A single responder has been heads-down for >90 minutes without a break — fatigue degrades judgment

**Rotate fatigued responders when**:
- A responder has been on the bridge >4 hours
- A responder is making repeated errors or asking the same questions
- The incident is entering a sustained watch period (post-mitigation stability monitoring)

Rotation requires a proper handoff — see handoff protocol in the On-Call Responder Rulebook.

## Phase 4: Mitigation and Stabilization

### Declaring Mitigation

**Mitigation ≠ Resolution.** Mitigation means: customer impact is confirmed at zero. The underlying cause may still be present.

Before declaring mitigation, verify:
- [ ] Error rates on all affected services are back within SLO thresholds
- [ ] Synthetic transactions across all affected customer journeys are passing
- [ ] Latency metrics are within normal range — not just error rates
- [ ] Comms Lead has drafted status page update reflecting mitigation
- [ ] TL confirms no active rollback or remediation action is still in-flight

Post in incident channel:
```
MITIGATION DECLARED — [HH:MM]
Customer impact: ZERO (confirmed via synthetics and error rate)
Underlying cause: [addressed / deferred to remediation]
Stability watch: Bridge remains open until [HH:MM + 30 min]
Status page: [Comms Lead, please publish degraded → operational]
```

### Post-Mitigation Stability Watch (30 Minutes)

Keep the bridge open. Keep the TL on the call. Check every 10 minutes:
- Error rates holding?
- Any new alerts firing?
- Any customer feedback channels showing continued complaints?

If stability holds for 30 minutes: proceed to resolution. If any metric degrades: you have not mitigated — restart Phase 2.

### De-escalation

- Update incident severity to reflect current state (e.g., SEV1 → SEV2 if partially mitigated)
- Release non-essential responders from the bridge with explicit acknowledgment: "Thanks [Name], you're released."
- Close the executive loop: brief VP that mitigation is confirmed, resolution TBD

## Phase 5: Resolution and Closure

### Resolution Criteria

Resolution requires ONE of:
1. Root cause is identified and addressed in production (fix deployed, verified)
2. Root cause is identified, a temporary mitigation is stable, and a remediation ticket is filed with an owner and due date

Resolution does NOT require: full post-incident analysis, a perfect explanation, or all action items identified.

### Incident Closure Checklist

- [ ] All decisions and actions logged in incident channel with timestamps
- [ ] Status page updated to "Operational" with brief resolution note
- [ ] PIR scheduled before bridge closes: date, time, facilitator assigned
- [ ] Jira/ticket opened for any deferred remediation work — owner assigned, not "team"
- [ ] Overnight monitoring handoff confirmed: who is watching for recurrence, and what are their alert thresholds?
- [ ] All responders formally released: "Incident [ID] is resolved. You're all released. Thank you."
- [ ] Incident channel pinned with summary post for async readers

### PIR Scheduling (Do Not Skip)

Before closing the bridge, confirm out loud:
- PIR date: within 5 business days for SEV1/SEV2
- PIR facilitator: named, not "someone from the team"
- Scribe timeline: Scribe posts draft timeline to PIR doc before the session

Post in incident channel:
```
PIR SCHEDULED — [Date/Time]
Facilitator: [Name]
Attendees: [IC, TL, Comms Lead, SMEs who were active]
Pre-read: Scribe timeline will be posted by [Date — 24h before PIR]
```

### Handoff to Monitoring

Post in incident channel:
```
OVERNIGHT WATCH — [Name] is monitoring for recurrence
Alert threshold: [specific metric and threshold to watch]
Escalation path: Page [IC name] if [specific condition] occurs
```
