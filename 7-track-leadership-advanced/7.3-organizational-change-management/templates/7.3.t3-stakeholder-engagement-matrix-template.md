## Column Legend

Before filling in the matrix, review what each column means.

| Column | What to Enter |
|--------|--------------|
| **Name** | Full name of the stakeholder |
| **Role / Title** | Current organizational role — be specific (e.g., "VP of Engineering, Consumer Products" not just "VP") |
| **Influence Level** | How much informal and formal power this person has over the change outcome: **H** (can accelerate or block significantly), **M** (can slow or assist at the margin), **L** (limited impact on outcome) |
| **Current Support** | Their current posture toward the change: Champion / Neutral / Skeptic / Blocker (see definitions below) |
| **Key Concern** | The specific concern or interest driving their current posture. For Champions: what they care about protecting. For Skeptics/Blockers: the root worry. Be specific — "worried about headcount" is better than "concerned." |
| **Engagement Action** | The specific next action to take with this stakeholder — a conversation, a briefing, a data share, an invitation to a review. Must be concrete and time-bound. |
| **Owner** | Who owns this stakeholder relationship and the engagement action |
| **Status** | Current status of the engagement action: Not Started / In Progress / Complete / Needs Revisit |

---

## Support Level Definitions

**Champion**: Actively and visibly supports the change. Will advocate in rooms you're not in. May be willing to be publicly associated with the change (all-hands presenter, testimonial in announcement).

**Neutral**: No strong opinion currently. Not opposing, not supporting. Typically focused on their own priorities. Can be moved to Champion with demonstrated value and a clear "what's in it for me."

**Skeptic**: Has specific concerns or doubts. Not yet in opposition — still willing to engage and ask questions. This is the most actionable category: address the concern specifically and a Skeptic often becomes neutral or Champion.

**Blocker**: Actively working against the change — withholding cooperation, raising objections in escalation paths, or organizing others against the change. Requires direct engagement, escalation, or a determination of whether their concern is substantive (and should change your plan) or is a power move.

### Example Row 1 — Champion

| Name | Role / Title | Influence Level | Current Support | Key Concern | Engagement Action | Owner | Status |
|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------|------------------|-------|--------|

|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------|------------------|-------|--------|

| Date | Stakeholder | Movement | What Caused It |
|------|------------|---------|---------------|
| [Date] | [Name] | Skeptic → Neutral | 1:1 with platform architect addressed PCI concern; platform will add PCI-compliant deployment mode in Q3 |
| [Date] | [Name] | Neutral → Champion | Attended beta demo; team deployed in 40 minutes; manager became vocal advocate |
| [Date] | [Name] | Champion → Neutral | Sponsor's team reorganized; new manager has no relationship with change initiative; need to re-engage |

*Note on regression*: Stakeholders can move backward. A Champion who loses their executive sponsor becomes Neutral. A Skeptic whose concern is ignored becomes a Blocker. Track movement in both directions.

## Quarterly Stakeholder Review Prompt

At the end of each quarter, ask these questions about the matrix:

1. Has anyone moved categories since last review? What caused it?
2. Are there stakeholders who should be on this list but aren't?
3. Are there stakeholders listed whose influence has changed (promotion, departure, scope change)?
4. Which engagement actions are still "Not Started"? Why? Is the plan still valid?
5. Have we won over any Skeptics? What specifically worked?
6. Are there Blockers who are no longer blocking? Can we move them to Neutral?
