## Segment Tracking — Adoption Curve

*Map your population to the classic diffusion segments. Update monthly.*

**Total population**: [Number of teams / individuals]

| Segment | Description | Count | % of Total | Status / Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Early Adopters** | Adopted proactively, before any push. Likely your beta teams or champions. | [n] | [%] | [e.g., All 5 beta teams fully migrated] |
| **Early Majority** | Adopted during broad rollout. Needed some communication and visible success proof, but not heavy persuasion. | [n] | [%] | [e.g., 8 of 14 targeted teams onboarded] |
| **Late Majority** | Adopted after the majority of peers. Needed peer pressure, a formal nudge, or a forcing function. | [n] | [%] | [e.g., 3 teams in active migration, motivated by sunset announcement] |
| **Laggards** | Have not adopted. Actively resisting or in genuine constraint (capacity, technical blocker, compliance). | [n] | [%] | [e.g., 2 teams: Checkout (Blocker-level resistance) and Fraud (PCI exception in progress)] |

**Segment transition triggers**:
- Early Adopter → tracked when: first production deployment or process use is logged
- Early Majority → tracked when: adoption completed without dedicated migration support
- Late Majority → tracked when: adoption required dedicated support or formal escalation
- Laggard → tracked when: team is past the expected adoption window with no migration plan confirmed

## Adoption Milestone Checklist

*Mark each milestone as it is achieved.*

- [ ] Beta cohort fully onboarded and using the new process/platform
- [ ] Champions program active with at least one external-facing activity completed
- [ ] Broad rollout communications delivered (all-hands, written announcement, documentation live)
- [ ] 50% of target population adopted
- [ ] 75% of target population adopted
- [ ] 100% of Early Majority adopted
- [ ] Late Majority plan confirmed (individual migration plans or forcing function in place)
- [ ] Laggard plans confirmed (exception, extended migration, or escalation path defined)
- [ ] New hires onboarding into the changed state as "normal" (not as exception)
- [ ] Change reflected in org charts, hiring profiles, and onboarding documentation

## Tracker Health Check (Monthly)

Ask these questions monthly to keep the tracker honest:

1. Are the metrics we're tracking still the right ones? Have any become meaningless?
2. Is adoption where we expected it to be at this point? If not, do we understand why?
3. Are our qualitative signals aligned with our quantitative metrics? (If numbers look good but signals are negative, trust the signals.)
4. Is any segment stalled for 2+ months? Does that segment have a concrete unblocking plan?
5. Are there stakeholders driving resistance that are not yet named in the stakeholder matrix?
