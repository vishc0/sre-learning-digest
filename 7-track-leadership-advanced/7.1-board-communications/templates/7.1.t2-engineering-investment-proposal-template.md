# Template — Engineering Investment Proposal

> **Usage notes:** This is a 1-2 page business case document, not a slide deck. Write in business language — no jargon without translation. Fill in all bracketed fields. Delete all guidance text before sharing with executives. For a board or CFO audience, you will also prepare a 1-slide summary using the key numbers from Section 4.

---

**Prepared by:** [Name, Title] | **Date:** [Date]

## 1. Problem

> *[Describe the current state in business terms. Lead with the cost or risk of the current state — not the technical description. Answer: what is this situation costing us today? Use numbers wherever possible. If you don't have exact numbers, use ranges with labels ("we estimate $X-$Y based on..."). Maximum: 3 short paragraphs.]*

**Current state:**
[Describe what exists today — what is working, what is not, what the gap is. One paragraph.]

**Cost of current state:**
[Quantify what this situation is costing the business today. Use the formulas in `reference/business-risk-quantification-reference.md`. Include: revenue impact, operational cost, customer impact, regulatory exposure. Provide a total annual cost estimate.]

**Trend:**
[Is this getting better, worse, or staying the same? What drives the trend? One or two sentences.]

## 2. Options

> *[Always three options. Never one. The middle option should be genuinely viable — not a strawman. Present options in a table for clarity. Your recommendation goes in Section 3.]*

| Option | Description | One-Time Cost | Annual Run Cost | Risk Reduction | Notes |
|--------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|----------------|-------|
| **Option A: Do Nothing** | Continue current state | $0 | $0 | 0% | [What happens if we choose this] |
| **Option B: Minimum Viable** | [Brief description] | $[X] | $[Y] | ~[Z]% | [Trade-offs — what it does and does not address] |
| **Option C: Full Investment** | [Brief description] | $[X] | $[Y] | ~[Z]% | [What this fully addresses — recommended] |

> *[Guidance on "Do Nothing": Always include it. It establishes the baseline and makes the risk-of-inaction argument visible. This is the most persuasive element of the table for a CFO who asks "what happens if we wait?"]*

## 3. Recommendation

> **Worked numbers example (fill in with your actual data):**
>
> Problem: Notification platform at 99.5% delivery. Costs: $5.8M/year in promotional impact losses, $1.8M/year in outage-related revenue, $1.44M/year in support cost = $9M/year total.
>
> Option C investment: $2.1M. Projected benefit: $6.5M/year at full realization. Payback: 3.9 months. 3-year NPV at 10% discount rate: $15.4M net benefit.
>
> Conservative (25th percentile) case: $3.25M/year benefit. Payback: 7.8 months. Still strongly positive.
