# Performance Conversation Documentation Template

> **Educational content only. Consult your company's legal counsel or HR Business Partner for specific situations.**

---

## Purpose

This template is for your own manager's notes — not a formal HR document unless submitted to HR. Its purpose is to create a contemporaneous, factual record of performance conversations. Courts and arbitrators weight records made close to the time of the event much more heavily than memories recalled months later.

**Best practices**:
- Complete within 24 hours of the conversation
- Be factual — record what was said and done, not interpretations
- Use specific dates, quotes, and numbers wherever possible
- Do not record speculation about the employee's motivations or character
- Store in a location accessible to HR if needed (follow your company's guidance on where manager notes should live)

# COMPLETED EXAMPLE

The following example shows how to fill in this template properly. All names and details are fictional.

---

## Record Header

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| **Employee Name** | Jordan Rivera |
| **Employee Title / Role** | Senior Software Engineer, Platform Team |
| **Manager Name** | Taylor Nguyen |
| **Date of Conversation** | 2026-05-14 |
| **Time** | 2:00 PM PT |
| **Location / Method** | Video — Zoom (employee remote, Portland OR) |
| **Meeting Type** | Formal Performance Discussion |
| **Others Present** | None |

---

## Section 1 — Performance Observation

| Performance Area | Specific Behavior or Metric Observed | Date(s) of Observation | Business Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sprint delivery | Completed 3 of 8 committed sprint items (37.5%). Team average: 78%. | Sprint ending May 9, 2026 | API gateway feature slipped to next sprint; downstream team notified May 10 |
| Code review response | 4 of 6 PR review requests responded to after 72+ hours; 2 had no response at end of sprint | Apr 28 – May 9, 2026 | Two PRs blocked for 4+ days; one engineer escalated to Taylor on May 8 |

---

## Section 2 — Prior Conversations on This Topic

| Prior Discussion Date | Method | Summary of What Was Discussed | Employee's Response at That Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-04-16 | Verbal in 1:1 | Sprint delivery at 52% in previous sprint; discussed blockers, Jordan mentioned unclear ticket acceptance criteria | Acknowledged delivery was low, said they'd talk to PM about criteria clarity |
| 2026-05-01 | Verbal in 1:1 | Followed up on April conversation; sprint velocity still below expectations; code reviews also flagged | Jordan said workload felt heavy; we agreed to remove one ticket from next sprint to create space |

---

## Section 3 — Manager Communication in This Conversation

**Key points covered**:
1. Sprint delivery has been below 60% for three consecutive sprints (March, April, and May). The team average is above 75%.
2. Code review response time is not meeting the team expectation of 48-hour turnaround.
3. These two issues together are creating dependency and reliability problems for the team.

**Expectations stated**: Sprint delivery at ≥75% committed items per sprint for the next 30 days. Code review response within 48 business hours for all assigned reviews.

**Consequence communicated**: If these expectations are not met over the next 30 days, the next step will be a formal Performance Improvement Plan with HR involvement.

---

## Section 4 — Employee's Response

**Employee's response to the performance observation**: Jordan acknowledged the sprint delivery numbers were accurate. Did not dispute the code review data. Said they had been "overwhelmed" since the infrastructure migration in March.

**Employee's response to the stated expectations**: Jordan said the 75% target felt achievable and that they understood the 48-hour code review expectation.

**Anything the employee disputed or disagreed with**: Jordan pushed back on the framing that the issues were impacting the team, saying "most of the delays weren't on the critical path." Taylor acknowledged that, and noted that the pattern of missed commitments still creates planning uncertainty regardless of path criticality.

**Any concerns or context the employee raised**: Jordan mentioned that they had taken on two architecture review requests from the staff engineer in April without those being reflected in sprint planning. Taylor noted this and agreed to review how unplanned work gets captured going forward.

---

## Section 5 — Agreed Next Steps

| Action | Owner | By When |
|---|---|---|
| Jordan to flag any unplanned work requests via Jira before accepting | Jordan | Immediately |
| Taylor to review sprint planning process so architecture review time is captured | Taylor | Before next sprint planning (May 19) |
| Check in on sprint delivery and code review metrics | Taylor + Jordan | June 1:1 (May 28) |

---

## Section 6 — Manager Support Offered

| Support Offered | Details |
|---|---|
| Sprint scope relief | Agreed to limit Jordan's sprint scope to 6 points for the next sprint (reduced from typical 10) to create headroom |
| Architecture review coordination | Taylor will intercept direct requests to Jordan from staff engineers and route through sprint planning |

---

## Section 7 — Follow-Up Date

**Next scheduled check-in on this topic**: May 28, 2026 (standard bi-weekly 1:1)

**What will be assessed at that check-in**: Sprint delivery rate for May 19–May 28 sprint; code review log for response time

## Section 8 — Signature Block

*Not required for this informal formal discussion — no written warning issued. This document is manager notes only.*

Jordan asked at the end of the meeting whether this conversation would go in their "HR file." Taylor responded that this was a manager's note, not a formal HR document, and that if a PIP were initiated HR would be involved and Jordan would receive formal documentation at that point.
