# Interview Question Legal Review Template

> **Educational content only. Consult your company's legal counsel or HR Business Partner for specific situations.**

---

## Purpose

This template helps Engineering Directors and hiring managers audit their interview questions before conducting interviews. The goal is not to constrain conversation — it is to keep questions focused on job-relevant capabilities, which also happens to be the safest legal ground.

The safe harbor principle: **Any question that is directly about the requirements of the job is safe.** Questions that wander into personal circumstances, identity, or status are where risk concentrates.

Use this template to:
1. Review your question list before the interview panel meets
2. Train interviewers on what to ask and what to avoid
3. Document that your interview process was reviewed for compliance

---

## How to Use

Read through each question in your planned interview. Find it (or the closest equivalent) in the audit table below. If flagged as Risky or No, replace it with the suggested alternative or rework it using the safe harbor framing at the bottom of this document.

| 12 | "What does your spouse do?" | **No** | Marital status and indirect inquiry into financial situation | Remove entirely |
| 13 | "What religion do you practice?" | **No** | Religion — Title VII | If the role involves weekend or holiday work: "This role requires availability on some weekends and holidays. Is that something you can commit to?" |

## Topics to Never Ask About — Organized by EEO Category

### Age (ADEA — protects workers 40+)
- Date of birth, graduation year, year you started working, "how many years until you retire"
- Anything that lets you calculate approximate age

### Race and National Origin (Title VII)
- Country of birth or origin, ethnicity, ancestry
- What language is spoken at home (unless that language is a documented job requirement)
- Citizenship (you may ask work authorization status)
- Surname or name origin

### Religion (Title VII)
- Religious affiliation, church membership, holidays observed, Sabbath practices
- Availability on religious holidays — instead, describe schedule requirements and ask if the candidate can meet them

### Disability (ADA)
- Any health condition, physical limitation, or medical history — before a conditional offer
- History of workers' compensation claims
- Whether they take medication
- Mental health treatment history

### Pregnancy and Family Status (Title VII / PDA / PWFA)
- Pregnancy plans, whether they have children or want them, childcare arrangements
- Marital status, spouse's employment
- Any question whose answer would reveal pregnancy or reproductive intentions

### Genetic Information (GINA)
- Family medical history, genetic test results
- Do not request medical records that could contain genetic information

### Military Status (USERRA)
- Do not ask about military discharge status or characterization
- You may ask if they have served in the military; you may not use service branch or discharge type as a screening criterion

### Financial / Credit Status
- Bankruptcy history (credit checks in hiring are separately regulated and require disclosure + consent — do not probe conversationally)

## Safe Harbor Framing — Use This to Rework Any Question

The safe harbor test: **Is this question directly about a requirement of the job?**

If yes — ask it. If no — do not ask it. If you are unsure — describe the job requirement and ask whether the candidate can meet it.

**Template**:
> "This role requires [specific, documented job requirement]. Can you [confirm / describe your experience with / commit to] that?"

**Examples**:

| Job Requirement | Safe Question |
|---|---|
| 24/7 on-call rotation, 1 week per quarter | "This role requires on-call coverage for one week per quarter, including nights and weekends during that rotation. Is that something you can commit to?" |
| Travel 25% of time | "This role involves approximately 25% travel, primarily to our New York and Seattle offices. Does that work for you?" |
| Lifting equipment up to 50 lbs (data center work) | "This role involves occasional physical work in our data centers, including lifting equipment up to 50 lbs. Are you able to perform that with or without accommodation?" |
| Security clearance eligibility | "This role requires eligibility for [specific clearance level]. Are you able to pursue that?" |
| On-site 4 days per week | "This role is based on-site in [city] four days per week. Can you commit to that?" |

**What you cannot do**: use the answer to any of the above to infer or ask about protected characteristics. If a candidate says "I can't commit to overnight on-call," accept that answer and evaluate based on the job requirement — do not ask why.

## Pre-Interview Panel Checklist

Before the panel convenes:

- [ ] Every interviewer has reviewed this template or received equivalent training
- [ ] Interview question list has been reviewed and risky questions replaced
- [ ] All interviewers know to redirect if a candidate volunteers personal information (e.g., "I have two kids") — acknowledge, do not probe, return to job-relevant questions
- [ ] Scoring rubric is based on job-relevant criteria, not "culture fit" defined by personal similarity
- [ ] Feedback will be documented in terms of specific, observable competencies — not general impressions
