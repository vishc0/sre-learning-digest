# Documentation Retention Reference

> **Educational content only. Consult your company's legal counsel or HR Business Partner for specific situations.**

---

## Purpose

This reference answers the question every manager eventually asks: "How long do I keep this?" Record retention rules are not about compliance theater — they protect you and the company when disputes arise months or years after an employment event.

**Key principle**: Retain records long enough to survive any foreseeable legal claim. Employment claims often surface long after the event. Destroying records before their retention period is worse than keeping them too long.

**Legal hold override**: If you receive notice of litigation, an EEOC charge, a DOL investigation, or any government inquiry, your company's retention schedule is suspended and all potentially relevant records must be preserved immediately. Contact Legal.

## Retention Quick-Reference Table

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Job applications and resumes (hired)** | 1 year from date of hire | 2 years | HR personnel file | HR | Includes all application materials for the selected candidate |
| **Job applications and resumes (not hired)** | 1 year from date of decision | 2 years | HR applicant tracking system | HR | EEOC regulations require retention of applications, test scores, and interview results |
| **Interview notes and scoring rubrics** | 1 year from date of decision | 2 years | HR applicant tracking system | HR | Manager's interview notes should be submitted to HR; do not keep personal copies long-term |
| **Offer letters** | Duration of employment + 3 years | Duration + 3 years | HR personnel file | HR | Includes any amendments or modifications to compensation |
| **I-9 forms** | 3 years from hire date OR 1 year after termination, whichever is later | Same as federal | Separate I-9 file (never in personnel file) | HR | I-9s must be kept separate from personnel files to prevent immigration status from influencing employment decisions |
| **Performance records — 1:1 notes (manager's informal notes)** | 1 year minimum | 2–3 years recommended | Manager's records (system designated by company) | Manager / HR | If performance notes could support or counter a legal claim, treat as litigation-sensitive. | **Performance records — formal reviews** | 2 years | 3 years | HR personnel file | HR | Annual reviews, mid-year reviews, calibration outcomes |
| **PIPs (Performance Improvement Plans)** | 2 years after conclusion | 3 years after conclusion | HR personnel file | HR | Includes all PIP check-in documentation, not just the initial plan |
| **Written warnings and disciplinary records** | 2 years | 3 years | HR personnel file | HR | Includes all levels: verbal warning documentation, written warning, final warning |
| **Harassment/discrimination investigation records** | 5 years | 5 years (or longer per company policy) | Legal / HR (separate secure file) | HR / Legal | Maintain separately from personnel file. | **Compensation records (wages, salaries, overtime)** | 3 years (FLSA) | 3 years | Payroll / HR systems | Payroll / HR | Includes pay rates, hours worked, total wages paid. Equal Pay Act also requires 2-year retention of wage rate schedules and job evaluations. |
| **Benefits enrollment records** | Duration of plan + 6 years (ERISA) | Same | HR benefits system | HR / Benefits | Includes enrollment forms, elections, beneficiary designations |
| **Training records** | 3 years | 3 years | HR learning management system | HR / L&D | Especially important for safety, harassment prevention, and compliance training (documents that training was delivered) |
| **Medical and accommodation records** | Duration of employment + 3 years | Duration + 3–4 years | Separate confidential medical file — NEVER in personnel file | HR | ADA requires separation of medical records from personnel files. | **Disciplinary records — termination related** | 3 years from date of termination | 4 years from date of termination | HR personnel file | HR | Termination letter, severance agreement, separation agreement |
| **Termination records — general** | 3 years | 4 years | HR personnel file | HR | Includes exit interview, separation checklist, reference policy acknowledgment |
| **FMLA/leave records** | 3 years | 3 years | HR (separate from personnel file if medical info included) | HR | FMLA notices, certifications, leave tracking records, return-to-work documentation |

## When "Legal Hold" Overrides Everything

If any of the following events occur, stop normal destruction schedules immediately and contact Legal:

- Receipt of an EEOC charge, state agency complaint, or DOL inquiry
- Service of a lawsuit or notice of litigation
- Any government audit, subpoena, or investigation
- Internal Ethics/Compliance investigation that could become external
- Any notification from Legal that a legal hold is in effect

Under a legal hold, records that would normally be destroyed must be preserved. Destroying documents after a legal hold notice is "spoliation" — courts treat it extremely unfavorably and can issue sanctions.

## Manager-Specific Guidance

### What managers personally control

Most records live in HR systems and are managed by HR. The records managers personally create and must be conscious of:

| Record Type | Manager Action |
|---|---|
| 1:1 notes with performance content | Retain for minimum 1 year; submit to HR if a PIP or termination follows |
| Interview notes and scoring rubrics | Submit to HR within 5 business days of the hiring decision; do not retain personal copies beyond 30 days |
| Email conversations about performance or conduct | Treat as potentially discoverable; write emails about employee performance as if a court will read them someday |
| Informal Slack messages about performance | Same as email — corporate Slack is discoverable |
| Text messages on corporate devices | Same — discoverable |

### What managers should never do

- Destroy or delete performance-related communications in anticipation of a complaint or claim
- Keep personal "shadow files" about employees that are not shared with HR
- Retain personal copies of medical information (accommodation requests, physician notes)
- Print and keep personal copies of HR personnel files
- Share investigation-related records with anyone not authorized by HR or Legal

## Quick Lookup by Scenario

| You are... | Records involved | Retention guidance |
|---|---|---|
| ...closing out a hiring process | Applications, interview notes, offer letter | Interview notes to HR within 5 days; HR retains all for 1–2 years |
| ...completing a PIP | All PIP documentation, check-in records, end assessment | Submit everything to HR; retain for 2–3 years |
| ...documenting a performance conversation | Your manager's notes | Keep for 1+ year; give HR a copy if formal action follows |
| ...concluding a harassment investigation | All investigation materials | HR / Legal retains for 5 years minimum, separate secure file |
| ...processing a termination | Termination letter, separation agreement, offboarding checklist | HR retains for 3–4 years |
| ...managing an employee's FMLA leave | All leave notices, certifications, return-to-work | HR retains for 3 years; medical info in separate file |
| ...notified of a lawsuit or complaint | Everything related to the person or issue | Legal hold — contact Legal immediately, do not destroy anything |
