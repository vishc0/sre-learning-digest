## Department OKR Set

> **Design principles before you write**:
> - **Objectives are qualitative, aspirational, and directional.** They answer "what do we want to be true by end of quarter?" — not "what will we do?"
> - **Key Results are quantitative and binary in spirit.** At quarter end, there is no ambiguity about whether you hit them. "Improve X" is not a KR. "Reduce X from N to M" is.
> - **3 objectives, 3 KRs each = 9 KRs total.** More than this and you are building a project plan, not an OKR set.
> - **Confidence at 70%.** A KR you are 100% sure you will hit is not a stretch — it is a commitment. A KR you cannot articulate a path to is not a target — it is a wish.
> - **One owner per KR.** Teams do not own KRs. Named individuals do.

## Confidence Score Guidance

> Use this calibration guide when setting confidence on each KR. Revisit confidence scores at each monthly check-in — they should change as you learn more.

| Confidence | Meaning | Action |
|------------|---------|--------|
| **< 40%** | You do not have a credible path to this target | Renegotiate the target or break it into a milestone KR for this quarter + a stretch for next |
| **50%** | Stretch / aspirational — you believe it is possible but cannot guarantee the path | Acceptable for innovation-type KRs; document the key bet you are making |
| **70%** | Well-calibrated — you have a plan, dependencies are identified, risks are known | **Google's recommended target confidence for most KRs** |
| **85%** | High confidence — you are very likely to hit this if nothing unexpected happens | Acceptable if the KR is a foundational prerequisite for other work |
| **≥ 90%** | Near-certain — you will hit this regardless of what happens | **Review for sandbagging.** If a KR is this certain, it may be a task, not a Key Result. Consider raising the target. |
