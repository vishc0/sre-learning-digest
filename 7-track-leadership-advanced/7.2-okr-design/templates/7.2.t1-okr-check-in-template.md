### Evidence

> **Evidence vs. assertion — this is critical**: Evidence is a specific, verifiable data point from a named system at a named point in time. An assertion is a claim without a traceable source. Your VP will ask "how do we know?" — answer that question before they ask it.

> Evidence examples (good):

> Assertion examples (bad — do not use):
> - "The team has been working hard on improving MTTD"

## Quarter Trajectory Assessment

> **Guidance**: Pick one status and justify it using the criteria below. Do not use Yellow to avoid delivering bad news. Red means you need VP help — use it when you need it.

| Status | Criteria | What it requires |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Green** | Overall score ≥ 0.6 AND no KR score < 0.3 AND blockers have owners and ETAs | Continue current plan. No VP intervention needed. |
| **Yellow** | Overall score 0.4–0.59 OR at least one KR score < 0.3 with a credible recovery plan | Present the plan to VP this cycle. May need resource or priority decision. |
| **Red** | Overall score < 0.4 OR at least one KR with no credible path to ≥ 0.5 by quarter end | Escalate immediately. VP needs to decide: reset target, reallocate resources, or accept the miss with documented rationale. |
