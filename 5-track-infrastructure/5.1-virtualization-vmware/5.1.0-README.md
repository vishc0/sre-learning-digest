# Virtualization & VMware — Training Framework

This framework is for SRE leaders and platform engineers preparing for Director/VP-level interviews who need to speak credibly about virtualization infrastructure, VMware architecture, and the post-Broadcom strategic landscape. It is not a VMware administrator guide — it is a decision-maker's reference. Every chapter anchors to mental models from RabbitMQ, Kubernetes, Kafka, and Splunk so you build on what you already know rather than starting from scratch.

## How to Navigate

### Path 1 — Quick Orientation (60 min)
For someone who needs working vocabulary fast — a vendor call tomorrow, an interview next week, or a platform review coming up.

1. `ch01-foundations-and-philosophy.md` — Get the Type 1/Type 2 and VM vs container distinction locked in
2. `ch02-vmware-architecture.md` — Understand how ESXi, vCenter, vSAN, and NSX fit together
3. `ch03-vmware-post-broadcom.md` — Know the Broadcom licensing story cold
4. `reference/hypervisor-comparison-matrix.md` — Know the landscape in one read

Skip the playbooks and templates until you need them operationally.

---

### Path 2 — Director Interview Prep (3–4 hours)
For VP/Director-level job search preparation where you'll be asked to make platform decisions under pressure.

1. `ch01` → `ch02` → `ch03` — Foundation sequence (must complete in order)
2. `ch04-migration-decision-framework.md` — The "stay vs migrate" decision is a common interview case
3. `ch08-director-decision-framework.md` — TCO framing and vendor negotiation stories
4. `playbook/vmware-broadcom-cost-assessment-playbook.md` — Walk an interviewer through how you'd actually do this
5. `templates/hypervisor-tco-comparison-template.md` — Know the model before you claim you'd build one
6. `ch07-disaster-recovery-with-vmware.md` — RTO/RPO at scale is a standard Director-level question

Practice: after each chapter, answer the question "If asked about this in an interview, what is my one-sentence lead and what example do I use from T-Mobile?"

---

### Path 3 — Deep Technical Study (Full framework, 8–10 hours)
For building durable knowledge of the domain — not just interview prep but the ability to lead platform decisions, run vendor evaluations, and own migrations.

Complete all 18 files in order. The chapters build on each other — networking (ch06) assumes architecture (ch02); DR (ch07) assumes resource management (ch05).

After completing chapters 1–9: run through all three playbooks as if executing a live engagement.
After playbooks: use templates as working documents for a hypothetical or real scenario.
Keep reference files open during any hands-on work.

## A Note on Analogies

Every chapter in this framework uses analogies drawn from middleware and platform engineering:

- **RabbitMQ** — used to explain vhost isolation, queue-level resource controls, and broker-level HA patterns
- **Kubernetes** — used to explain control plane / data plane separation, scheduling, resource policies, and overlay networking
- **Kafka** — used to explain replication, partition placement, and storage durability concepts in vSAN and vSphere HA
- **Splunk** — used where relevant for observability patterns and index tiering (maps to vSAN storage policy tiering)

The goal is not to make VMware sound like Kubernetes — they are different systems with different failure modes. The goal is to give you a starting mental model that is right enough to build on, rather than learning from a blank slate.
