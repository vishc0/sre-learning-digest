## Why Databases Are the Hardest Reliability Problem

Ask any Director of Platform Engineering what caused their worst incidents: the answer is almost always a database. Not because databases are poorly engineered — it's because they are the stateful core of every system, and state is hard. You can replace a failed application pod in 10 seconds. Replacing or recovering a database takes 10 minutes to 10 hours depending on how well you've prepared.

This framework teaches you to think about database reliability the way a Director does: not as an operational concern to delegate, but as a strategic investment with measurable outcomes tied to business risk.

## How to Use This Framework

**For interview prep**: Read ch01, ch03, and ch08 first. These contain the director-level narrative frames. Then read ch06 for SLO fluency.

**For operational readiness**: Start with the playbooks and references. Use templates in your next quarterly review.

**For a panel interview**: The Cassandra material in ch03 is your competitive differentiator. Every Director candidate says "we have replicas." Almost none can describe consistency level tradeoffs, compaction strategies, and repair operations from operational experience.

**For a system design interview**: ch05 and ch08 together give you the "managed vs. self-hosted" and "replicated vs. distributed" decision trees that panel interviewers are looking for.
