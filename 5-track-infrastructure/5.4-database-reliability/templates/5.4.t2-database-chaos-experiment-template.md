## 2. Hypothesis

> Write the hypothesis before you look at any data. A hypothesis that is written after the experiment is a post-hoc rationalization, not a scientific finding.

**Hypothesis statement** (fill in all four parts):

> "We believe that **[FAILURE_SCENARIO]** will result in **[OBSERVABLE_BEHAVIOR]** because **[REASONING]**. If our hypothesis is incorrect, we expect **[ALTERNATIVE_OUTCOME]** instead."

*Example: "We believe that killing one Cassandra node in the us-west-2a AZ will result in zero application errors and < 10% read latency increase because our replication factor is 3 with LOCAL_QUORUM consistency, meaning two healthy nodes can still satisfy quorum. If our hypothesis is incorrect, we expect to see a spike in ReadTimeout exceptions and client-visible 500 errors, indicating the application does not retry correctly or the replication factor is not what we believe."*

**What we are learning**: [LEARNING_GOAL] *(e.g., Validate that our RF=3 configuration survives single-node failure with no customer impact. Confirm our application retry logic works. Verify PagerDuty alert fires within 3 minutes.)*

**Prior experiments this builds on**: [PRIOR_EXPERIMENT_IDS or None]

## 7. Abort Criteria

> If **any** of the following conditions are observed, the conductor calls "ABORT" immediately. All observers have authority to call abort — there is no hierarchy in an abort decision. Rollback begins within 60 seconds of the abort call.

| Criterion | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Read/Write P99 latency | > [MULTIPLIER]x SLO for > [DURATION] *(e.g., > 2x SLO for > 2 minutes)* | Sustained latency breach exceeds acceptable customer impact |
| Error rate | > [THRESHOLD]% *(e.g., > 1%)* for > [DURATION] *(e.g., > 60s)* | Application errors reaching customers |
| Data loss detected | Any confirmed data loss | Non-negotiable — abort immediately |
| Cascading failure | Failure spreading beyond [TARGET_SCOPE] | Blast radius exceeded |
| Recovery time exceeded | > [DURATION] without signs of recovery after rollback starts *(e.g., > 5 minutes)* | Rollback is not working — escalate |
| PagerDuty alert not firing | Expected alert did not fire within [DURATION] *(e.g., 5 minutes of failure injection)* | Monitoring gap — abort and fix observability first |
| Conductor judgment | Any unexpected behavior that the conductor is not comfortable with | No justification required — safety first |

**Abort communication method**: [METHOD] *(e.g., Say "ABORT" on the call, type `!abort` in [SLACK_CHANNEL], call [ONCALL_NUMBER])*

### 11.2 Actual Behavior Observed

[Fill in during and after experiment — be specific. Include metric values, not just "latency went up."]

*Example: Node `cassandra-prod-3` was killed at 14:02 UTC. Within 45s, `nodetool status` showed the node as `DN`. Application read latency P99 rose from 38ms to 61ms (within SLO). Error rate remained at 0.02% — no spike. PagerDuty alert fired at 14:04:12 UTC (2m 12s after failure). The application continued serving traffic successfully throughout. Node was uncordoned at 14:17 UTC and returned to `UN` state at 14:19 UTC. Replication lag peaked at 3.2s during catch-up and normalized by 14:22 UTC.*

### 11.3 Unexpected Findings

[Document any behavior that was not anticipated — even if not negative. These are the most valuable learnings.]

The concurrent repair caused replication lag to spike higher than expected (3.2s vs. expected < 1s). Adding "confirm no repair running" to checklist.*
