# Datacenter Operations — Director/VP Interview Preparation Framework

## What This Is

A structured knowledge base for an SRE Manager with 21+ years of enterprise telecom experience preparing to interview for Director and VP Platform Engineering roles. Every chapter is written at the decision-making level — not "what is a UPS" but "how does UPS design affect your CapEx budget and your risk posture."

T-Mobile context is woven throughout: hybrid physical/cloud infrastructure, carrier-grade uptime expectations, enterprise compliance requirements, and the regulatory environment that makes a physical DC presence non-negotiable in parts of the business.

## Director-Level Themes Covered

**Capital allocation**: Tier choice, PUE impact on OpEx, TCO models for colo vs owned vs cloud, refresh cycle budgeting.

**Risk management**: Redundancy models (N, N+1, 2N, 2N+1), cooling failure cascade risk, power event sequencing, MTTR vs MTBF trade-offs.

**Compliance and audit readiness**: SOC 2 Type II physical controls, what auditors actually check, evidence packages, audit findings that block enterprise sales.

**Vendor and contract management**: Colo negotiation leverage, what to push on (remote hands SLA, cross-connect pricing), what colo providers never concede (power SLA structure), exit clause mechanics.

**Strategic framing**: DC as liability vs competitive moat — a nuanced argument for telecom specifically. When to exit to cloud vs when physical presence is the differentiator.

## How to Use This for Interview Prep

**30 minutes before an interview**: Read ch08 and the tier comparison reference. These two documents cover 80% of Director-level DC strategy questions.

**Deep dive sessions**: Read one chapter per session. End each chapter by writing one paragraph answering "what would I say if asked about this in a 45-minute VP interview?" Your answer, not a summary of the chapter.

**Incident scenarios**: The playbooks are the answer to "walk me through how your team responds to X." Don't memorize them — understand the decision logic at each step. Interviewers probe the decision points, not the sequence.

**Behavioral stories**: The templates show what operational maturity looks like. If you've used something like these at T-Mobile, that's a STAR answer waiting to be built.
