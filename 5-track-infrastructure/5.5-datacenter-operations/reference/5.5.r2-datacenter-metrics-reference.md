# Datacenter Metrics Quick Reference

**Use this table**: Instant lookup for any DC efficiency metric — formula, benchmark, what's good vs. bad.

---

## Primary Efficiency Metrics

### PUE — Power Usage Effectiveness

**Formula**:
```
PUE = Total Facility Power (kW) / IT Equipment Power (kW)
```

Where:
- **Total Facility Power**: Everything the utility meter measures — IT load + cooling + lighting + power conditioning losses + security systems
- **IT Equipment Power**: Power consumed by servers, storage, network equipment (measured at PDU output or server power supplies)

**Perfect score**: 1.0 (impossible in practice — means zero overhead)  
**Lower = better**

| PUE Value | Rating | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 1.03–1.1 | Exceptional | Hyperscaler purpose-built (Google, Meta, Microsoft) |
| 1.1–1.2 | Excellent | Modern facility, liquid cooling or airside economizer |
| 1.2–1.4 | Good | Well-run modern DC with hot/cold containment, economizer |
| 1.4–1.6 | Average | Standard enterprise DC, reasonable cooling, some containment |
| 1.6–2.0 | Below Average | Older facility, poor containment, no economizer |
| 2.0+ | Poor | Legacy facility, significant efficiency opportunity |

**Industry benchmarks by facility type** (2024 data):
- Hyperscaler (Google, AWS, Azure): 1.10–1.20
- Enterprise colo (Equinix, Digital Realty): 1.20–1.40
- Average US enterprise DC: 1.58 (Uptime Institute 2023 survey)
- Legacy on-premises: 1.7–2.5+

**PUE dollar impact** (at $0.08/kWh commercial rate, 10MW IT load):

| From PUE | To PUE | Annual Savings |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| 2.0 | 1.5 | $3.5M/year |
| 1.8 | 1.4 | $2.8M/year |
| 1.5 | 1.2 | $2.1M/year |
| 1.4 | 1.2 | $1.4M/year |

*Formula: Savings = IT_load_kW × (PUE_old - PUE_new) × 8,760 hours × $/kWh*

**PUE measurement frequency**: Annualized PUE uses 12-month rolling average. Point-in-time PUE can vary by season (cooling loads are higher in summer). Always compare PUE year-over-year at the same seasonal point if using point-in-time.

### DCiE — Data Center Infrastructure Efficiency

**Formula**:
```
DCiE (%) = (IT Equipment Power / Total Facility Power) × 100
         = (1 / PUE) × 100
```

**Higher = better** (inverse of PUE)

| PUE | DCiE |
|-----|------|
| 1.0 | 100% (theoretical perfect) |
| 1.2 | 83.3% |
| 1.5 | 66.7% |
| 2.0 | 50.0% |

DCiE is the same metric as PUE expressed differently. Some organizations prefer DCiE because "higher is better" is more intuitive for a business audience. PUE is more commonly used in technical literature.

### CUE — Carbon Usage Effectiveness

**Formula**:
```
CUE = Total CO₂ Emissions (metric tons/year) / IT Equipment Energy (kWh/year)
```

**Lower = better**. A CUE of 0 would mean zero carbon emissions from the facility.

**Why it matters**: CUE adds the carbon intensity of the power source to PUE's efficiency measure. A facility with PUE 1.2 running on coal power may have higher CUE than a facility with PUE 1.5 running on renewable energy. For ESG reporting and sustainability commitments, CUE is the more meaningful metric.

**Benchmarks**:
- 0.0: 100% renewable energy source
- 0.3–0.5: Mix of renewables + low-carbon grid
- 0.8–1.2: Average US grid (varies by region and season)
- 1.5+: Carbon-intensive grid (coal-heavy)

**CUE = PUE × Carbon Intensity Factor** (where carbon intensity is the grams of CO₂ per kWh of the grid or power purchase)

### WUE — Water Usage Effectiveness

**Formula**:
```
WUE = Annual Site Water Usage (liters) / IT Equipment Energy (kWh)
```

**Units**: liters/kWh  
**Lower = better**

Water is consumed by cooling towers (evaporative cooling), adiabatic cooling systems, and humidification systems. Facilities using airside economization or direct liquid cooling with dry coolers have significantly lower WUE than evaporative tower-based cooling.

| WUE | Context |
|-----|---------|
| 0.0 | No water cooling (all airside) |
| 0.2–0.5 | Efficient hybrid cooling |
| 1.0–1.8 | Standard cooling tower-based cooling |
| 2.0+ | High water consumption — notable in water-scarce regions |

WUE is becoming more significant as datacenter water consumption faces regulatory scrutiny in drought-prone regions.

## Rack Density Metrics

### kW/Rack — Average Rack Power Density

**Formula**:
```
Average kW/rack = Total IT Power (kW) / Total Populated Racks
```

| Era / Workload Type | Typical kW/Rack | Cooling Implication |
|--------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Legacy (pre-2010) | 2–4 kW | Air cooled, any layout |
| General enterprise compute (2015–2020) | 5–10 kW | Air cooled, containment recommended |
| Dense compute / modern enterprise (2020–2024) | 10–20 kW | Air cooled with containment required; approaching limits |
| AI training (GPU clusters) | 30–80 kW | Liquid cooling required (DLC or RDHx) |
| AI inference / HPC | 20–50 kW | Rear-door HX minimum; DLC preferred |
| Future AI/accelerated compute | 100+ kW/rack | Immersion or full DLC required |

**Cooling ceiling for air cooling**: ~20–25 kW/rack is the practical limit for hot/cold aisle containment air cooling. Above this, specialized cooling solutions are required regardless of facility design.

## Power-Related Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Good Value | Notes |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------|
| PDU utilization | Actual kW / Rated kW | < 80% (NEC continuous load rule) | Above 80% is a code violation for continuous loads |
| Power headroom | (NEC limit − Actual) / NEC limit | > 20% headroom | < 10% triggers capacity alert |
| Generator fuel runtime | Fuel capacity (gal) / Burn rate (gal/hr) | ≥ 72 hours | 72-hour reserve is the enterprise standard target |
| UPS battery runtime | Varies by system | ≥ 10 minutes at full load | Must cover generator start + ATS transfer + margin |
| UPS load (% of rated) | Actual load / UPS rated output | 50–75% | < 40% = oversized UPS; > 85% = undersized |
| A/B feed balance | ABS(A_kW − B_kW) / MAX(A_kW, B_kW) | < 20% imbalance | Significant imbalance means single-feed equipment risk |
