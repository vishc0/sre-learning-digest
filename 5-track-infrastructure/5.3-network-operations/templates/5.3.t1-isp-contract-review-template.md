## 2. SLA Terms Review

Work through each commitment. For each: confirm the target is documented in the contract body (not just a sales slide), understand how it is measured, and note the credit that applies if missed.

### 2.1 Availability

- [ ] **Availability commitment documented**: [FILL: e.g., 99.9%]
  - Monthly downtime allowance at this target: [FILL: e.g., 99.9% = 43.8 min/month]
  - Measurement method stated in contract: [FILL: e.g., provider-side ping monitoring / customer-reported outages only]
  - **Watch**: "customer-reported" measurement is a red flag — it means they only count what you open a ticket for. Push for provider-side synthetic monitoring.
- [ ] **Availability SLA applies to**: [FILL: full circuit availability / partial degradation counted / only complete outages]

### 2.2 Latency

- [ ] **Latency commitment documented**: [FILL: e.g., < 20ms RTT within region]
  - Measurement endpoints specified: [FILL: from/to specific PoPs, or vague "within the network"?]
  - Measurement method: [FILL: e.g., ICMP RTT average / P95 / P99]
  - **Watch**: Latency SLAs that specify averages instead of percentiles are nearly meaningless — a 100ms spike is hidden in a 10ms average. Push for P95 or P99 commitment.

### 2.3 Packet Loss

- [ ] **Packet loss commitment documented**: [FILL: e.g., < 0.1% within provider backbone]
  - Measurement method: [FILL: e.g., interface counters on provider routers / synthetic probes]
  - Measurement window: [FILL: e.g., 5-minute average / hourly average]

### 2.4 Jitter (Required for Voice / Video / Real-Time Services)

- [ ] **Jitter commitment documented**: [FILL: e.g., < 5ms one-way delay variation]
  - Applicable if: voice, video conferencing, real-time trading, gaming infrastructure
  - If not in contract and you carry voice/video: **this is a negotiation item before signing**

### 2.5 Mean Time to Repair (MTTR)

- [ ] **MTTR commitment documented**: [FILL: e.g., 4-hour MTTR for primary circuits, 8-hour for backup]
  - Clock starts: [FILL: when customer opens ticket / when provider detects fault — prefer provider-detected]
  - Commitment applies: [FILL: 24×7 / business hours only — 24×7 required for production circuits]
  - Escalation MTTR: [FILL: does MTTR accelerate if escalated?]
  - **Red flag**: MTTR > 8 hours for a mission-critical internet circuit is unacceptable. For dark fiber or wave, 24-48 hours may be typical, but verify what your RTO requires.

### 2.6 Mean Time to Respond (MTTR first response)

- [ ] **First response time documented**: [FILL: e.g., ticket acknowledgment within 15 minutes]

## 3. Credit Provisions Review

Service credits are your only contractual remedy for SLA failures. Understand the math before you sign — credits are often too small to matter unless negotiated.

### 3.1 Credit Mechanics

- [ ] **Credit calculation method documented**
  - [FILL: describe how credits are calculated — e.g., "1/720 of MRC per hour of downtime" or "fixed tiered credits based on outage duration"]
  - Credit calculation example for a 4-hour outage at your MRC: [FILL: work the math — e.g., $10,000 MRC × (4/720) = $55.56 credit for a 4-hour outage on a $10k circuit. Is that meaningful?]

- [ ] **Credit cap documented**: [FILL: e.g., "credits capped at 30% of monthly MRC"]
  - **Red flag benchmark**: Credit cap < 30% of MRC for a mission-critical circuit. If a circuit is down for the entire month, you should recover a meaningful portion of cost.
  - Target to negotiate: 100% MRC credit for full-month outage on primary circuits. Carriers will resist; push for at least 50%.

- [ ] **Credit claim window documented**: [FILL: e.g., "credits must be claimed within 30 days of incident"]
  - **Watch**: Short claim windows (< 30 days) combined with slow RCA delivery means you may miss the window. Negotiate for ≥60 days.

- [ ] **Credit form**: [ ] Invoice credit  [ ] Check  [ ] Service extension  [ ] Account credit
  - Prefer invoice credit — it reduces next month's bill automatically without process friction.

- [ ] **Credit process documented**: [FILL: how do you file a claim? Is it a form, a ticket, a phone call? Who approves?]

### 3.2 Credit Red Flags

| Red Flag | Present? | Severity |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Credits only for full outages, not degradation | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | High |
| MTTR clock starts at customer ticket, not problem detection | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | High |
| Credit cap < 30% of MRC | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | High |
| Credit claim window < 30 days | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | Medium |
| No credit for latency or packet loss violations | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | Medium |
| Credit in "service extension" form only | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | Medium |
| No jitter SLA for voice/video circuits | [ ] Yes / [ ] No | Medium (if applicable) |

## 5. Physical Diversity Review

Physical diversity is the most important and most overlooked part of network contracts. Two circuits from two providers that share a fiber conduit give you zero redundancy.

- [ ] **Fiber path documentation requested**: [FILL: Yes — received / Yes — pending / No — need to request]
  - Document what you received: [FILL: describe the fiber path documentation — route maps, pole/duct IDs, entry points]
  - **If not provided**: escalate to account manager and make it a contract condition. If they refuse, this is a major risk flag.

- [ ] **Last-mile entry point confirmed as physically separate from other ISPs**:
  - Entry point location: [FILL: which building entrance, riser, or MDF room does this circuit use?]
  - Other ISP entry point location: [FILL: for contrast — do they share a building entrance?]
  - Confirmed different: [ ] Yes / [ ] No / [ ] Unconfirmed — investigation needed

- [ ] **Facility type confirmed**:
  - [ ] Carrier-neutral colocation (e.g., Equinix, CoreSite) — preferred; multiple carrier options
  - [ ] Provider-owned facility — risk: if provider has a facility issue, both your circuit and their NOC may be affected
  - [ ] Customer premise / on-net building — risk: single fiber entry point, provider-managed last mile

- [ ] **Shared infrastructure risk**:
  - Does this provider share backbone, conduit, or last mile with any other provider already in contract? [FILL: Yes / No / Unknown]
  - If Yes: [FILL: describe the shared segment and assess whether it negates diversity]

- [ ] **Meet-me room (MMR) confirmed**: [FILL: which MMR does the circuit terminate in? Is it the same MMR as the other carrier?]

## 7. Negotiation Leverage Points

Use these before finalizing the contract. The time to negotiate is before signing, not during an outage.

| Item | What to Push For | Carrier Likely Position | Priority |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|---------|
| Credit cap | ≥ 50% of MRC; 100% for full-month outage | 25–30% of MRC | High |
| Credit claim window | ≥ 60 days | 30 days | Medium |
| MTTR clock start | Provider fault detection, not customer ticket | Customer ticket open | High |
| Fiber path documentation | Required before circuit activation | Resist disclosure | High |
| Escalation SLA | Committed response times at each tier, in contract body | Best-efforts language | Medium |
| Early termination fee (ETF) | Prorate linearly, cap at 50% of remaining term | Full remaining MRC | Medium |
| Rate lock | Full term; no mid-term rate adjustments | Annual CPI adjustment clause | Medium |
| Renewal notice window | 90-day notice before auto-renew | 30 days | Medium |
| SLA credits for partial degradation | Latency and packet loss violations included | Availability only | Medium |
