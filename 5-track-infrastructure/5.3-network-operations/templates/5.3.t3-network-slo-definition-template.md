## 4. Error Budget

### 4.1 Error Budget Calculation

For a **[FILL: e.g., 99.95%]** availability SLO over a rolling 28-day window:

```
Total minutes in window        = 28 days × 24 hours × 60 min = 40,320 minutes
Allowed downtime (error budget) = 40,320 × (1 - 0.9995) = 20.16 minutes/28-day window
Allowed downtime per month      ≈ 21.9 minutes/calendar month
```

| SLO Target | Error Budget (28-day) | Error Budget (monthly) |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 99.99% | 4.03 minutes | 4.38 minutes |
| 99.95% | 20.16 minutes | 21.9 minutes |
| 99.9% | 40.32 minutes | 43.8 minutes |
| 99.5% | 201.6 minutes (3.36 hours) | 219 minutes |

**Current remaining budget**: [FILL: updated weekly — e.g., "As of YYYY-MM-DD, 14.2 of 20.16 minutes remain (70.4% remaining)"]

### 4.2 Error Budget Burn Rate

Burn rate = (error budget consumed rate) / (error budget available rate)

| Burn Rate | Interpretation | Response |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| < 1x | Budget consuming slower than replenishing | Normal operations |
| 1x | Exactly on track — will exhaust at window end | Watch closely |
| 2x | Will exhaust in 14 days | Notify SRE team lead |
| 5x | Will exhaust in ~5.5 days | Freeze non-critical changes |
| 14.4x | Will exhaust in 2 hours (fast burn) | Page on-call, incident response |

### 4.3 Error Budget Policy

- **> 50% remaining**: Feature velocity unrestricted. Experimentation allowed.
- **25–50% remaining**: Director-level review required for high-risk changes. Inform stakeholders.
- **< 25% remaining**: Change freeze except for reliability improvements. Weekly director report required.
- **0% remaining**: All non-emergency changes halted. Daily exec report. Reliability sprint initiated.
- **Recovery**: Budget resets on rolling basis as incidents age out of the 28-day window.

## 7. SLO Exceptions and Exclusions Policy

### 7.1 Approved Exclusion Categories

The following event types are excluded from SLO calculation when properly documented in the incident record:

| Exclusion Type | Requirements | Approval Required |
|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Planned maintenance | Declared ≥24h in advance via change management; customer notification sent | SRE Manager |
| Emergency maintenance | Declared ≥2h in advance where possible; retroactive justification if not | Director |
| Force majeure (natural disaster, carrier backbone failure) | Documented with third-party evidence | VP/Director |
| Carrier maintenance (ISP-initiated) | Carrier change ticket number on file; 48h advance notice received | None (auto-excluded) |
| DDoS mitigation (traffic scrubbing period) | Security incident ticket open; scrubbing duration logged | Security Manager |

### 7.2 Exclusion Process

1. Open an exclusion request ticket during or within 24 hours of the event.
2. Tag the incident record with `slo-exclusion-candidate`.
3. SRE Manager reviews and approves/rejects within 48 hours.
4. Approved exclusions are applied to the SLO calculation in the monthly review.
5. All exclusions are reported to stakeholders in the monthly SLO report — transparency is mandatory.

### 7.3 Exclusion Limits

- Planned maintenance exclusions are capped at [FILL: e.g., 4 hours/month] without Director approval.
- No more than [FILL: e.g., 3] planned maintenance windows per 28-day period without stakeholder notification.
