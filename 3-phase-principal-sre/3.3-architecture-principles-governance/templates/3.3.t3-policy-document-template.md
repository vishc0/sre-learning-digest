> This section contains the mandatory requirements. Each requirement must be:
> - Written with "must" or "must not" — not "should," "shall," or "is expected to"
> - Specific and unambiguous — a compliance auditor can determine pass/fail without interpretation
> - Auditable — there is an observable artifact (config, ADR, log, scan result) that proves compliance

> Number every statement. Each numbered statement is separately auditable and enforceable.

## 6. Consequences of Violation

> Consequences must be proportional, graduated, and applied consistently.
> Inconsistent enforcement destroys the policy's credibility faster than anything else.
> Document the escalation path so no manager is surprised by what happens next.

**Detection → Response**:

| Severity | Definition | Response | Timeline |
|----------|-----------|---------|----------|
| Minor | First occurrence, no operational impact, self-reported or discovered in routine audit | Document finding. Team writes remediation plan. VP informed. | Remediation within 30 days |
| Moderate | Repeat occurrence, OR single occurrence with operational impact (incident caused or contributed to) | ARB review required. Named remediation plan with VP-approved deadline. Architecture Team monitors compliance. | ARB review within 14 days; remediation plan within 7 days of ARB review |
| Serious | Systemic violation (3+ occurrences), OR regulatory/audit finding, OR violation caused a Sev-1 incident | VP-directed remediation program. Formal audit finding logged. Domain leadership accountability review. | Immediate escalation to VP; remediation timeline set by VP |

**Escalation path**:
- Minor: [Domain Lead] → documents and resolves
- Moderate: [Domain Lead] → [Architecture Team] → [ARB] → [VP informed]
- Serious: [Domain Lead] → [VP] directly → [ARB] → [Audit/Compliance if regulatory impact]

> **On repeat violations**: A pattern of Minor violations from the same team signals either a policy
> that cannot be complied with (policy needs revision) or a culture problem (leadership intervention
> needed). Either requires VP attention.
