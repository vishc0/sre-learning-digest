## ADR Quality Checklist

> Run this checklist before submitting for review. An ADR that fails 3 or more items will be returned.

- [ ] **Context explains WHY this decision was needed now** — not just what was decided, but what forced the decision (incident, volume, regulatory trigger, business commitment)
- [ ] **At least two options were evaluated** — with specific pros, cons, and a reason each was chosen or rejected
- [ ] **Decision statement is one sentence** — no paragraph, no justification in the decision statement itself
- [ ] **Consequences section is honest about trade-offs** — negative consequences are documented; this is not a marketing document
- [ ] **All relevant principles have been assessed** — no principle left blank unless N/A with explanation
- [ ] **Required follow-on work has named owners and due dates** — not just "someone will do this"
- [ ] **Review date is set** — with a specific trigger condition if timeline-based review is insufficient
- [ ] **Tier assignment is correct** — Tier 1 and Tier 2 decisions have ARB reference section populated after review
- [ ] **Title follows Verb-Noun convention** — e.g., "Use Kafka for Order Event Streaming" not "Kafka Decision" or "Messaging Architecture"
- [ ] **This ADR can stand alone** — a new team member reading only this document would understand the decision, why it was made, and what changed as a result
