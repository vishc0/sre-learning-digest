# Technical Debt Register

> **What this register is**: A risk register, not a backlog. It tracks the engineering liabilities that
> are consuming operational capacity, slowing delivery velocity, or creating unacceptable risk exposure —
> whether or not those liabilities will be fixed in the next sprint.
>
> **Who owns it**: The VP of Operations is the register owner. Domain engineering leads maintain the
> debt items within their domains. The Architecture Team audits the register quarterly for completeness.
>
> **Who reviews it**: The VP and domain leads review the full register quarterly. High-priority items
> are reviewed monthly. Items marked "Kills" (existential risk) are escalated immediately to VP and
> domain leadership regardless of sprint cycle.
>
> **Priority Score formula**: `(Annual Interest Estimate + Risk Premium) / Fix Cost Estimate`
> - Annual Interest = engineering time consumed per year by this debt (incidents + velocity tax + security overhead)
> - Risk Premium = 0 (Low risk), 5 (Medium risk), 15 (High risk — Tier 1 system, active CVE, or regulatory path)
> - Fix Cost = estimated engineer-weeks to resolve
> - Higher score = higher priority. Score > 1.0 means you are spending more maintaining the debt than it would cost to fix it.
>
> **Review cadence**: Full register quarterly. All items scored. Scores change as operational evidence accumulates.

### Operational Risk Assessment

**Risk Level**: High / Medium / Low

> High = active CVEs, Tier 1 system, on a regulatory audit path, or has caused a Sev-1 in the last 6 months.
> Medium = has caused Sev-2 or Sev-3 incidents; degrades MTTR; slows development measurably.
> Low = known issue, manageable impact, no recent incident contribution.

**Operational Impact**:
- **Incident contribution**: [How many incidents in the past 4 quarters (rolling) trace to this debt item? Cite incident IDs if known — e.g., "INC-20260115, INC-20260312 — both root-caused to this pattern."]
- **MTTR impact**: [Does this debt increase time-to-diagnose or time-to-fix? By how much? — e.g., "Lack of structured logging adds ~45 minutes to mean diagnosis time per incident"]
- **Velocity impact**: [Does this debt slow feature development? Estimate hours/week consumed — e.g., "Estimated 6 hrs/week across the team: 2 hrs debugging unexpected side effects, 4 hrs manual regression testing due to no unit tests"]
- **Security exposure**: [Any active CVEs? Known exploit paths? Unpatched libraries? — e.g., "CVE-2024-XXXXX: CVSS 8.1, affects NGINX < 1.25.3; currently running 1.18.0"]
- **Regulatory exposure**: [Is this on the path of any compliance audit? PCI-DSS, SOC 2, GDPR? — e.g., "Billing data on this path; PCI-DSS Requirement 6.3.3 requires patching high-severity CVEs within 1 month of release"]

**"Kills" Category**: Yes / No

> Mark "Yes" ONLY if this debt item, if unaddressed, could result in: a sustained Sev-1 taking down a Tier 1 system,
> a regulatory finding that triggers a stop-sell or consent order, or a security breach via a known exploit path.
> A "Kills" classification triggers immediate VP escalation outside the quarterly review cycle.

[If Yes: state specifically why this is existential risk. E.g., "Active CVSS 9.1 CVE in the authentication library with a published exploit. If exploited, attacker has unauthenticated access to the customer data store. Tier 1 system. Kills = Yes."]
