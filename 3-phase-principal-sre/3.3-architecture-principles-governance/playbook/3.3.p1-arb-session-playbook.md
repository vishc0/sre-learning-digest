# ARB Session Playbook — VP/ARB Chair

**Audience**: VP or designated ARB Chair  
**Purpose**: Run a tight, decision-producing Architecture Review Board session  
**Cadence**: Weekly 60-minute session + monthly 2-3 hour deep dive  

---

## Pre-Session (48 Hours Before)

**VP/Chair actions — non-negotiable before the session opens:**

- [ ] Agenda circulated to all ARB members: each proposal listed with submitting team, requested decision tier, and link to ADR draft
- [ ] Pre-read requirement communicated: every ARB member must have read all submitted materials before arriving — this is not optional
- [ ] Async decisions dispatched: proposals meeting async criteria (Tier 2, complete materials, no obvious conflicts) circulated for async vote — requires 4+ approvals within the 48-hour window; record results before session
- [ ] Quorum check completed: confirm 4+ of 6 core members available. If quorum fails, reschedule within 72 hours — do not delay proposals unnecessarily
- [ ] Chair prep done: VP/Chair reviews all proposals independently, identifies principle conflicts or standards deviations that need framing before open discussion begins

**Async approval criteria (all must be true):**
- Tier 2 decision (no cross-domain dependency changes, no exceptions)
- ADR draft complete — all required sections filled
- Principles self-assessment completed by submitting team
- No ARB member has flagged a concern within the first 24 hours of circulation

---

## Session Agenda Template (60-Minute Weekly Session)

```
0:00 – 0:05  | Housekeeping
              |   Quorum confirmed (name who is present)
              |   Agenda confirmed (any last-minute additions or removals?)
              |   Prior session decisions ratified (no debate — just confirm the record is accurate)

0:05 – 0:15  | Async decisions — formal recording
              |   Read each async decision aloud: proposal, vote count, outcome
              |   Record in decision log
              |   No debate. If a member wants to re-open an async decision, it goes to next session.

0:15 – 0:45  | Active proposals (maximum 2 per session, 15 minutes each)
              |   Per proposal:
              |     0:00 – 0:05  Presenting team presents: problem, decision, why this option, what they need approved
              |     0:05 – 0:10  ARB questions (chair manages the clock — cut off at 5 minutes)
              |     0:10 – 0:14  ARB deliberation (presenting team may be asked to step out)
              |     0:14 – 0:15  Decision called and recorded by scribe

0:45 – 0:55  | Follow-up items
              |   Conditions from prior approvals — status check only
              |   If a condition is overdue: escalate to named owner, set new deadline, record in log
              |   Not a discussion — a status check

0:55 – 1:00  | Governance health (30 seconds per metric)
              |   ADRs overdue for finalization (approval granted but ADR not finalized within 2 weeks)
              |   Open exceptions count — any newly overdue?
              |   ARB cycle time — are we meeting SLA commitments?
              |   If any metric is red: flag for chair's follow-up action, not session discussion
```

---

## Presentation Requirements Checklist

The VP/Chair verifies this before any proposal proceeds. If any item is missing, the proposal is returned — no exceptions.

- [ ] ADR draft complete: Title, Status=Proposed, Context, Options Considered (≥2 alternatives with pros/cons), Decision, Consequences
- [ ] Principles self-assessment: all 12 principles marked Compliant / Not Applicable / Exception Required — with brief rationale for anything marked Exception Required
- [ ] Standards compliance: all technology selections mapped to their Technology Radar quadrant
- [ ] Operational readiness attestation: observability plan documented, runbook owner named, rollback strategy documented
- [ ] Dependency map: services this change depends on + services that depend on this change — new dependencies explicitly highlighted
- [ ] Exception request form (if applicable): completed and attached before the session, not during

**Chair action when materials are incomplete:** Return the proposal to "pending pre-read complete" status. Communicate to the submitting team what is missing and by when it must be corrected. Do not debate incomplete proposals — this wastes ARB time and rewards teams that underprepare.

## Decision Recording (Scribe — Real-Time During Session)

The ARB scribe records every decision in the decision log during the session, not after:

| Field | What to Record |
|-------|---------------|
| Date | Session date |
| Proposal title | Exact name from the agenda |
| Submitting team | Team name and lead |
| Decision | Approve / Approve with Conditions / Defer / Reject with Alternative |
| Conditions | If conditional: each condition stated specifically, named owner, deadline |
| Rejection rationale | If rejected: the alternative recommendation — ARB never returns a bare "No" |
| ADR reference | ADR number (fill in post-session after the ADR is finalized) |

**Decisions the ARB can make:**

- **Approve**: proceed as proposed. ADR must be finalized within 2 weeks.
- **Approve with Conditions**: proceed, but conditions must be met by stated deadline. Conditions are tracked in the decision log; named owner is accountable. Return to ARB for verification if required.
- **Defer**: submitting team needs to provide specific additional information. ARB specifies exactly what is missing — a Defer is not a soft rejection. Resubmission timeline agreed during the session.
- **Reject with Alternative**: ARB provides a specific alternative path forward. Rejected proposals are never left without a recommended direction.

---

## Post-Session (Within 24 Hours)

- [ ] Decision log updated with full session outcomes
- [ ] Decision log distributed to all ARB members and all submitting teams
- [ ] Conditional approvals: conditions entered into tracking system with named owners and due dates; follow-up sessions scheduled
- [ ] Rejected proposals: alternative recommendation formalized in writing; submitting team notified with explicit path forward
- [ ] ADR finalization reminders sent to all teams with approvals in the last 2 weeks

## Monthly Deep-Dive Session (2–3 Hours)

Held once per month, replacing or extending the regular weekly session. Additional agenda items:

**Technology Radar Review (30–45 minutes)**
- Review technologies currently in Trial: are any ready to promote to Adopt? Any that failed evaluation and should move to Hold?
- Any new technology assessments to present from the field?
- Any technologies in Adopt that show signs of needing deprecation?

**Standards Evolution (20–30 minutes)**
- Any existing standards that are outdated and should be deprecated?
- Any Trial technologies ready to be promoted to a formal standard?
- Any standards with widespread non-compliance (suggesting the standard is wrong, not the teams)?

**Architecture Health Review (20 minutes)**
- ADR coverage: what percentage of architectural decisions made in the last quarter have an associated ADR?
- ARB compliance rate: what percentage of changes that required ARB review actually went through ARB review?
- Cycle time trend: is the ARB getting faster or slower? What is driving the change?

**Open Exceptions Audit (15 minutes)**
- Any exceptions past their sunset date?
- Any exceptions due for annual renewal — are teams prepared with their renewal case?
- Any exceptions generating disproportionate incidents (cost of exception becoming visible)?

**Post-Incident Architecture Findings (15 minutes)**
- PIR findings from the quarter that require architectural governance response
- Any recurring incident patterns that indicate a standards or principles gap?
- Any PIR recommendations that require a new ADR or an exception to be closed?

## ARB Chair Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Letting debate run past the time box.**  
The chair's job is to protect the agenda. Call time, state the remaining decision options, and force a vote. An ARB that debates endlessly produces no decisions — and teams learn to avoid bringing proposals.

**Approving incomplete proposals "because the team is under pressure."**  
This is how rubber-stamping begins. Every exception to the pre-read requirement teaches teams that incomplete materials are acceptable. The team's deadline is not the ARB's problem — that is a delivery planning failure to address separately.

**Making the decision before the ARB deliberates.**  
The chair frames the discussion and manages the clock. The chair does not telegraph a preferred outcome before the ARB has spoken. If the chair has a strong view, express it during deliberation, not before.

**Skipping the governance health check.**  
Thirty seconds at the end of every session. This is how overdue ADRs, stacking exceptions, and SLA misses get caught early. Skipping it means governance degrades silently between quarterly reviews.

**Deferring without specifying what is needed.**  
A Defer without specifics is a soft rejection that wastes everyone's time. When deferring, state exactly: what information is missing, who should provide it, and when it should be resubmitted.

**Allowing the ARB to become a bottleneck.**  
If teams are complaining about ARB cycle times, that is a VP problem to solve — not a reason to lower the bar. Solutions: increase async approval use, add Tier 2 fast-track criteria, or add ARB capacity. Do not resolve a bottleneck by weakening governance.
