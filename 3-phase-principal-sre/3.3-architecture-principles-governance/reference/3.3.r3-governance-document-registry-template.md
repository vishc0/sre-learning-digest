# Governance Document Registry

**This registry is the single source of truth for all governance artifacts in the Operations domain.**

If a document is not in this registry, it does not officially exist as a governance artifact — it is an
informal document with no enforcement standing, no review accountability, and no audit trail. Teams may
choose to comply with or ignore informal documents at will. Only registered documents carry the authority
of the governance framework.

**Registry Owner**: Architecture Team
**VP Accountable**: VP Operations
**Review Cadence**: Registry is audited quarterly alongside the debt register. All documents with overdue
reviews are reported to the VP.

**How to register a new document**: Submit the document to the Architecture Team with a completed Document
Registration Request (template at [link]). The Architecture Team assigns the Doc ID, validates the owner
and review schedule, and adds the entry within 5 business days.

## Registry Health Metrics

> Run this assessment quarterly. A healthy governance framework has all documents reviewed on schedule
> and no stale documents in active circulation.

**How to assess registry health:**

| Metric | Healthy | Caution | Critical |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| % of Level 1-3 documents reviewed within schedule | > 90% | 75-90% | < 75% |
| # of documents with overdue review (Next Review < today) | 0 | 1-2 | 3+ |
| # of Level 3 policies with no compliance audit in last 12 months | 0 | 1-2 | 3+ |
| # of active documents not in the registry (discovered via team audit) | 0 | 1-2 | 3+ |
| % of ADRs linked from the registry (for reference) | > 80% | 60-80% | < 60% |

**Overdue Review Alert**:

> Run this query against the registry quarterly: find all rows where `Next Review` < today's date and Status = Active.
> These are governance gaps — the VP must approve either: (a) an updated review date with commitment, or
> (b) a deprecation of the document. Documents cannot remain active without a scheduled review.

> Tip: Add this as a standing agenda item in the quarterly architecture review. Five minutes to scan the
> registry is less expensive than an audit finding that a critical policy hasn't been reviewed in 18 months.
