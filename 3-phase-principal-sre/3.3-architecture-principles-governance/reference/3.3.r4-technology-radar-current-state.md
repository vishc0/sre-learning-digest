# Technology Radar — Current State

**Version**: Q2 2026
**Published**: 2026-04-01
**Next Scheduled Update**: 2026-07-01 (quarterly cadence)
**Owner**: Architecture Team
**Authority**: This radar is a Level 2 Standards document. Technologies in the Adopt ring are the required
default. Technologies in Hold should not be used in new systems and should be migrated away from in
existing systems on a defined timeline.

> **How to read this radar**: The radar is organized into four quadrants (Languages & Frameworks,
> Platforms & Infrastructure, Data Management, Tools & Techniques) and four rings:
>
> - **Adopt**: Use this. It is proven at our scale, operationally understood by our SRE team, and
>   supported by platform tooling. Choosing an Adopt-ring technology requires no additional approval.
>
> - **Trial**: Worth using in a limited capacity. These technologies have a clear value proposition
>   and are being evaluated across one or more non-critical or bounded use cases. Trial technologies
>   require Architecture Team awareness (notify, don't ask permission) before adoption.
>
> - **Assess**: Explore but don't commit. These are technologies worth tracking — promising but not
>   yet proven at our scale, or not yet operationally understood. Assessing a technology means running
>   a spike or a proof of concept with Architecture Team involvement. Not for production systems.
>
> - **Hold**: Do not start new work with this technology. Existing systems using Hold-ring technologies
>   should have a migration plan. Using a Hold-ring technology in a new system requires an ARB-approved
>   exception.

## How to Propose a Radar Change

> Any engineer or team can propose a technology be added, moved, or removed from the radar.
> The Architecture Team reviews all proposals at the quarterly radar session.

**Process:**

1. **Create a Radar Change Proposal** in the Architecture Team Confluence space (template at [link]).
   - Include: technology name and version, proposed ring, rationale (why this ring), operational evidence (if moving from Trial to Adopt: what production experience justifies this?), alternatives evaluated.

2. **Share for team input** — post the proposal in the #architecture Slack channel at least two weeks before the quarterly radar session. Engineers who have experience with the technology should comment.

3. **Architecture Team review** at the quarterly session. The team votes on the ring placement. Majority required; VP breaks ties.

4. **ARB notification** — Adopt-ring moves and Hold designations are reported to the ARB at the next session. ARB does not vote on radar placement but may raise concerns.

5. **Radar update** — published within 5 business days of the quarterly session. Teams notified via #engineering-announce.

**Key questions the review process answers:**
- Is this technology operationally understood by the SRE team?
- Is there a runbook? Are there trained on-call engineers?
- What is the security posture (CVE track record, patch cadence, license terms)?
- What is the operational cost (managed vs. self-hosted, licensing, training)?
- What is the migration path for teams currently using alternatives?
