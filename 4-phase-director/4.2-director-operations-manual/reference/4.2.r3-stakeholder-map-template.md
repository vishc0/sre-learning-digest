# Stakeholder Map Template

**Purpose**: Track every Director-level relationship that affects your ability to operate — authority above you, peers you depend on, customers you serve, vendors you manage, and regulators you comply with. An unmapped stakeholder is an unmanaged risk.

**When to use**: Fill in the first draft by end of Day 60. Update the "Last Interaction Date" and "Relationship Health" columns monthly. Full review and re-assessment quarterly.

---

## Instructions

1. **First pass (Day 30)**: List all names. Do not assess health until you have met each person at least twice.
2. **Day 60 update**: Fill in health ratings based on actual interactions. Be honest — Amber is not a failure, it is useful information.
3. **Monthly review**: Update Last Interaction Date and Health columns. Any relationship that has gone more than 30 days without direct contact from the Director is at risk of drifting.
4. **Red relationships get Director attention that week**: Do not wait for the monthly cycle. A Red relationship means something has broken — the longer it goes unaddressed, the harder it is to repair.
5. **New stakeholders**: Add them as identified. The map is never finished — the business adds people.

## Relationship Health Guide

Use this guide consistently across the team so "Green" means the same thing to everyone.

### Green — Healthy, Proactive Relationship

All of the following are true:
- Director initiates contact proactively — does not wait for the stakeholder to reach out with a problem
- No surprises in either direction — stakeholder gets advance notice of things that will affect them
- Both parties have a shared understanding of what each needs from the other (see "What They Need / What Director Needs" columns)
- When tension arises (and it will), it is addressed directly and resolved
- Trust is present: the stakeholder is confident that the Director will escalate appropriately and follow through on commitments

**Frequency signal**: Director has had meaningful contact with this stakeholder in the past 30 days. For VP/CTO-level stakeholders: contact in the past 7 days.

---

### Amber — Reactive or Strained Relationship

One or more of the following is true:
- Communication is reactive — the Director is responding to the stakeholder's requests rather than proactively reaching out
- A surprise has occurred (the stakeholder learned about something important from someone other than the Director)
- There is some tension or unresolved disagreement that has not been addressed directly
- The stakeholder has expressed concern or frustration, even informally
- Contact has lapsed for 30–60 days without a clear reason

**Director action for Amber**: Schedule a 1:1 within 5 business days. Come prepared with something concrete — an update, a decision, a request for their input. Do not wait for the next scheduled cycle.

---

### Red — Broken or At-Risk Relationship

One or more of the following is true:
- There has been a significant surprise — the stakeholder learned about something material from someone other than the Director and is dissatisfied
- Communication has broken down — the stakeholder is not responding, or the Director has been avoiding reaching out
- Active distrust: the stakeholder has escalated concerns about the Director to a third party (VP, HR, peer Director)
- A commitment was made and not kept, and it has not been addressed
- Contact has lapsed > 60 days with no explanation

**Director action for Red**: Address this week — not this month. Schedule a meeting within 48 hours. Acknowledge the breakdown directly; do not pretend it has not happened. Bring a concrete plan for how you will operate differently. Loop in your VP if the relationship is with a peer VP or above — you should not manage a Red relationship with a senior stakeholder without your VP knowing.

---

## Quarterly Review Checklist

Run this at the start of each quarter:

- [ ] All rows have been updated with a "Last Interaction Date" within the past 30 days (for active relationships)
- [ ] No Amber relationships have been Amber for > 60 days without an active recovery plan
- [ ] No Red relationships exist without VP awareness
- [ ] At least one new stakeholder added if the business has added relevant roles
- [ ] "What Director Needs From Them" column reflects any changes in priorities or dependencies
- [ ] Vendor relationships reviewed against contract renewal dates — no renewal should surprise the Director
