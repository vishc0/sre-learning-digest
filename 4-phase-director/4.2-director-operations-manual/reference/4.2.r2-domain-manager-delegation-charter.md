# Domain Manager Delegation Charter

**Purpose**: This document defines, in writing, what each Domain Manager owns independently, what they decide and notify the Director, and what requires Director or VP approval. It eliminates the ambiguity that causes both over-escalation (managers asking permission for things they own) and under-escalation (managers making decisions they should not make alone).

**When to issue**: Within 60 days of the Director's start date. Both parties must sign. Verbal understandings do not count.

**Review schedule**: 90-day review after signing (first check: is the delegation working?). Annual review thereafter.

---

## How to Use This Charter

Read each table row as a decision example, not an exhaustive list. If a decision is not listed:
- If it clearly falls within the manager's domain and does not set a cross-team precedent or involve unbudgeted spend, the manager owns it.
- If uncertain: manager brings it to the Director's next 1:1 and gets a ruling, which may result in the charter being updated.

The "Inform Director?" column means: send a Slack message or email within 24 hours of the decision. Not a formal approval request — just notification so the Director is not caught off guard.

## Role 1: Incident & Problem Management Manager

**Primary Domain Ownership**: End-to-end lifecycle of all production incidents (P1 through P4) and all problem management records. Owns the on-call schedule, incident process playbooks, post-incident review process, and the problem backlog. The Director does not run incident bridges — this manager does.

| Decision Category | Their Authority | Inform Director? | Director Approval Required? | VP Approval Required? |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Incident Severity Declaration** | Full authority to declare P1/P2/P3/P4 on any incident. No Director approval needed at any severity. | Yes — notify Director of any P1 within 15 minutes of declaration via direct message (text if urgent). | No | No |
| **Incident Bridge Membership** | Full authority to pull any engineer, including engineers from other teams, into a P1 bridge. | No — this is SWARM protocol. Notify domain leads afterward. | No | No |
| **Customer Communication During Incidents** | Authorized to send status page updates for P1/P2 incidents using approved templates. | Yes — notify Director when customer-facing communication is sent. | Director approval required before any direct communication to an Enterprise customer (named account) outside the status page. | VP approval required for any communication that acknowledges an SLA breach before the breach is confirmed by Director and VP. |
| **Incident Closure** | Full authority to close any incident at any severity. | Yes for P1 — notify Director when P1 is closed and confirmed resolved. | No | No |
| **Post-Incident Review (PIR) Process** | Full authority to schedule, run, and publish PIR findings for all incidents. | Yes — share PIR summary with Director within 48 hours of publication. | No | No |
| **Problem Ticket Creation** | Full authority to open problem tickets at any priority. | Yes — notify Director of new P1 problem tickets within 24 hours. | No | No |
| **Problem Priority and Assignment** | Full authority to set priority and assign problem tickets to any team within Operations. | Yes for cross-team assignment — notify the affected domain manager and Director simultaneously. | Director approval required to assign a problem to a team outside Operations (e.g., Engineering, Product). | No |
| **Problem Backlog Prioritization** | Full authority to re-prioritize within the problem backlog. | Yes — any change to the top 3 P1 problems requires Director notification within 24 hours. | Director approval required if a P1 problem is deprioritized below active remediation (i.e., acceptance of extended resolution timeline > 30 days). | No |
| **On-Call Schedule Management** | Full authority to manage on-call rotation within the Incident & Problem team. Temporary coverage adjustments (< 2 weeks) within the team: full authority. | Yes — notify Director if any engineer's on-call load exceeds 8 pages/engineer/week for a 7-day period. | Director approval required for any on-call coverage arrangement that borrows engineers from another domain team for > 2 weeks. | No |
| **Process and Playbook Changes** | Full authority to update playbooks, runbooks, and process documentation within the Incident & Problem domain. | Yes — notify Director of material process changes (new escalation paths, changed severity definitions, new communication templates) before they take effect. | Director approval required for any change to P1 incident severity criteria or to the escalation matrix that affects other domain teams. | No |
| **Tooling Configuration — Incident Tooling** | Full authority to configure PagerDuty (routing rules, escalation policies, on-call schedules) and ServiceNow (incident workflows within the Operations group). | Yes for changes to Tier 0 routing rules — notify Director before implementing. | Director approval required for any change to alerting thresholds on Tier 0 services that could reduce alert sensitivity. VP approval not required. | No |
| **Vendor Engagement — Incident Tooling** | Full authority to engage PagerDuty and ServiceNow support contacts for technical issues. | No | Director approval required for any commitment > $5,000 outside the existing contract (e.g., professional services, premium support tier upgrades). | VP approval required for contract renewals or renegotiations > $50,000. |
| **Team Staffing Decisions** | Full authority to manage task assignments, project assignments, and skill development within their team. | Yes — notify Director of any staffing change that reduces on-call coverage capacity. | Director approval required for any team member placed on a formal performance improvement plan (PIP) or removed from on-call rotation for performance reasons. | VP approval required for any involuntary separation. |
| **Budget Spending — Discretionary** | Full authority to approve purchases within the approved operations budget up to $2,500 per purchase, per quarter, within their domain's discretionary budget. | Yes for any purchase > $1,000 — notify Director with a one-line description. | Director approval required for any purchase > $2,500 or any purchase not in the approved budget. | VP approval required for unbudgeted spend > $25,000. |

## Role 2: Change & Release Management Manager

**Primary Domain Ownership**: End-to-end change management lifecycle — change intake, CAB facilitation, standard change catalog, emergency change process, change failure tracking, and post-implementation reviews for failed changes. Also owns the deployment freeze calendar and the deployment pipeline quality gates (in partnership with Engineering).

| Decision Category | Their Authority | Inform Director? | Director Approval Required? | VP Approval Required? |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Standard Change Approval** | Full authority to approve any standard change on the pre-approved catalog without CAB. No Director review needed. | No | No | No |
| **Normal Change — CAB Facilitation** | Full authority to run the CAB, facilitate the review, and approve or reject normal changes based on CAB consensus. | No | No | No |
| **Normal Change — CAB Override** | No independent authority to override a CAB rejection without additional review. | N/A | Director approval required to override a CAB rejection. | No |
| **Emergency Change Authorization** | Full authority to authorize an emergency change in conjunction with the on-call Duty Manager when a P1 incident requires it, outside of standard CAB hours. | Yes — notify Director within 2 hours of any emergency change authorization (text/Slack). | No (emergency change authority is pre-delegated for P1 incident response). | No |
| **Change Freeze — Declaration and Lift** | Full authority to declare and enforce standard change freezes per the published freeze calendar (major holidays, peak traffic periods already defined). | Yes — notify Director before implementing any unplanned freeze (i.e., not on the published calendar). | Director approval required to declare an unplanned change freeze that affects more than one domain team. | VP approval required for any change freeze that affects Engineering or Product release schedules. |
| **Standard Change Catalog — Updates** | Full authority to add, modify, or remove standard change types from the catalog. | Yes — notify Director of any material change to the catalog (new pre-approved types, removal of existing types) before publication. | Director approval required for any change to the catalog that removes a previously approved change type that is in active use by Engineering. | No |
| **Deployment Pipeline Quality Gates** | Full authority to set and enforce quality gate thresholds (test pass rate, performance gate criteria, security scan gate criteria) within the Operations-owned pipeline. | Yes — notify Director before changing any gate threshold for Tier 0 or Tier 1 services. | Director approval required to lower any gate threshold for Tier 0 services (raising thresholds requires only notification). | No |
| **Post-Implementation Review (PIR) for Failed Changes** | Full authority to conduct PIRs for failed changes and publish findings. | Yes — share PIR summary with Director within 48 hours for any failed change that caused a P1 or P2 incident. | No | No |
| **Change Failure Rate Response** | If weekly CFR > 5%: full authority to initiate a mandatory change review and impose a 24-hour cooling-off period for normal changes from the team with the highest failure rate. | Yes — notify Director before implementing the cooling-off period. | Director approval required if the cooling-off period would delay a named customer commitment or regulatory deadline. | No |
| **Vendor Engagement — Release Tooling** | Full authority to engage vendor support for tooling issues (deployment pipeline, JIRA, CI/CD tooling). | No | Director approval required for any commitment > $5,000 outside the existing contract. | VP approval required for contract renewals or renegotiations > $50,000. |
| **Process and Playbook Changes** | Full authority to update change management policies, CAB meeting cadence, and process documentation. | Yes — notify Director of material policy changes before they take effect. | Director approval required for any change to emergency change criteria or to the definition of what constitutes a standard vs. normal vs. emergency change. | No |
| **Cross-Team Change Coordination** | Full authority to coordinate change windows and dependencies with Engineering, Product, and other Operations domain teams. | No — coordination is their job. | Director approval required if a coordination dispute cannot be resolved (e.g., two teams claim conflicting change windows on the same service). | No |
| **Release Communication** | Full authority to publish change notifications on internal channels per the communications plan. | Yes — notify Director if any customer-facing maintenance notification is sent. | Director approval required for any customer-facing communication about a planned maintenance window that may exceed 30 minutes. | VP approval required for any planned maintenance window that will breach a contractual notification requirement. |
| **Budget Spending — Discretionary** | Full authority up to $2,500 per purchase within approved budget. | Yes for > $1,000. | Director approval for > $2,500 or unbudgeted. | VP approval for unbudgeted > $25,000. |

## Role 3: Platform Reliability Manager

**Primary Domain Ownership**: Service Level Management (SLO/SLA tracking and reporting), availability monitoring, capacity planning, environment management, performance engineering (gates and production performance monitoring), and infrastructure reliability for all Tier 0/1/2/3 services. This role has the broadest technical scope in the Operations org.

| Decision Category | Their Authority | Inform Director? | Director Approval Required? | VP Approval Required? |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **SLO Target Setting** | Full authority to propose SLO targets for internal review. Cannot finalize SLO targets independently — these require Director sign-off because they have contractual implications. | N/A | Director approval required for all new or modified SLO targets before they are communicated externally or embedded in customer contracts. | VP approval required for any SLO target tied to an enterprise customer SLA. |
| **SLA Breach Declaration** | Full authority to declare an SLA breach has occurred based on availability data. Breach notification process is then Director-owned. | Yes — notify Director immediately (call/text) upon confirming any Tier 0/1 SLA breach. | Director owns all SLA breach communications — Platform Reliability Manager does not communicate breaches to customers independently. | VP must be notified within 1 hour of any confirmed SLA breach. Director is responsible for VP notification. |
| **Error Budget Decisions** | Full authority to recommend burn or freeze based on error budget consumption. Implementation of a freeze requires Director approval. | Yes — notify Director when any Tier 0/1 service has consumed > 50% of its monthly error budget by the 15th of the month. | Director approval required to implement an error budget freeze (halting non-critical changes to a service). | No |
| **Capacity Planning** | Full authority to plan, model, and recommend capacity changes. Full authority to execute approved capacity plans. | Yes — notify Director of any capacity plan that involves a cost increase > $10,000/month. | Director approval required for any capacity investment > $50,000 (one-time or recurring) not in the approved budget. | VP approval required for unbudgeted capacity spend > $100,000. |
| **Environment Management** | Full authority to provision, decommission, and reconfigure non-production environments. | Yes — notify Director before decommissioning any environment that has been in active use for > 6 months (institutional knowledge risk). | Director approval required to decommission any production or DR environment. | VP approval required for any DR environment change that affects the disaster recovery posture. |
| **Performance Gate Configuration** | Full authority to configure performance gates and set thresholds for all services. | Yes — notify Director before lowering any performance gate threshold for Tier 0/1 services. | Director approval required to disable or bypass a performance gate for Tier 0/1 services, even temporarily. | No |
| **Production Performance Incident Response** | Full authority to declare a performance incident, initiate investigation, and implement remediation within the platform (infrastructure-level changes). | Yes — notify Director of any production performance incident affecting Tier 0/1 services within 30 minutes of detection. | Director approval required for any platform-level change (configuration, scaling, circuit breaker activation) that could cause a brief degradation during the fix. | No |
| **Monitoring and Alerting Configuration** | Full authority to configure, tune, and update monitoring and alerting across all Datadog dashboards, Splunk alerts, and synthetic monitors. | Yes — notify Director before changing any Tier 0 alerting threshold that could reduce detection sensitivity. | Director approval required to remove any alerting coverage on Tier 0 services, even temporarily. | No |
| **Vendor Engagement — Infrastructure** | Full authority to engage cloud providers (AWS, Azure), CDN vendors, and database vendors for technical support. | No | Director approval required for any commitment or contract modification > $10,000. | VP approval required for contract renewals or renegotiations > $100,000 or any multi-year commitment. |
| **Tool Procurement** | Full authority to evaluate and pilot new observability/reliability tools. No authority to purchase without Director approval. | Yes — notify Director before beginning any formal vendor evaluation that will require engineering time > 40 hours. | Director approval required for any tool purchase or new SaaS subscription. | VP approval required for any tooling commitment > $50,000/year. |
| **Cross-Domain Technical Decisions** | Full authority to make technical reliability decisions within the platform infrastructure layer. | Yes — notify Director and Engineering VP for any infrastructure change that will affect the developer experience (e.g., deployment time, environment availability, CI/CD pipeline behavior). | Director approval required for any cross-cutting architectural change that affects how Engineering teams deploy to production. | VP approval required if the change affects a customer-facing infrastructure component in a way that could degrade the customer experience. |
| **On-Call Load Management — Platform Team** | Full authority to manage on-call rotation within the Platform Reliability team, including temporary coverage arrangements within the team. | Yes — notify Director when any platform team engineer exceeds 8 pages/week for any 7-day period. | Director approval required for shared on-call arrangements that borrow engineers from other domain teams for > 2 weeks. | No |
| **Budget Spending — Discretionary** | Full authority up to $2,500 per purchase within approved budget. | Yes for > $1,000. | Director approval for > $2,500 or unbudgeted. | VP approval for unbudgeted > $25,000. |

## Role 4: Architecture & Transformation Lead

**Primary Domain Ownership**: Technology architecture standards and compliance, architectural review board (ARB) facilitation, transformation program delivery (automation, toil reduction, modernization), technical debt roadmap, and cross-team architectural governance. This role has a longer time horizon than the other three — most of their work operates at the quarter-to-year level, not the week-to-sprint level.

| Decision Category | Their Authority | Inform Director? | Director Approval Required? | VP Approval Required? |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|
| **Architecture Standards — Creation** | Full authority to draft new architecture standards and bring them to the ARB for adoption. | Yes — notify Director when a new standard is being drafted that will affect Engineering teams outside Operations. | Director approval required before any new standard is formally adopted and published. | VP approval required for any standard that will affect Engineering team's technology choices (language, framework, cloud service selection). |
| **Architecture Review Board (ARB) — Facilitation** | Full authority to facilitate the ARB, schedule reviews, and document findings. | Yes — share ARB meeting summary with Director within 48 hours of each ARB session. | No | No |
| **Architecture Review — Approval/Rejection** | Full authority to issue ARB findings (approve, conditional approve, or reject) for any architecture review. | Yes — notify Director of any ARB rejection, as rejections create Engineering team friction that may reach the Director. | Director approval required before issuing an ARB rejection for a proposal that has VP-level sponsorship. | No |
| **Architecture Compliance — Audit** | Full authority to audit existing services against current standards and publish compliance status. | Yes — share quarterly compliance report with Director before publishing externally. | Director approval required before the compliance report is shared with Engineering leadership or VP-level stakeholders. | No |
| **Technical Debt Roadmap** | Full authority to maintain and prioritize the technical debt backlog within the Operations domain. | Yes — notify Director when a new item is added to the top 10 by risk or severity. | Director approval required to add a technical debt item to the quarterly roadmap that will require Engineering team capacity (cross-domain impact). | VP approval required for any technical debt remediation initiative with a budget > $100,000 or that will take > 1 quarter of a named Engineering team's capacity. |
| **Transformation Program Delivery** | Full authority to manage transformation initiatives within Operations (automation projects, toil reduction, tool consolidation). Full delivery authority within approved scope and budget. | Yes — weekly status update to Director on any transformation initiative > 4 weeks in duration. | Director approval required for any scope change that adds > 20% to the original estimate or that changes the delivery date by > 4 weeks. | VP approval required for any transformation initiative with a budget > $200,000. |
| **Automation — Operations Internal** | Full authority to design, build, and deploy automation within the Operations domain (runbook automation, self-healing scripts, monitoring automation). | Yes — notify Director before deploying any automation that auto-remediates production incidents without human confirmation. | Director approval required for any fully automated remediation that can modify production infrastructure without a human in the loop. | No |
| **Vendor Engagement — Architecture and Tooling Evaluation** | Full authority to engage vendors for architecture and tooling evaluations, including PoC (proof of concept) engagements. | Yes — notify Director when a PoC engagement begins that will require > 40 engineering hours or any vendor access to non-production environments. | Director approval required for any PoC that requires vendor access to production data or systems. | VP approval required for any PoC with an associated vendor cost > $25,000. |
| **Cross-Domain Technology Decisions** | Full authority to provide architectural guidance and recommendations across all four domain teams. Recommendation authority only — implementation authority belongs to the respective domain manager. | Yes — notify Director when a cross-domain architectural recommendation is made that will require re-work from another team. | Director approval required when an architectural recommendation from this role conflicts with a delivery commitment made to Engineering leadership or the VP. | No |
| **Standards Exception Handling** | Full authority to grant time-limited (< 90 days) architecture standards exceptions with documented rationale. | Yes — notify Director of any exception granted within 24 hours. | Director approval required for any exception > 90 days or any exception to a security-related standard. | VP approval required for exceptions to standards that have regulatory compliance implications. |
| **Team and Resource Allocation — Transformation Projects** | Full authority to allocate Operations engineers to transformation projects within approved capacity. | Yes — notify Director if transformation project work requires > 20% of any domain team's sprint capacity (cross-domain borrowing). | Director approval required to redirect any domain team engineer to transformation work for > 2 consecutive sprints if it reduces that team's on-call or incident response capacity. | No |
| **Budget Spending — Discretionary** | Full authority up to $2,500 per purchase within approved budget. | Yes for > $1,000. | Director approval for > $2,500 or unbudgeted. | VP approval for unbudgeted > $25,000. |

## Signature Block

This charter reflects a shared understanding of decision authority between the Director and each Domain Manager. It is not a restriction on either party's judgment — it is a documented agreement on where judgment calls live. When a situation arises that is not covered here, the Domain Manager brings it to the Director's next 1:1. If it needs to be covered going forward, the charter is updated.

**Review schedule**: 90 days after signing (first check). Annually thereafter, or when the Director's role, the domain manager's role, or the organizational structure changes materially.
