# Director's Operations Manual

## What This Is

This is your operating system for the Director of Operations role. Not a theory document. Not a leadership framework. A working manual that tells you what to do on Monday morning, what to review at month end, which decisions you own, and how to know if your org is healthy or quietly failing.

The domain knowledge — incident management, problem management, change governance, SLM, availability, architecture — you already have. This manual is the **meta-layer**: how a Director ties all of those domains together into a functioning organization that works when you are not in the room.

That phrase — *when you are not in the room* — is the core test of a Director. If your team makes good decisions without you present, you are doing the job. If they wait for you, call you, or escalate everything, you have not yet done the job. This manual is the tool you use to close that gap.

## How to Use This Manual

### First week in role

Read in this order:
1. [ch01 — The Director's Job](ch01-the-directors-job.md) — understand the role shift before you do anything else
2. [ch07 — The 90-Day Director Plan](ch07-the-90-day-director-plan.md) — this is your onboarding playbook; start executing it on day one
3. [ch03 — Decision Rights and Delegation](ch03-decision-rights-and-delegation.md) — you will be asked to make decisions before you are ready; know your framework

4. [ch04 — Your Domain Managers](ch04-your-domain-managers.md) — as you meet your directs, use this to calibrate what good looks like
5. [ch02 — The Operating Rhythm](ch02-the-operating-rhythm.md) — establish your weekly cadence by the end of week two
6. [ch06 — Stakeholder Management](ch06-stakeholder-management.md) — identify your key stakeholders and have first conversations

7. [ch05 — Metrics the Director Tracks](ch05-metrics-the-director-tracks.md) — use the reference card in `reference/` for your daily/weekly checks
8. [ch08 — Building and Sustaining the Team](ch08-building-and-sustaining-the-team.md) — review quarterly during people planning

### When running meetings

- Weekly ops review → [weekly-ops-review-playbook.md](playbook/weekly-ops-review-playbook.md)

## The Single Principle This Manual Is Built On

> **A Director's job is to make the org work when they are not in the room.**

Every rhythm, ritual, delegation, and metric in this manual exists to test and improve that condition. If you find yourself indispensable to daily operations, you have not yet finished building the org.

## Key Definitions Used Throughout

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Domain manager** | Direct report who owns a specific operational domain (Incident, Problem, Change, SLM, Availability, Environment, Performance, Architecture) |
| **Operating rhythm** | The fixed cadence of meetings, reviews, and reports that create organizational predictability |
| **Decision right** | Explicit authority to make a category of decision without seeking approval |
| **Delegation charter** | Written agreement between Director and domain manager defining decision authority |
| **Director decision log** | Persistent record of significant decisions made, options considered, rationale, and outcome |
| **Weekly ops review** | Monday meeting with all domain managers — the primary mechanism for org-wide visibility |
| **QBR** | Quarterly Business Review — the Director's 30-minute slot with the executive team |
| **Toil** | Repetitive, manual, automatable operational work that provides no lasting value |
| **On-call load** | Pages per engineer per week; the leading indicator of team health and system reliability |
