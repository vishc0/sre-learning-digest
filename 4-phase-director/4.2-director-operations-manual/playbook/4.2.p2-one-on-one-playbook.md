# One-on-One Playbook

The Director's weekly 1:1 with each domain manager. 30 minutes. Not a status report — status comes in the weekly ops review. This is a development and unblocking conversation.

**Meeting spec:**
- When: Tuesday and Wednesday (spread across both days, 30 min each)
- Who: Director + one domain manager
- Format: Director listens 70%; domain manager talks 30%
- Cadence: Weekly, never cancel without rescheduling same week

**The three standing Director questions (ask in every 1:1):**
1. "What's blocking you that only I can remove?"
2. "What decision are you waiting on — from me or from anyone?"
3. "What are you worried about that you haven't told me in the weekly ops review?"

**The one development question (rotate monthly):**
Month A: "What's one thing you want to get better at in the next quarter?"
Month B: "What's one thing I'm doing that makes your job harder?"
Month C: "If you could change one process in your domain, what would it be?"

**What this meeting is NOT:**
- Not a status update (that's the weekly ops review)
- Not a performance review (that's the quarterly career conversation)
- Not a problem-solving session (that's as-needed)
- Not a meeting where the Director talks for 25 minutes and asks "any questions?"

**Director's notes protocol:**
Take notes during the 1:1. Within 2 hours, send a 3-line follow-up: (1) what you heard, (2) what you committed to, (3) what you need back from them. This is your accountability mechanism.

**Cadence variations:**
- New domain manager in first 90 days → 45 minutes, twice weekly
- Domain in crisis → 45 minutes, daily until stabilized
- High-performing domain, established manager → 30 minutes weekly is appropriate; do not reduce below weekly

**What to do when the domain manager says "everything is fine":**
That is a failure signal, not a success signal. In a healthy 1:1, the domain manager should always have at least one thing they need. Ask: "If you had a magic wand and could change one thing about how this org runs, what would it be?" That usually opens the real conversation.
