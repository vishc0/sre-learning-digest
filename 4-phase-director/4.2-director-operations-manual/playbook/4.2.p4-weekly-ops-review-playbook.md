# Weekly Ops Review Playbook

How to run the Monday weekly ops review with domain managers. This is the primary mechanism for Director-level visibility into the entire operational estate.

**Meeting spec:**
- When: Every Monday, 9:00–9:50 AM (50 minutes hard stop)
- Who: Director + all domain managers (Incident, Problem, Change, SLM, Availability, Environment, Performance, Architecture)
- Format: Async pre-read 30 minutes before; synchronous discussion only
- Owner: Director facilitates; domain managers present their segment

**Pre-read requirement (due Sunday night or Monday 8:30 AM):**
Each domain manager sends a 3-bullet status card:
1. What happened this week (facts only — no editorializing)
2. What's at risk this week (changes, known weaknesses, capacity gaps)
3. What I need from the Director (decisions, escalations, air cover)

**Agenda: 50 minutes**

| Segment | Time | Owner | Format |
|---------|------|-------|--------|
| P1/P2 Incident Review | 0–10 min | Incident Manager | 3 columns: incident, MTTR, problem record created? |
| Open P1 Problem Review | 10–20 min | Problem Manager | 3 columns: problem, days open, fix delivery risk |
| Change Calendar Review | 20–30 min | Change Manager | High-risk changes this week + freeze adherence |
| Environment + Release Status | 30–40 min | Env + SLM | Environment incidents, release readiness |
| Risks + Director Decisions | 40–50 min | All | What escalation does Director need to make? |

**Director's facilitation rules:**
- Start with the pre-read status cards open — do not re-read them aloud
- Ask "what's the risk?" not "what happened?" — the pre-read handled history
- When a domain manager surfaces a decision: make it in the room or set a time to decide by end of day
- Hard stop at 50 minutes — items not reached → Director's async decision queue
- Never go around the table asking for status — use the pre-read and use meeting time for risk and decisions only

**Output of every weekly ops review:**
1. Decision log entries (use the Director Decision Log template)
2. Escalation actions (who does what by when)
3. 3-bullet VP status summary (Director writes this in 10 minutes after the meeting ends)

**What to do when a domain manager has no pre-read at start time:**
Give them 2 minutes to write 3 bullets in the shared doc. Do not skip them. Do not reschedule. After the meeting, have a direct conversation about why the pre-read wasn't ready — it's a predictability problem.

**When to cancel the weekly ops review:**
Never. If the Director is traveling, run it async: domain managers post their 3-bullet cards; Director responds in writing by noon Monday with decisions and flags. A 50-minute meeting that runs every week without fail is worth more than a well-run meeting that sometimes doesn't happen.
