# Quarterly Business Review Playbook

How to prepare and present the quarterly operational scorecard to the executive team. You have 30 minutes. You will not get more.

**Preparation timeline:**
| Week Before QBR | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| Week -3 | Pull three months of metric data; identify trends |
| Week -2 | Domain managers submit quarterly summaries |
| Week -1 | Director builds slides; reviews with a peer Director for a sanity check |
| Day before | Dry run the presentation out loud — 30 minutes exactly |

**Slide structure (4 slides):**

**Slide 1: Operational Health Summary**
Green/amber/red by domain. One-line status per domain. One headline number: overall availability vs. target.
Time: 3 minutes. This tells the room whether to relax or lean forward.

**Slide 2: What Went Well (Top 3)**
Three operational improvements this quarter with measurable outcomes.
Format: "[Thing we did] → [measurable result]."
Example: "Reduced change failure rate from 12% to 6% → $1.8M in avoided SLA credits."
Time: 7 minutes. Do not list everything. Three things you are proud of and can defend.

**Slide 3: Top 2 Risks and Mitigation Plans**
Two risks you are actively managing. For each: what is the risk, what's the likelihood, what are you doing, what do you need from the exec team.
Time: 10 minutes. This is where the executive conversation happens. Expect questions.

**Slide 4: What You Need (Decisions and Resources)**
Two to three specific asks. Each ask has a business case (one sentence) and a deadline.
Example: "I need approval for two additional SRE headcount by March 1 to support the Q2 platform migration. Without this, we carry 40% key person risk on the migration."
Time: 10 minutes. This is the most important part. Do not spend 25 minutes on history and leave 5 minutes for asks.

**What NOT to do in the QBR:**
- Do not spend the first 20 minutes narrating what happened last quarter — they can read the monthly report
- Do not present every metric on your dashboard — pick the 5 that matter most
- Do not hedge your risks with "we're monitoring the situation" — tell them what you're doing and what you need
- Do not leave without a clear yes/no on your asks — if the room can't decide, set a follow-up date before you leave

**The one question every QBR should answer:**
"Is Operations a competitive advantage or a liability right now?" Your presentation should answer that question, implicitly or explicitly.
