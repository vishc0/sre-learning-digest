# Monthly Reporting Playbook

How to prepare and deliver the monthly ops report to the VP. This report is the Director's primary accountability artifact — it demonstrates that the org is being managed, not just reacted to.

**Timeline:**
| Day of Month | Action | Owner |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Day 1 | Domain managers notified: metrics due by Day 3 | Director |
| Day 3 | Domain managers submit metric tables + notable events | Domain managers |
| Day 4 | Director reviews all submissions; flags gaps | Director |
| Day 5 | Director writes executive summary (3 sentences) + narrative | Director |
| Day 5 EOD | Full report submitted to VP | Director |
| Day 7 | 15-minute verbal walkthrough with VP | Director + VP |

**What the VP actually reads:**
The executive summary at the top — 3 sentences. That is what they read first, and if it's well written, it sets the frame for everything that follows. Write it last.

**Executive summary formula:**
Sentence 1: Overall operational health (green/amber/red) + one-line rationale.
Sentence 2: The most significant operational event this month and what you did about it.
Sentence 3: The one risk you are actively managing and what you need from the VP (if anything).

**What the VP drills into:**
Any metric that is red or amber. Any trend that is worsening month over month. Any "notable event" that they haven't heard about before (this should never happen — no surprises protocol applies here too).

**Report sections:**
1. Executive Summary (3 sentences — Director writes this last)
2. Operational Health Summary (table: domain, status RAG, one-line summary)
3. Section per domain (metric table, trend indicator, notable events, risks next month)
4. Team Health (on-call load, open headcount, PTO coverage, attrition flags)
5. Appendix: full metric data tables

**The 15-minute VP walkthrough format:**
- Minutes 0–3: "Here's the headline — overall health is X, here's why"
- Minutes 3–8: "The two things I want you to pay attention to are..." (the amber/red items)
- Minutes 8–12: "Here's what I'm doing about [risk]"
- Minutes 12–15: "I need one thing from you this month: [specific decision or air cover]"

**What to do if a metric is late from a domain manager:**
Submit the report with a placeholder for that domain and note "data submitted late — will send addendum by [date]." Do not delay the VP report to wait for one domain manager. Address the lateness separately.
