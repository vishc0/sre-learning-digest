# Director Decision Log

**Purpose**: Permanent record of significant Director-level decisions. Append-only — never delete or edit a past entry. Review quarterly to assess outcomes and calibrate future decision-making.

**File location**: Keep this file in a location accessible to your Chief of Staff and your manager. Share with your VP if asked. Do not treat this as confidential — if you can't write it here, you shouldn't be making the decision unilaterally.

---

## What Qualifies as a Significant Decision

Log a decision if it meets **any** of these criteria:

- Affects more than one team's work or priorities
- Involves budget authority (approving unbudgeted spend, reallocating budget, approving contractor extensions)
- Sets a precedent — if someone on your team says "but you did this for team X," the precedent matters
- Will be questioned later — any decision that a VP, peer Director, or auditor might ask you to justify in 6 months
- Changes a policy, process, or standard that the org operates by
- Results in someone being moved, promoted, performance-managed, or removed from a rotation

**Do not log**: routine approvals within established policy, standard change approvals, weekly ops decisions that reverse themselves within a sprint.

## Example Entry 1 — Incident Process Change

---

**Decision ID**: D-2026-001
**Date**: 2026-04-08
**Title**: Adopt severity-based SWARM protocol for P1 incidents, replacing the existing tiered escalation matrix

### Context
In Q1 2026, three P1 incidents exceeded their SLA window due to delayed escalation. Root cause analysis on all three showed that the tiered escalation matrix (which required Duty Manager sign-off before pulling in senior engineers) added 10–18 minutes to bridge formation time. The current SLA target is 30-minute MTTR. The escalation overhead was consuming one-third of the MTTR budget before an engineer had touched the incident.

### Options Considered

| Option | Description | Key Trade-Off |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| A — SWARM Protocol | On P1 declaration, all domain leads automatically join bridge within 5 minutes. No escalation approval required. | Faster response. Higher noise for non-critical P1s. Senior engineers in more bridges. |
| B — Automated Escalation | Tooling (PagerDuty) auto-escalates without human approval after 5-minute timeout. | Reduces delay without changing culture. Requires tooling config. Does not change the root behavior. |
| C — Status Quo with Coaching | Retrain Duty Managers to approve faster. | Lowest disruption. Low confidence it works — behavior was already the expectation. |

### Decision Made
Director approved Option A — SWARM protocol — effective April 15, 2026, for all P1 incidents. Duty Manager role retained for bridge coordination, not for escalation gate authority.

### Rationale
Options B and C treat symptoms rather than root cause. The tiered escalation matrix assumed scarcity of senior engineering attention; the post-incident reviews showed the real problem was decision latency, not engineer availability. SWARM removes the decision node. The trade-off (senior engineers in more bridges) is acceptable — P1 frequency is 3–4 per month, and bridge participation rarely exceeds 30 minutes.

### Stakeholders Informed
| Stakeholder | Role | How Informed | Date |
|------------|------|-------------|------|
| [VP Name] | VP Operations | 1:1 pre-decision alignment | 2026-04-06 |
| All domain managers | Direct reports | Team standup + written decision memo | 2026-04-08 |
| Engineering VP peers | Peer Directors | Email with rationale | 2026-04-09 |
| On-call rotation leads | Team leads | Dedicated 30-min training session | 2026-04-14 |

### Outcome
**Outcome Status**: [x] Correct
**Date Reviewed**: 2026-07-01
**Actual Outcome**: MTTR Tier 1 improved from 28 minutes (Q1 average) to 21 minutes (Q2 average) — a 25% reduction. No SLA breaches in Q2 attributable to delayed escalation. Senior engineer feedback on SWARM was positive; no complaints about bridge noise volume. One edge case identified: P1s declared late at night had incomplete SWARM because not all domain leads had PagerDuty mobile app configured. Corrected in May.

### Review Date
2026-07-01 (90 days post-implementation)

## Example Entry 2 — Headcount Decision

---

**Decision ID**: D-2026-007
**Date**: 2026-05-28
**Title**: Submit FTE conversion request for Platform Reliability contractor to VP for approval; accept 60-day risk window if delayed

### Context
Contractor engineer on the Platform Reliability team (contract role, 11 months tenure) holds the primary operational knowledge for the legacy ingestion pipeline, which processes 40% of production event volume. The contractor's current contract expires August 15, 2026. An informal conversation on May 22 indicated they have received an outside offer and will not accept another short-term extension. Conversion to FTE requires VP approval due to headcount freeze on new FTE additions (budget cycle constraint). Director has authority to extend contractors but not to convert.

### Options Considered

| Option | Description | Key Trade-Off |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| A — Submit FTE conversion request | Request VP approval for Grade 7 FTE conversion. ~$38K annual cost delta (benefits-loaded). | Best outcome if approved. 60-day approval risk window — contractor may accept outside offer before decision comes through. |
| B — 60-day contractor extension | Extend at current rate while recruiting for permanent backfill. | Contractor may decline extension. Delays the inevitable and adds cost without securing retention. |
| C — Begin external backfill immediately | Start FTE requisition through normal pipeline. 90-day hire + 90-day ramp = 180-day risk window. | Preserves headcount freeze compliance. Extremely high operational risk on ingestion pipeline during gap. |

### Decision Made
Director submitted FTE conversion request (HR-2026-447) to VP on May 28, 2026. Simultaneously opened contingency backfill requisition (REQ-2026-118) so that recruitment does not wait on conversion approval. Contractor informed of Director's intent; not given a formal commitment pending VP approval.

### Rationale
Option C is operationally unacceptable — a 180-day gap on a 40%-of-production pipeline would require Director to either accept unacceptable availability risk or pull another engineer from a different team with its own on-call obligations. Option B buys time but does not solve the retention problem and adds $18K in incremental cost without certainty. Option A carries approval timing risk, but the contingency backfill requisition hedges that risk. Decision to open contingency in parallel was made within Director's authority.
