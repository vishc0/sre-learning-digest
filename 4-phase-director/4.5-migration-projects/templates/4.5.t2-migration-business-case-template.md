## Risk-of-Inaction Analysis

The true cost of not migrating. This section is frequently omitted in weak business cases and is the strongest lever in the CFO conversation — it reframes "spending money" as "avoiding larger future costs."

| Risk | Probability (3 yr) | Financial Impact | Risk-Adjusted Cost |
|------|------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Vendor platform EOL — forced migration at premium cost | | $___ | $___ |
| Security incident due to unpatched/unsupported platform | | $___ | $___ |
| Licensing audit exposure (true-up, audit penalty) | | $___ | $___ |
| Regulatory / compliance finding (fine, remediation cost) | | $___ | $___ |
| Competitive disadvantage — delayed feature delivery | | $___ | $___ |
| **Total risk-adjusted cost of inaction over 3 years** | | | **$___** |

**How to read this table**: Probability × Financial Impact = Risk-Adjusted Cost. A 30% chance of a $2M penalty is a $600K risk-adjusted cost. This should be compared directly against the migration investment.

## Strategic Benefits (Non-Quantified)

These benefits are real but not modeled in the financial summary because they depend on future decisions. List them; quantify if evidence supports it; do not manufacture precision.

- [Capability unlocked 1 — e.g., ability to deploy on a 2-week cycle instead of quarterly]
- [Capability unlocked 2 — e.g., elasticity to absorb 3x peak load without manual intervention]
- [Capability unlocked 3 — e.g., platform standardization enabling engineers to rotate across 4 teams instead of 1]

**Director framing for this section**: Strategic benefits are the reason executives fund migrations that look borderline on NPV alone. Name the capability, not the sentiment. "Faster deployment" is sentiment. "Reduces release cycle from 8 weeks to 2 weeks, enabling 4x more feature throughput" is a capability.
