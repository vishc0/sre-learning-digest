# Delegation Charter Template

> **Instructions**: Complete this charter with your manager within the first 30 days of the manager taking on the role. This document is the operating contract between Director and manager — it defines authority, accountability, and communication expectations. Review and re-sign annually. Update any time a major structural change affects the domain (reorg, headcount shift, scope change, new platform). This document should be stored in your team's Confluence space, not in email.

## Mission

> One sentence. Why does this manager's role exist? Not a job description — the reason this function needs a dedicated owner. The test: if you removed this manager, what specific harm would occur to the team or the business?

[ONE SENTENCE — e.g., "This role exists to ensure that every production incident is resolved with structure and speed, that every root cause is understood and addressed, and that the on-call team is protected from burnout."]

## Full Authority

> **Definition**: Decisions this manager makes alone. No Director involvement needed — not even a notification. The Director finds out in the weekly update, not a same-day message.

> **Instruction to complete**: List every decision this manager should own independently. If you can't list at least 8 items, the role is not well-defined enough — either the scope is too narrow or you haven't thought through what you actually want this manager to own. Be specific: "Declares P2 and P3 incidents" is a real decision; "manages incidents" is not.

- [DECISION 1 — e.g., "Declares and owns P2 and P3 incidents — severity classification, IC assignment, bridge management, and bridge close"]
- [DECISION 2 — e.g., "Assigns on-call shifts and manages rotation schedules within the established rotation structure"]
- [DECISION 3 — e.g., "Approves or rejects changes to runbooks and playbooks within the Incident & Problem domain"]
- [DECISION 4 — e.g., "Determines whether a PIR is required for a given incident, and selects the PIR facilitator"]
- [DECISION 5 — e.g., "Closes problem records in JIRA/ServiceNow once root cause is confirmed and corrective action is in place"]
- [DECISION 6 — e.g., "Determines training and certification priorities for individual ICs within the approved learning budget"]
- [DECISION 7 — e.g., "Sets sprint priorities for the incident management team's continuous improvement backlog"]
- [DECISION 8 — e.g., "Approves or rejects requests for on-call swap arrangements between rotation members"]
- [DECISION 9 — e.g., "Makes tactical changes to alerting thresholds within approved SLO bounds — no approval needed for threshold adjustments that do not change the SLO"]
- [DECISION 10 — e.g., "Determines meeting cadence and format for team rituals: standups, retrospectives, on-call reviews"]
- [Add as many as needed. Do not cap this list — a well-defined role should have 10–15 items here.]

## Decide and Notify

> **Definition**: The manager makes the call independently. The Director does not need to approve — but must be notified the same business day. These are consequential enough that the Director should not be surprised when they learn about them in the weekly update.

> **Instruction to complete**: Aim for 3–6 items. These are typically decisions that cross organizational boundaries, involve significant resources, or create a precedent. If you have more than 8 items here, ask yourself whether some belong in Full Authority instead.

- [DECISION 1 — e.g., "Engages external teams (Security, Network, Middleware, Database) during a P2 or P3 incident as additional responders — notify Director same business day via Slack"]
- [DECISION 2 — e.g., "Extends a PIR timeline beyond the standard 5-business-day window — notify Director with reason and new target date"]
- [DECISION 3 — e.g., "Implements a temporary process change to incident management workflow during a high-severity period (e.g., adds a mandatory 30-min check-in during sustained P2 events) — notify Director same business day"]
- [DECISION 4 — e.g., "Issues an advisory to dependent teams that a known risk exists in production — notify Director via Slack before sending the advisory"]
- [DECISION 5 — e.g., "Approves overtime for an IC or analyst responding to a complex problem investigation — notify Director same business day"]
- [Add or remove as needed for your domain.]

## Consult Director Before Deciding

> **Definition**: Director input is required before the manager makes a call, but the manager still makes the final call. This is consultation, not approval. The Director's role here is to provide context the manager may not have — cross-domain visibility, political awareness, budget headroom, executive communication threads. If the manager consults the Director and the Director is unavailable, the manager may use judgment and proceed.

> **Instruction to complete**: Aim for 3–5 items. These are typically decisions that have cross-domain or cross-team implications, or that set a precedent that other domains would need to be aware of.

- [DECISION 1 — e.g., "Proposes a structural change to the incident severity classification framework — consult Director before drafting proposal for broader team review"]
- [DECISION 2 — e.g., "Proposes a change to the on-call rotation model that would affect headcount requirements — consult Director before communicating to the team"]
- [DECISION 3 — e.g., "Considers adding a new required stakeholder notification tier to the incident communication protocol — consult Director, as this affects VPs and business partners outside the team"]
- [DECISION 4 — e.g., "Proposes a new tooling adoption (e.g., switching incident management platform) that would affect the wider ops team — consult Director before evaluating vendors"]
- [DECISION 5 — e.g., "Considers a significant change to PIR format or frequency that would affect how PIR outputs are consumed by product or engineering leadership"]
- [Add or remove as needed.]

## Escalate to Director

> **Definition**: Director decides. Manager brings the situation, relevant context, and a recommended option — but the Director owns the outcome.

> **Instruction to complete**: The items below are standard for every charter. Add domain-specific triggers as needed. Do not remove the standard items.

**Standard (non-negotiable for every manager in this org):**
- All personnel actions: offers, counteroffers, performance improvement plans, termination recommendations, promotion nominations
- Headcount requests — additions, backfills, contractor conversions
- Budget requests that exceed the manager's approved discretionary spend threshold: [$AMOUNT — confirm with Director]
- Any P1 incident where the Director has not already joined the bridge (Director joins P1 bridges by default — this item covers scenarios where the Director is unreachable or the P1 escalates unexpectedly)
- Disputes involving a VP or above from another organization
- Any media, legal, or regulatory inquiry related to this domain

## Key Metrics

> **Instruction**: Define 5–8 metrics that tell the Director whether this manager's domain is healthy. Every metric needs a target (what good looks like), a warning threshold (when to pay close attention), and a red threshold (when the Director steps in). The measurement source should be a specific system — not "dashboards" but "PagerDuty weekly report" or "Splunk SLO dashboard in the Ops Metrics app."

| Metric | Target | Warning Threshold | Red Threshold | Measurement Source |
|--------|--------|------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| [METRIC 1 — e.g., Mean Time to Resolve (MTTR) — P2 incidents] | [e.g., ≤ 45 min rolling 4-week average] | [e.g., 46–60 min for 2+ consecutive weeks] | [e.g., > 60 min for any week, or > 45 min for 4+ consecutive weeks] | [e.g., PagerDuty weekly metrics report] |
| [METRIC 2 — e.g., Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA) — P2 incidents] | [e.g., ≤ 5 min] | [e.g., 6–10 min average] | [e.g., > 10 min average, or any P2 unacknowledged > 15 min] | [e.g., PagerDuty on-call report] |
| [METRIC 3 — e.g., Recurring incident rate (same root cause recurrence within 30 days)] | [e.g., < 5% of total incidents] | [e.g., 5–10%] | [e.g., > 10%] | [e.g., JIRA Problem Management board — recurring flag query] |
| [METRIC 4 — e.g., PIR completion rate (PIR closed within 5 business days of incident)] | [e.g., 100% of P1s, ≥ 85% of qualifying P2s] | [e.g., < 85% of P1s in any calendar month] | [e.g., Any P1 PIR open > 10 business days without Director-approved extension] | [e.g., Confluence PIR tracker] |

## Director Steps In When

> **Instruction**: These are the specific triggers that cause the Director to move from observer to active participant in this manager's domain. They should be specific — not "performance degrades" but "MTTR trend has degraded 20%+ over 4 consecutive weeks." A well-defined list here prevents ambiguity about when the Director is overstepping vs. appropriately engaged.

- [TRIGGER 1 — e.g., "MTTR trend has degraded 20% or more compared to the prior 4-week baseline and no recovery plan is in place within 2 weeks of the degradation being identified"]
- [TRIGGER 2 — e.g., "Any single engineer on the on-call rotation has exceeded 15 actionable pages in a single week — Director joins the on-call load conversation"]
- [TRIGGER 3 — e.g., "A P1 incident runs longer than 2 hours without a credible mitigation path — Director joins the bridge and may assume IC or stakeholder communication functions"]
- [TRIGGER 4 — e.g., "A PIR action item is more than 30 days past its committed due date with no owner update — Director escalates to the accountable team's manager"]
- [TRIGGER 5 — e.g., "Weekly update is not received by deadline for 2 consecutive weeks without prior notice — Director initiates a 1:1 to understand the blocker"]
- [TRIGGER 6 — e.g., "Any personnel or team dynamics issue surfaces that the manager flags as outside their authority or comfort level to resolve"]
- [TRIGGER 7 — e.g., "A cross-domain escalation is unresolved after 48 hours — Director mediates between domain managers"]
- [TRIGGER 8 — e.g., "Engineering or product leadership raises a concern about incident management quality directly to the Director rather than the manager — Director loops the manager in and determines whether a process adjustment is needed"]
