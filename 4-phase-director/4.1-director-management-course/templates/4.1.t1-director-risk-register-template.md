# Director Operational Risk Register

**Owner**: Director of Operations  
**Review cadence**: Director review — monthly; VP review — quarterly (presented in quarterly operational review)  
**Purpose**: Track, manage, and communicate the operational risks under the Director's accountability  
**Scope**: Risks within your operational domain that require Director-level awareness and action. Routine operational issues managed by Senior Managers do not belong here.

---

## How to Use This Register

**What belongs here:**
- Risks that could cause Tier 1 SLA breaches or customer-visible incidents if not mitigated
- Risks with financial, regulatory, or reputational consequence at Director-visible scale
- Risks that require Director action or cross-functional coordination to mitigate
- Risks that your VP should know exist, even if currently managed

**What does not belong here:**
- Individual incidents (those belong in ServiceNow)
- Routine operational tasks (those belong in the ops review)
- Personal or team performance issues (those belong in 1:1 documentation)

**Monthly review process:**
1. Review each row — has anything changed in likelihood or impact?
2. Update mitigation effectiveness — is what you're doing working?
3. Identify any risks that should be closed (risk resolved) or escalated (residual risk is H)
4. Add any new risks identified since last review
5. Update "Next Review" dates
6. Pull the top 5 for VP status reporting

## Field Definitions

| Field | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| Risk Name | Short, specific name for the risk — describe what could go wrong, not what you're doing about it. Wrong: "Monitoring improvements." Right: "Legacy monitoring platform failure during peak traffic." |
| Domain | Operational domain: Reliability, Platform, Security, Change Management, Vendor, People/Capacity, Compliance |
| Likelihood | Probability of occurrence in the defined timeframe (see definitions below) |
| Impact | Consequence if the risk materializes (see definitions below) |
| Risk Score | Derived: H+H = Critical; H+M or M+H = High; M+M = Medium; anything with L = Low. Used to prioritize management attention. |
| Current Mitigation | Specific, named actions being taken right now to reduce likelihood or impact. "We are monitoring it" is not a mitigation. |
| Mitigation Effectiveness | How much the current mitigation reduces risk: High = eliminates or near-eliminates; Medium = significantly reduces; Low = reduces marginally |
| Residual Risk | The risk level that remains AFTER applying the current mitigation. This is what you are actually living with. |
| Owner | The named individual (usually a Senior Manager) who is accountable for mitigation execution. Not a team name. |
| Opened | Date this risk was first added to the register |
| Next Review | Date this specific risk should be reviewed again. High residual risks: monthly. Medium: quarterly. Low: at least annually. |
| Status | Open = active; Closed = risk resolved (add closure note); Escalated = requires VP or above decision/awareness |

---

## Likelihood Definitions

| Level | Definition | Timeframe |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| High | Likely to occur if no mitigation action is taken | Within the next 90 days |
| Medium | Possible; elevated probability relative to baseline | Within the next 6 months |
| Low | Possible but not probable under current conditions | Within the next 12 months |

---

## Impact Definitions

| Level | Definition | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| High | Tier 1 SLA breach; regulatory consequence; > $500K financial impact; significant customer-visible outage | Multi-hour Tier 1 outage; PCI/HIPAA finding; major customer escalation |
| Medium | Tier 2 SLA miss; customer escalation to executive level; $50K–$500K financial impact | Tier 2 degradation; single enterprise customer escalation; unplanned project delay > 1 sprint |
| Low | Internal disruption; < $50K financial impact; manageable within team without escalation | Single team impacted; minor metric miss; recoverable delay |

## Escalation Rule

**Any risk where residual risk = H after mitigation is applied must be escalated to VP immediately.**

This means: you have done everything in your power, and significant risk remains. The VP needs to make a resource, priority, or risk-acceptance decision. Holding this information from your VP is a career risk for you, not just an operational risk for the org.

Escalation format:
"I want to flag an operational risk that I am managing but cannot reduce to acceptable levels without additional support. [Risk name]. Current mitigation: [X]. Residual risk: High. Options: (a) [option + cost/trade-off], (b) [option + cost/trade-off]. My recommendation: [your recommendation]. I need [specific decision or resource] by [date]."
