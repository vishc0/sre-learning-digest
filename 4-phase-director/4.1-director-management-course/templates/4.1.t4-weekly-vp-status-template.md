# Weekly VP Status Template

**Format**: Written async, sent end-of-day Friday (or before Monday morning standup, per VP preference)  
**Length**: One page maximum  
**Purpose**: Keep VP informed; surface risks before they escalate; get decisions without scheduling a meeting

---

## How to Use This Template

Fill in all sections. Leave no section blank — if a section has nothing this week, write "None this week" explicitly. A blank section looks like it was skipped.

The highlights section is written for someone who has not been in your meetings this week. The metrics section requires no prose — the table speaks.

If your VP has expressed a preference for a different format or cadence, adjust. This template is the default.

## Guidance Notes

**What makes a strong weekly status:**
- Every metric has a number in it — no blank cells
- Every red metric has a one-line explanation
- The decision section is either "none" or contains a decision that genuinely requires VP response
- Highlights are outcome-oriented, not activity-oriented
- Risks are genuinely VP-level concerns, not routine operational items

**What weakens a weekly status:**
- Highlighting team activities ("we ran the ops review") instead of outcomes
- Using the risks section for items you are already handling without escalation
- Leaving metric cells blank because "the data isn't available yet" — fix the data pipeline, or note "data pending"
- Writing a decision item that you could actually decide yourself
- Running over one page — if it doesn't fit, you haven't edited

**Calibrating to your VP:**
After sending this format for three weeks, ask: "Is this format giving you what you need? Is there anything missing or anything I should cut?" Adjust based on feedback. The format is a starting point, not a contract.
