# Director's Mini Management Course

## What This Is

A self-paced, practical management training program for a Director of Operations stepping into a senior leadership role. Not theory — actionable frameworks, templates, and decision models you can use Monday morning.

This course replaces what a formal training program would give you over 6 months in a classroom. It assumes you already have deep operational expertise. It does not teach you how operations works. It teaches you how to lead an org that runs operations — which is a different job entirely.

---

## Who This Is For

A new or aspiring Director or VP managing a 20–100 person operations function at a mid-size enterprise. You are responsible for:

- Upward reporting to a VP and executive team
- Budget and headcount decisions
- Managing senior managers who manage managers who manage individual contributors
- Vendor contracts and external partner relationships
- Translating executive strategy into operational execution without being the one executing it

If you came up through the technical track (engineer → lead → senior manager → director), this course addresses the parts of the Director role that your technical depth will not prepare you for.

## Course Philosophy

A Director's job is to create the conditions in which the org works when they are not in the room.

Every module builds toward that. If you are the smartest technical person in the room and also the person who makes every decision — you have not become a Director yet. You have become a very busy Senior Manager with a larger title.

The transition from Senior Manager to Director is not about getting more authority. It is about transferring authority deliberately, holding people accountable for results rather than methods, and building an org that can produce outcomes you weren't involved in designing.

That shift is harder than any technical challenge you have solved. This course is designed to make it concrete.

## How to Use This Course

Each module has the same structure:
- A framework section (the mental model)
- Practical application (what this looks like Monday morning)
- Common failure modes (what goes wrong and why)
- A self-assessment at the end

Read a module. Spend a week applying it. Come back and re-read before moving on.

The playbooks in `playbooks/` are reference documents for specific situations. Pull them when you hit the situation they cover — don't try to read them all upfront.

The templates in `templates/` are ready-to-use. Modify the structure to match your org's norms, but do not remove sections — each one exists because an executive or senior manager will expect it.

## A Note on What This Course Does Not Cover

This course does not cover:
- Technical SRE practice (you already know this)
- Company-specific HR processes (read your company handbook)
- Legal obligations for employment decisions (consult HR and legal)
- Financial accounting (consult your finance business partner)

It covers the management judgment layer — the decisions, frameworks, and habits that determine whether a Director succeeds or struggles.
