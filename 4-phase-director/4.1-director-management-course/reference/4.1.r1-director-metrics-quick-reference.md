# Director Metrics Quick Reference

**Audience**: Director of Operations  
**Purpose**: One-page decision guide — which metrics to check, how often, what "red" means, and what to do about it  
**How to use**: Print or pin this. The daily check takes 5 minutes. Weekly review takes 15. If a metric goes red, the "Action if Red" column tells you the first move.

---

## Daily Metrics — 5-Minute Morning Check

Check these before your first meeting every day. Source dashboards should be bookmarked and accessible in under 60 seconds each.

| Metric | Definition | Target | Source | Action if Red |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|--------------|
| **SLO burn rate** | Rate at which error budget is being consumed vs. expected rate. A 2x burn rate means you will exhaust the month's error budget in half the expected time. | < 2x expected burn for any Tier 1 service | Datadog / Splunk SLO dashboard | Page the on-call Senior Manager immediately. Join the bridge if it escalates. This is a Tier 1 response. |
| **Open P1 incidents** | Count of active, open P1 incidents right now | 0 at any given moment | ServiceNow or PagerDuty | Director joins the incident bridge within 15 minutes. Not to solve — to ensure command structure is functioning and customer communication is running. |
| **Overnight change failures** | Changes executed in the last 12 hours that failed or were rolled back | 0 | ServiceNow change module | Review with Change Manager today. Every failed change needs a reason and a decision: retry with fixes, or defer. Do not let failed changes sit unresolved. |
| **On-call page volume** | Pages received per engineer in the last 24 hours | < 5 pages per engineer in any 24-hour window | PagerDuty reporting | Flag to responsible Senior Manager if any engineer received > 10 pages. Above 10 is fatigue territory. Investigate signal vs. noise ratio. |

## Weekly Metrics — 15-Minute Friday Review

Review these at week close. Surface anything amber or red in your weekly VP status.

| Metric | Definition | Target | Source | Action if Red |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|--------------|
| **MTTR — all P1 and P2** | Mean time from incident declaration to customer impact restored to zero | Tier 1: < 30 min average; Tier 2: < 2 hours average | ServiceNow incident module | Open a problem ticket for every P1 or P2 where MTTR exceeded target. Review with Senior Manager in weekly 1:1. Repeated MTTR misses signal systemic issues: on-call expertise, runbook quality, tooling, or handoff failures. |
| **Incident volume by severity** | Count of P1/P2/P3/P4 incidents opened this week | P1: 0; P2: < 2 per week; P3: less than prior week trend | ServiceNow | If P2+ incidents show a repeating pattern (same service, same failure mode, same time window), escalate to problem management immediately. Pattern = unmitigated known error. |
| **Change failure rate** | Percentage of changes deployed this week that caused an incident or required rollback | < 5% of total changes | ServiceNow change + incident correlation | Request CAB review of all failed changes within 72 hours. Root cause required for every failed change above P3 impact. If failure rate > 10%, consider change freeze for non-critical services until root cause is understood. |
| **Open P1 problems** | Count of problem tickets at P1 severity currently open | < 3 at any time | ServiceNow problem module | Director personally reviews any P1 problem older than 30 days. If a problem has been open for 30 days without meaningful progress, either the root cause is elusive (needs a different approach) or the owner is not driving it (needs a different owner). Determine which. |
| **SLO compliance (weekly)** | Percentage of time Tier 1 services met their contracted SLO this week | > 99.9% for Tier 1 services | SLO dashboard | Same-day conversation with the responsible Senior Manager. Any week below 99.9% on Tier 1 is a VP-reportable event — include it in your weekly status without waiting to be asked. |
| **Deployment frequency** | Number of production deployments per service this week | Target varies by service maturity; generally: > 1 deployment per week per active service | CI/CD pipeline | If a service has not had a production deployment in > 2 weeks and is under active development, investigate: pipeline failure, CAB delay, or team capacity issue? Each has a different fix. |

## Monthly Metrics — Hour-Long Review (First Week of Month)

Review with each Senior Manager in their monthly domain review. Present summary in your monthly VP touchpoint.

| Metric | Definition | Target | Source | Action if Red |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|--------------|
| **DORA: Deployment frequency** | Deployments per service per week, monthly average | Elite: > 1/day; High: 1/week; Medium: 1/month; Low: < 1/month. Target at least "High." | CI/CD pipeline (GitHub Actions, Jenkins, ArgoCD) | If a service is at "Low" or "Medium," investigate: is it the pipeline, the governance process, or the team? Identify and fix the constraint. Infrequent deployments create large blast radius when they do happen. |
| **DORA: Lead time for changes** | Commit-to-production time, monthly average | Elite: < 1 hour; High: < 1 day; Medium: < 1 week. Target at least "High." | CI/CD pipeline | If lead time is > 1 week, something is creating delay: long test cycles, CAB approval lag, manual gates, or large batch sizes. Ask the Senior Manager to map the pipeline and identify the constraint. |
| **DORA: Change failure rate** | Percentage of production deployments that caused an incident or required rollback, monthly | < 5% (Elite: < 1%) | ServiceNow + CI/CD correlation | If > 5%: review pre-deployment testing gates. Are changes going to production without adequate testing? Review CAB process — are high-risk changes being identified and scrutinized? |
| **DORA: MTTR trend** | Average MTTR for P1/P2 incidents, monthly trend | Declining month-over-month | ServiceNow | If MTTR is flat or rising over 3 consecutive months, the problem management process is not producing improved runbooks. Review problem closure quality — are root causes producing actionable fixes? |
| **SLA breaches** | Count of SLA breaches this month where credits were issued or where contractual thresholds were crossed | 0 credit-issuing breaches | ServiceNow SLA module | Notify VP same day a credit-issuing breach is confirmed. Customer communication plan is Director-level decision. Every breach must have a PIR and a problem ticket opened within 48 hours. |
| **On-call load average** | Pages per engineer per on-call night, monthly average | < 3 pages per engineer per night | PagerDuty monthly report | If average > 5: the alert signal-to-noise ratio is broken, or there are systemic reliability issues generating alert volume. Both require immediate attention — on-call load above 5 is an attrition risk. Engineers leave teams with bad on-call before they complain about it. |
| **Toil percentage** | Estimated percentage of engineering time spent on toil (repetitive, automatable, undifferentiated work) | < 30% (target < 20%) | Team survey, estimation, or time-tracking (monthly pulse) | If toil > 40%: the team is running, not building. Identify the top 3 toil sources and put them on the automation roadmap. If the team cannot reduce toil because they are too busy with toil to automate it, you have a capacity and prioritization problem — escalate. |

## Quarterly Metrics — Executive Scorecard

Presented in the quarterly operational review. These are the metrics your VP and SVP will see.

| Metric | Definition | Target | Source | Notes |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Availability by tier** | Actual uptime vs. SLA commitment, quarterly | Tier 1: ≥ 99.99%; Tier 2: ≥ 99.9%; Tier 3: ≥ 99.5% | SLO dashboard | Show as a trend chart over 4 quarters, not just current quarter. Trend matters more than a single data point. |
| **Error budget consumption** | Percentage of the annual (or quarterly) error budget consumed by realized downtime | < 100% at quarter end; alert if > 50% at midpoint | SLO dashboard | If consuming budget faster than planned at quarter midpoint, adjust: freeze non-critical changes, increase reliability investment, or adjust SLO target with business alignment. |
| **Problem backlog health** | Open P1/P2 problems at end of quarter, and average age of open problems | < 5 P1 problems open at any time; average age < 45 days | ServiceNow | A growing, aging problem backlog is evidence that problem management is a reporting function, not a resolution function. It is also a risk accumulator — each open problem is a risk not yet mitigated. |
| **Team health index** | Composite of: attrition rate (rolling 12 months), average on-call load (pages/night/month), toil percentage (quarterly survey) | Green: all three in target range; Amber: one out of range; Red: two or more out of range | HR (attrition) + PagerDuty + team survey | Team health is a lagging indicator — by the time it goes red, you have already lost 6–12 months of stability. Review it quarterly; act on early amber signals. |
| **Transformation velocity** | Count of applications modernized, migrated, or EOL'd this quarter vs. roadmap commitment | Per roadmap commitment (defined in annual planning) | Portfolio or program tracker | If consistently below roadmap commitment, investigate: is it resource, technical complexity, dependency, or scope creep? Persistent under-delivery on transformation erodes leadership credibility. |
| **Budget vs. actuals** | Year-to-date actual spend vs. approved budget | Within 5% (over or under) | Finance / ERP system | Both overspend and material underspend require explanation. Overspend: what unplanned cost drove it? Underspend: what planned investment was not made — is that a good thing (cost discipline) or a risk (deferred capability)? |

## Metrics Interpretation Reference

**The difference between a metric and a signal:**
A metric is a number. A signal is a number that tells you something is wrong. Every metric in this reference has a defined threshold at which it becomes a signal. Until it crosses that threshold, it is background information. When it crosses the threshold, it requires action — not discussion.

**Trend matters more than current value:**
A metric at 4.9% change failure rate is "at target." A metric that has been at 4.9% for three months after being at 2.5% is a trend that demands attention. Always look at 3–4 periods when reviewing monthly and quarterly metrics.

**Correlation tells you where to look:**
When two metrics go red simultaneously, the overlap is often the root cause. P2 incident volume up + change failure rate up = look at change quality. MTTR up + problem backlog growing = problem management process is not producing runbooks fast enough. Train your Senior Managers to spot correlations before they escalate to you.

**The metric you don't have is the one that bites you:**
If you do not have a source for a metric in this reference, that is the first priority — not optimizing a metric you already have. Missing data creates blind spots. Blind spots create surprises.
