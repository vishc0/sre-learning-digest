[Describe what success looks like 12 months after a solution is deployed. What is different? What is no longer happening? What is now possible? Define in measurable terms where possible.]

## 3. Strategic Capability Test

**Capability Classification**: [Commodity / Differentiator / Uncertain]

**Rationale**  
[Answer this question directly: Would a competitor purchasing the same vendor solution at the same price have the same strategic advantage in the market as we would by building this in-house? If yes, this is a commodity — buying levels the playing field rather than creating disadvantage. If no — if your proprietary data, operational context, or specific implementation approach creates advantage that a vendor cannot replicate — it may be a differentiator. State your reasoning in 2–4 sentences.]

**Implication**  
[If commodity: "Building this capability requires strong justification because the market already offers solutions that are functionally equivalent. The burden of proof is on the build option to demonstrate why the buy alternative is insufficient." If differentiator: "Purchasing a vendor solution for this capability requires strong justification because vendor solutions may not replicate the strategic advantage available through a custom implementation. The burden of proof is on the buy option." If uncertain: "The strategic classification is unclear. This decision will be driven by TCO and risk assessment rather than strategic capability framing."]
