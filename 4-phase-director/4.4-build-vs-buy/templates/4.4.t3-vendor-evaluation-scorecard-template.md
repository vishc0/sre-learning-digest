## Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **1** | Does not meet requirement. Significant gap that would require workaround or custom development to address. |
| **2** | Partially meets requirement with material gaps. Workaround exists but adds cost or complexity. |
| **3** | Meets requirement. Solves the problem as defined. No significant gaps for the stated use case. |
| **4** | Meets requirement well. Exceeds minimum in ways that reduce implementation risk or total cost. |
| **5** | Exceeds requirement. Best-in-class performance on this criterion. Provides durable advantage. |

**Calculation**: Weighted Score = Raw Score × Weight (expressed as decimal, e.g., 25% = 0.25)

## Criterion Definitions

### Functional Fit (25%)

**What is being scored**: Does the vendor's product solve the actual problem stated in the problem statement, for the specific use cases defined in the RFP?

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | Product addresses all must-have requirements and more than half of nice-to-have requirements without customization. PoC confirmed capability for the hardest integration or highest-load scenario. |
| **3** | Product addresses all must-have requirements. Nice-to-haves are mostly absent but not blocking. PoC confirmed capability for core use cases. Minor configuration required. |
| **1** | Product fails to meet one or more must-have requirements. Gaps would require significant custom development to bridge. PoC failed or was not completed. |

**How to score this**: Score based on PoC results first, then RFP response. Do not score on demo or marketing materials alone.

---

### 3-Year TCO (20%)

**What is being scored**: Total cost of ownership over 3 years, including license/subscription, implementation, integration, training, and maintenance — compared to competing options.

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | 3-year TCO is more than 15% below the next cheapest option. Pricing is transparent, predictable, and locked by contract. |
| **3** | 3-year TCO is within 10% of competing options (within the margin of estimation error). Pricing model is clear and usage-based predictability is acceptable. |
| **1** | 3-year TCO is more than 20% above competing options, OR pricing model is opaque/consumption-based in ways that make 3-year projection unreliable. |

**How to score this**: Use the TCO model from the decision document. Score the vendor relative to the alternatives — this is a comparative score, not an absolute one.

---

### Vendor Stability (15%)

**What is being scored**: The likelihood that this vendor will remain a viable, independent, customer-focused company for the duration of the contract term and beyond.

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | Established vendor (7+ years), profitable or funded through foreseeable future, no acquisition rumors, strong market position, active product investment. |
| **3** | Vendor is 3–7 years old with credible funding or revenue, no known acquisition discussions, product roadmap is publicly committed. Some uncertainty about long-term trajectory. |
| **1** | Vendor is early-stage with thin funding runway, has recently changed ownership or is rumored acquisition target, or is discontinuing the specific product you evaluated. |

**How to score this**: Check Crunchbase for funding history, LinkedIn for team growth/attrition trends, and news for acquisition rumors. Ask the vendor directly for revenue and headcount trends — evaluate how they respond, not just what they say.

---

### Integration Complexity (15%)

**What is being scored**: The engineering effort required to integrate this vendor's product with your existing stack (EKS, Kafka, Splunk, Okta, RabbitMQ, or whatever your named stack is). Lower complexity is a higher score.

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | Native integration with your stack components exists and was confirmed working in PoC. Integration required less than 1 engineer-week. Standard protocols (OAuth2, REST, OpenMetrics) throughout — no proprietary SDK required. |
| **3** | Integration requires moderate engineering effort (2–4 engineer-weeks). Standard APIs used but some custom configuration required. PoC integration was successful with expected effort. |
| **1** | Integration requires significant engineering effort (>6 engineer-weeks), or required proprietary SDK with no standard alternative, or PoC integration failed and required vendor professional services involvement. |

**How to score this**: Score based on actual PoC integration experience. If PoC was not run for this vendor, use the RFP technical response and penalize for lack of validated evidence.

---

### Support Quality (10%)

**What is being scored**: The quality and responsiveness of vendor support — during the sales/PoC period (as a leading indicator) and as reported by reference customers (as a lagging indicator).

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | Vendor was responsive, technically credible, and helpful during PoC. References independently confirmed high support quality 18+ months post-implementation. Named support contacts provided. Contractual support SLAs are specific and credit-backed. |
| **3** | Vendor was responsive during sales cycle. References report support quality as "adequate" — problems get resolved but response times are longer than expected. SLAs exist but credits are minimal. |
| **1** | Vendor was unresponsive or evasive during PoC. References report degraded support quality post-contract, escalation difficulties, or high engineer turnover on the account team. SLA terms are vague or not credit-backed. |

**How to score this**: Support quality during the sales cycle is a preview of support quality post-contract. Vendors who are slow to respond when they are trying to win your business will be slower after they have it.

---

### Exit Ease (10%)

**What is being scored**: How difficult and costly would it be to stop using this vendor's product if needed — based on data portability, API standards, and contract terms.

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | Data portability clause in contract specifies format (open standard) and timeline. Data export was tested and succeeded. APIs are standard protocols; integrations can be repointed to a replacement with minimal rewriting. Termination for convenience clause with no penalty. |
| **3** | Data portability clause exists but format is partially proprietary or timeline is 30+ days. APIs are a mix of standard and proprietary. Termination for convenience clause requires 90+ days notice or has minor penalties. |
| **1** | No data portability clause in contract; vendor controls data format. Data export was not tested or failed. Proprietary APIs throughout; integration replacement would require full rewrite. No termination for convenience; early exit penalties are material. |

**How to score this**: Score based on the contract review findings (see `vendor-contract-review-template.md`) and the data export test result from Phase 3 of the vendor evaluation. A score of 1 on exit ease is a hard flag — reconsider the vendor unless the contract can be renegotiated.

---

### Reference Quality (5%)

**What is being scored**: The quality and candor of reference conversations — not whether the reference was positive, but whether the reference provided useful, honest information about the real-world experience of using this vendor's product.

| Score | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **5** | References were independently sourced (not vendor-provided only). References described specific challenges and how they were resolved. References gave an honest assessment of support quality, implementation timeline accuracy, and any price changes at renewal. |
| **3** | References were vendor-provided. References were generally positive with one or two candid observations about challenges. Implementation timeline was described as "roughly as expected" with minor delays. |
| **1** | References declined to speak or gave only scripted positive responses. No independently sourced references found. Vendor provided only testimonials, not actual contacts. |

**How to score this**: A reference that tells you only positive things is not a reference — it is a marketing asset. Score down for references that will not engage with the hard questions.
