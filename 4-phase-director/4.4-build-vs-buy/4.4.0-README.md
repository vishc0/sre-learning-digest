# Build vs. Buy Decision Framework

**Purpose**: Turn the gut-feel build-vs-buy debate into a repeatable analytical process. Director and VP candidates are tested on this competency in architecture and governance interviews because it sits at the intersection of strategy, finance, vendor management, and engineering leadership. This framework gives you a structured, defensible answer every time.

---

## Why This Matters at Director Level

Four outcomes kill Director careers:

1. **Building a commodity** — 18 months of engineering time to recreate what PagerDuty already does. You own the maintenance forever.
2. **Buying a differentiator** — purchasing the ML anomaly detection your competitors can also purchase. You sold your competitive advantage for a vendor relationship.
3. **Building what you can't maintain** — the platform team that built the internal deployment tool left. Now it's a strategic liability no one can touch.
4. **Buying what you can't exit** — Broadcom acquires VMware. Directors with no exit clause face 3-5x licensing increases and zero leverage.

This framework exists to prevent all four.

## How to Use This Framework

**For interviews**: Read ch01-02 first — foundations and the strategic capability test are the conceptual core. Then ch03-04 for financial and vendor fluency. ch05-06 for modern platform context. ch07-08 for governance and executive communication.

**For active decisions**: Start with the `build-vs-buy-decision-template`, use ch02 to classify the capability, ch03 to model TCO, and ch04 or the vendor evaluation playbook if buy is the direction.

**For quick lookup**: The three reference files are standalone — use them mid-meeting or mid-conversation without rereading chapters.
