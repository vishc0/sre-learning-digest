# Post 1: Zero Sev1 — Reliability Culture Story

---

36 months. Zero Sev1 incidents. Here is what actually made that possible.

It was not the monitoring stack. It was not the runbooks. It was not even the on-call rotation.

It was the conversation we had the first time something almost became a Sev1 — and did not.

We ran a blameless postmortem on a near-miss. No pages fired. No customers affected. Most teams would have called that a win and moved on. We spent 90 minutes on it anyway. We wrote up the failure mode, the detection gap, and the three system changes that would have prevented it from ever getting close.

That postmortem culture compounded. Engineers started surfacing near-misses voluntarily — because they knew the outcome was a better system, not a performance conversation. In 36 months managing a 25-million-message-per-day notification platform at T-Mobile, we never had a single incident that rose to Sev1.

The insight Directors rarely talk about: psychological safety is a reliability investment, not an HR concept. Every engineer who stays quiet about a fragile system because they fear blame is technical debt you cannot see on any dashboard.

What killed most Sev1s before they became Sev1s? Humans who felt safe enough to say "this worries me" three weeks before it failed.

The monitoring stack matters. The culture around it matters more.

What is the near-miss your team is not talking about right now?
