Build a STAR-format behavioral story for a Director/VP SRE interview. Ask me for the topic if not provided.

Common topics: incident command, team scaling, platform reliability, DevSecOps transformation, stakeholder conflict, technical decision under pressure, budget trade-off, hiring/performance management, influencing without authority.

For the given topic, produce:

---

## STAR Story: $TOPIC

**Situation**
[Context: T-Mobile, 25M msg/day notification platform, 15-person SRE team, RabbitMQ/Kubernetes/Splunk stack. Set the stakes — what was at risk?]

**Task**
[What were you specifically responsible for? Use "I" not "we". What was the expectation?]

**Action**
[3-5 specific actions you took. Name tools, decisions, stakeholders. Show systems thinking + leadership simultaneously. No generics.]

**Result**
[Quantified outcome: % improvement, $ saved, time reduced, incidents prevented, team growth. Include timeline if impressive.]

**Director/VP Lens**
[One sentence on what this shows about your readiness for a Director/VP role — strategic thinking, org influence, or business impact.]

**Transferable Hook**
[How to connect this story to the interviewer's world at their company specifically.]

---

Then ask: "Should I also prepare the follow-up questions an interviewer might ask after this story?"

Key rules:
- Never use "we" without also crediting "I did X specifically"
- Every story must end with a number
- Frame for the level being targeted (Director = org impact, VP = business impact)
- H1B should never come up in STAR stories unless directly asked
