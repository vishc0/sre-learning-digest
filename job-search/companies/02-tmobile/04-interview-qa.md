# T-Mobile Internal — Talking Points for Internal Discussion

## For Performance / Promotion Review Conversation

**If asked: Why should you be promoted to Director?**
"Three things. First, scope: I already manage 4 production platforms and 15 engineers — that's Director scope, not Principal scope. The title hasn't caught up with the reality. Second, impact: the MART framework I built has been adopted beyond my platforms; the AI tooling I deployed is unique in T-Mobile's notification org. I've delivered Director-level impact for 3+ years at Principal compensation. Third, readiness: I'm not asking to grow into a Director role — I'm already executing it."

**If asked: What would you do at Director level that you can't do now?**
"Three things I can't fully accomplish at Principal level: (1) Set SRE standards across the entire notification domain — not just my 4 platforms but every notification-adjacent team. (2) Build and own the AI-SRE capability at org level — right now I've built it for my team, but the pattern needs to spread. (3) Hire and develop the next generation of SRE leaders — I have engineers who are ready to become Principals but I need Director authority to make those promotions happen."

**If asked: What's your market value?**
"Based on my profile — 21 years, 10 years T-Mobile Principal SRE, 25M msgs/day platform, 15 reports, AI tooling in production — companies like Amazon, Apple, Adobe, and Capital One are at $190K–$280K base for Principal/Manager equivalent. I'm sharing that not as a threat but as a calibration point. I want to stay at T-Mobile; I want to grow here. The compensation needs to reflect market reality for that to be the right decision."

**If asked: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?**
"I see myself owning the SRE architecture for T-Mobile's entire customer communications domain — not just notifications, but how every customer-facing event is observed, governed, and reliably delivered. That's a Director or Distinguished Engineer scope. If that path exists here, I'm fully committed to building it. If T-Mobile doesn't see that scope for me, I'd need to find it elsewhere."

## Signs the Internal Path Is Real vs. Being Managed
- Real: "Let's get you into Director-track discussion with [VP name] within the next quarter"
- Being managed: "You're doing great, let's revisit at your next review" (kick the can)
- If being managed: set a specific 90-day deadline and external applications begin in parallel
