## H1B Verification Steps

### Step 1: Check USCIS/LCA data before applying

Use these free tools to verify H1B sponsorship history:

- **myvisajobs.com**: Search company name → see LCA filings by year, job title, and salary. Example: [myvisajobs.com/Visa-Sponsor/Komodo-Health/1301147_Visa.htm](https://www.myvisajobs.com/Visa-Sponsor/Komodo-Health/1301147_Visa.htm)
- **h1bgrader.com**: Gives approval rate score per company. Example: [h1bgrader.com/h1b-sponsors/socure-inc-pg27r5yg05](https://h1bgrader.com/h1b-sponsors/socure-inc-pg27r5yg05)
- **h1bdata.info**: Raw USCIS H1B data with filing counts. Search by employer name.

Interpretation guide:
- 10+ LCA filings = active sponsor, low friction
- 1–9 LCA filings = sponsors occasionally; ask HR explicitly
- 0 filings = company has never sponsored OR is very new. Ask in recruiter call — do not assume they won't.

### Step 2: Ask on recruiter call 1 — exact script

Use this verbatim during the first recruiter screen, after they confirm interest in your profile:

> "One thing I want to confirm early to save both of our time — I'm on H1B with an approved I-140, so I'm eligible for H1B transfer. Does [Company Name] sponsor H1B transfers for roles at this level? I've found that most companies this size do, just want to confirm before we go further."

Why this script works:
- "Approved I-140" signals you are not a fresh international hire — you are mid-pipeline, lower friction
- "H1B transfer" is less scary to HR than "H1B sponsorship" — it's a transfer, not a new cap filing
- "Save both of our time" signals you are professional and not hiding it
- Asking after they confirm interest reduces the chance of early screen-out before they know your value

### Step 3: LinkedIn signals check

Before applying to a company where USCIS data shows 0 filings:
1. Search LinkedIn: `[Company Name] employees` → filter by "India" nationality or common H1B origin countries
2. If you see several current employees from India, Mexico, or Southeast Asia in engineering roles, the company almost certainly sponsors
3. Check those employees' profiles for job start dates — recent hires (2023+) under H1B era suggest active sponsorship

### Step 4: Confirm premium processing in offer negotiation

Once an offer is made, before signing:
- Request premium processing coverage (~$2,805 in 2025): `"I'd like to include premium processing coverage for the H1B transfer as part of the offer package."`
- This is standard at any company that regularly sponsors. If they push back, it is a yellow flag about their immigration support program.
