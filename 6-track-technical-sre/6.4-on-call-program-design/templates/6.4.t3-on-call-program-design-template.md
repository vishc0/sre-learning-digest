## 3. Severity Definitions

| Severity | Label | Definition | Example — Notification Platform | Response SLA |
|---------|-------|-----------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| **P0** | SEV1 — Critical | Full service outage; customer-visible impact affecting >10% of users; data loss; regulatory impact | Notification delivery rate drops to 0%; messages not delivered to any customers; DLQ overflowing with no processing | Acknowledge within 5 min; all-hands response; manager notified immediately |
| **P1** | SEV2 — High | Partial outage; customer-visible impact affecting 1-10% of users; SLO breach in progress | Notification delivery rate below 90%; specific carrier segment failing; API p99 latency >5s | Acknowledge within 10 min; Tier 1+2 response; manager notified within 15 min |
| **P2** | SEV3 — Medium | Degraded performance; <1% customer impact; SLO at risk but not breached | Delivery rate 90-95% (below SLO but above critical threshold); individual pod restarts with service recovery | Acknowledge within 30 min; Tier 1 response; manager async notification |
| **P3** | SEV4 — Low | Minor issue; no customer impact; SLO healthy | Single pod OOMKilled and restarted cleanly; alert fired and auto-resolved; non-customer-facing service degraded | Ticket created; no page; addressed in next business day triage |

## 4. Escalation Policy

### 4a. Escalation Tiers

| Tier | Role | Contact Method | Response SLA | When to Escalate To |
|------|------|----------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **1** | Primary On-Call Engineer | PagerDuty page → phone if no ack in 5 min | Acknowledge in 5 min | — |
| **2** | Secondary On-Call (Senior SRE) | Auto-escalation after Tier 1 timeout, or explicit request from Tier 1 | Acknowledge in 10 min | No Tier 1 ack in 10 min; Tier 1 requests backup |
| **3** | On-Call Manager | Phone call for P0/P1; Slack for P2 inform | Respond in 15 min for P0; 30 min for P1 | Any P0; P1 not resolved in 20 min; resource/authority needed |
| **4** | Director / VP | Phone call only | Situational awareness within 30 min of P0 | P0 >60 min; business/regulatory impact; media-visible event |

### 4b. Escalation Policy Published In

- PagerDuty: [link to escalation policy config]
- Confluence: [link to written policy document]

### 4c. Escalation Rules

- Never page Tier 2 simultaneously with Tier 1 for routine P1/P2 incidents
- Always call (do not just message) Tier 3 for P0 events
- For security incidents: notify security team lead AND Tier 3 simultaneously, regardless of severity classification
- For data incidents: notify Tier 3 within 15 minutes of identification, regardless of confirmed scope

## 6. Alert Standards

| Standard | Target | Current | Notes |
|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| Pages/engineer/week | ≤7 | [current] | Measured weekly; reported in monthly ops review |
| Actionable rate | ≥70% | [current] | Measured monthly; reported in monthly ops review |
| After-hours page ratio | ≤25% | [current] | % of pages outside 7am–10pm local time |
| P0/P1 alert coverage | 100% have runbooks | [current] | Audited quarterly |
| Runbook link in alert | 100% of P0/P1 alerts | [current] | Validated in quarterly alert audit |

### 6a. Required Fields for Every Alert

Every alert that can generate a PagerDuty incident must include:
- **Title**: descriptive, service-prefixed (e.g., "notification-service: delivery-rate-below-95pct")
- **Severity**: P0/P1/P2/P3
- **Runbook URL**: direct link, not a link to the team Confluence page
- **Service owner**: team or squad name
- **Environment**: prod / staging / dev

## 7. Runbook Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|------------|---------|
| **Required sections** | Trigger (exact alert name), Context (system description and impact), Triage steps (specific commands with expected outputs), Resolution paths (decision tree), Expected resolution state, Escalation path, Related runbooks |
| **Review cadence** | Top-20 alerts: quarterly. All other runbooks: biannually. |
| **Freshness threshold** | Runbook not updated in 90+ days is flagged as "potentially stale" in automated weekly check |
| **Review owner** | Each runbook must have a named owner (not a team — an individual) |
| **Pre-publish checklist** | Author must complete the runbook quality checklist (see reference/runbook-quality-criteria-reference.md) before publishing |
| **Test requirement** | New runbooks must be walked through during a game day or actual incident within 60 days of publication |
