## 1. Dashboard Purpose and Audience

## 2. Panel 1 — Pages per Engineer per Week

**Visualization type**: Line chart with one line per engineer, 13-week rolling window
**Purpose**: Identify individual engineers with disproportionate pager load; track trend

**Configuration:**
- X-axis: Week (ISO week number)
- Y-axis: Pages per week
- One series per on-call engineer (by name, not by alias)
- Threshold line at 7 pages/week (warning — yellow)
- Threshold line at 10 pages/week (critical — red)
- Include a team average line (dashed, gray)

**Alert threshold**: If any engineer's 4-week average exceeds 10 pages/week, post to #oncall-health Slack channel tagging the SRE Manager

**Splunk SPL:**
```spl
index=pagerduty sourcetype=pagerduty_webhook earliest=-91d
| eval week=strftime(_time, "%Y-W%V")
| stats count as pages_that_week by week, assigned_to_summary
| eval pages_per_week=pages_that_week
| timechart span=1w sum(pages_per_week) by assigned_to_summary
```

**Grafana alternative**: Use PagerDuty data source plugin with a time-series query grouped by `assigned_to_user.summary`, span 1w, 13-week range

## 3. Panel 2 — Actionable Rate Trend

**Visualization type**: Line chart, single series (team aggregate), 13-week rolling window
**Purpose**: Track whether alert quality work is improving the signal-to-noise ratio

**Configuration:**
- X-axis: Week
- Y-axis: Actionable rate % (0-100)
- Target line at 70% (green)
- Warning threshold at 50% (yellow)
- Critical threshold at 30% (red)

**Definition for this dashboard**: Actionable = incident was acknowledged AND resolved more than 5 minutes after acknowledgment (proxy for "human work was required"). Auto-resolved incidents without acknowledgment are non-actionable.

**Alert threshold**: If 4-week rolling actionable rate drops below 50%, post to #oncall-health and tag SRE Manager

**Splunk SPL:**
```spl
index=pagerduty sourcetype=pagerduty_webhook earliest=-91d
| eval week=strftime(_time, "%Y-W%V")
| eval ack_to_resolve_sec=resolve_time-acknowledge_time
| eval actionable=if(acknowledge_time>0 AND ack_to_resolve_sec>300, 1, 0)
| stats count as total, sum(actionable) as actionable_count by week
| eval actionable_rate=round(actionable_count/total*100, 1)
| timechart span=1w avg(actionable_rate)
```

## 4. Panel 3 — After-Hours Page Ratio

**Visualization type**: Stacked bar chart — three segments per week bar
**Purpose**: Show human cost burden: what fraction of on-call work happens outside business hours

**Segments:**
- Business hours (7am–10pm local time, weekdays): green
- After-hours weekday (before 7am or after 10pm, weekdays): yellow
- Weekend (Friday 10pm – Monday 7am): red

**Configuration:**
- X-axis: Week
- Y-axis: Page count (absolute, not %) — stacked bars show composition
- Overlay line: after-hours % of total (right Y-axis)
- Reference line: 25% after-hours target

**Alert threshold**: If after-hours page ratio exceeds 40% for 2 consecutive weeks, tag SRE Manager in #oncall-health

**Splunk SPL:**
```spl
index=pagerduty sourcetype=pagerduty_webhook earliest=-91d
| eval hour=strftime(_time, "%H")
| eval dow=strftime(_time, "%u")
| eval time_category=case(
    dow>5 OR (dow=5 AND hour>=22) OR (dow=1 AND hour<7), "weekend",
    hour<7 OR hour>=22, "after_hours_weekday",
    true(), "business_hours")
| eval week=strftime(_time, "%Y-W%V")
| stats count by week, time_category
| xyseries week time_category count
```

## 5. Panel 4 — Top 10 Noisiest Alerts

**Visualization type**: Table (no chart — ranked list)
**Purpose**: Surface the highest-priority targets for alert quality work; track campaign progress

**Columns:**
| Alert Name | Service | Severity | Fire Count (30d) | Actionable Rate % | Last Fired | Status |
|-----------|---------|---------|-----------------|------------------|-----------|--------|

**Configuration:**
- Sort by Fire Count descending
- Conditional formatting: Actionable Rate <50% → red cell; 50-70% → yellow; >70% → green
- "Status" column manually maintained: KEEP / REVIEW / IN CAMPAIGN / COMPLETED
- Refresh: weekly (Sunday midnight)

**Splunk SPL:**
```spl
index=pagerduty sourcetype=pagerduty_webhook earliest=-30d
| stats count as fire_count,
        sum(eval(if(acknowledge_time>0 AND (resolve_time-acknowledge_time)>300,1,0))) as actionable,
        max(_time) as last_fired
  by alert_name, service_name, priority
| eval actionable_rate=round(actionable/fire_count*100,1)
| eval last_fired_date=strftime(last_fired, "%Y-%m-%d")
| sort -fire_count
| head 10
| table alert_name, service_name, priority, fire_count, actionable_rate, last_fired_date
```

## 6. Panel 5 — MTTA by Severity

**Visualization type**: Two tables side by side (median MTTA and P95 MTTA)
**Purpose**: Track responsiveness; identify severity levels where response SLAs are being missed

**Configuration:**
- Rows: P0, P1, P2 (P3 excluded — no page)
- Columns: Median MTTA (minutes), P95 MTTA (minutes), SLA Target (minutes), SLA Met % (last 30 days)
- Reference values for SLA targets:
  - P0: SLA = 5 min
  - P1: SLA = 10 min
  - P2: SLA = 30 min
- Color coding: green if SLA Met % >90%, yellow 70-90%, red <70%
- Time window: rolling 30 days

**Splunk SPL:**
```spl
index=pagerduty sourcetype=pagerduty_webhook earliest=-30d
| eval mtta_min=round((acknowledge_time - created_time)/60, 1)
| eval sla_target=case(priority="P1",5, priority="P2",10, priority="P3",30, true(),999)
| eval sla_met=if(mtta_min<=sla_target,1,0)
| stats median(mtta_min) as median_mtta,
        perc95(mtta_min) as p95_mtta,
        avg(sla_met)*100 as sla_met_pct
  by priority
| sort priority
```

## 7. Panel 6 — Rotation Coverage Health

**Visualization type**: Calendar heatmap (7-day rolling, 13-week view)
**Purpose**: Identify coverage gaps — days where fewer than 2 engineers are scheduled on-call (primary + secondary)

**Configuration:**
- Calendar heatmap with one cell per day
- Cell value: number of engineers on-call that day
- Color coding:
  - 0 engineers: bright red (unacceptable)
  - 1 engineer: orange (single point of failure)
  - 2 engineers: green (healthy — primary + secondary)
  - 3+ engineers: dark green (over-covered — check for inefficiency)

**Data source**: PagerDuty schedules API — `/schedules/{id}/users` for each day in the window
(For Splunk: pull from PagerDuty audit log if schedule events are indexed)

**Alert threshold**: Any day with 0 or 1 engineer scheduled triggers an immediate Slack post to #oncall-health and tags the Schedule Owner for remediation before that date arrives

## 8. Alert Thresholds for Dashboard Panels

When any of these conditions are detected, the dashboard generates a Slack notification to #oncall-health channel, tagging the SRE Manager:

| Condition | Threshold | Slack Tag |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Individual engineer pages/week | >10 (4-week avg) | @sre-manager |
| Team actionable rate | <50% (4-week avg) | @sre-manager |
| After-hours page ratio | >40% (2 consecutive weeks) | @sre-manager |
| Coverage gap detected | Any day with <2 engineers | @schedule-owner |
| P0 MTTA SLA missed | SLA met % <80% (30-day) | @sre-manager + @director |
