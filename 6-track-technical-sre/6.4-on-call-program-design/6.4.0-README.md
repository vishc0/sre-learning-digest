# On-Call Program Design — SRE Training Framework

## What This Framework Covers

This framework teaches on-call program design at the Director level — not just how to configure PagerDuty, but how to diagnose broken programs, redesign them systematically, and present on-call health as a reliability metric to executives.

The material is built for an SRE Manager or Principal SRE preparing for Director/VP Platform Engineering interviews, or for someone who has just inherited an on-call program and needs to rapidly assess its health.

**Platform context throughout**: EKS/Kubernetes, Splunk, PagerDuty, high-volume notification services (25M+ messages/day).

---

## Who This Is For

- SRE Managers moving to Director/VP roles who need to articulate on-call program design decisions
- Principal SREs tasked with redesigning or auditing an inherited on-call program
- Engineering Managers who need to justify headcount or tooling investment using on-call health data

---

## How to Use This Framework

### Chapters (Read in Order)
The chapters build a mental model. Start with `ch01` if you are new to the domain. Jump to `ch06` or `ch08` if you are in the middle of an active problem.

### Playbooks (Use During Execution)
Playbooks are numbered step-by-step guides with decision gates. Pull the relevant playbook when you are doing the work, not when you are learning the concepts.

### Templates (Fill In and Use)
Templates are working documents. Copy, fill in, and use them directly. They include example values to reduce time-to-useful.

### References (Look Up During Conversations)
Reference files are quick-lookup tables. Use them when you need a benchmark number or comparison during a meeting or interview.
