# Capacity Planning — SRE Training Framework

**Audience**: SRE Managers and Directors preparing for Director/VP Platform Engineering roles  
**Level**: Director-level decision framing throughout — financial justification, VP/CFO presentations, owning the capacity investment narrative  
**Context**: Built around T-Mobile-scale infrastructure (EKS/Kubernetes, Cassandra, Splunk, 25M msg/day notification platform)

---

## What This Framework Covers

Capacity planning is where SRE intersects with financial planning. This framework teaches you to think about capacity not as a technical exercise in provisioning nodes, but as a risk management and investment decision that you present to VPs and CFOs.

The framework is structured in four layers:

- **Chapters**: Conceptual grounding — WHY before HOW. Each chapter builds a Director-level mental model for one domain of capacity planning.
- **Playbooks**: Operational procedures — numbered step-by-step processes for specific situations. Role-labeled. Decision gates included.
- **Templates**: Working documents — fill-in artifacts you use in real reviews, real proposals, real audits.
- **References**: Quick-lookup tables — formulas, sizing guidelines, failure pattern libraries. Use during incidents and reviews.

---

## How to Use This Framework

**For interview preparation**: Read chapters 1, 3, 7, and 8 first. These establish the mental models and Director-level framing that interviewers test.

**For building a capacity practice from scratch**: Follow the chapters in order (1 → 8), then implement using the playbooks and templates.

**During a capacity incident**: Go directly to `reference/capacity-incident-patterns-reference.md` and `playbook/capacity-crisis-response-playbook.md`.

**For a quarterly review**: Use `playbook/quarterly-capacity-review-playbook.md` + `templates/capacity-model-template.md` + `templates/capacity-executive-summary-template.md`.

**For a new product launch**: Start with `playbook/product-launch-capacity-playbook.md` at T-minus 8 weeks.
