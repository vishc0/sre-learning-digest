# Chapter 7 — Capacity Review Process: Making Capacity Planning a Habit, Not a Crisis

## Why Cadence Is the Only Solution to Reactive Capacity Management

Ad-hoc capacity planning has a structural failure mode: it is triggered by signals that are already late. A Splunk alert fires at 80% CPU utilization. A capacity ticket opens. The team scrambles to provision more resources. But provisioning takes time — especially for databases, carrier agreements, and large compute expansions. By the time the reactive process completes, you may have already been in a degraded state for days, or the utilization may have crossed 90% before new capacity comes online.

The solution is not better alerting. You cannot alert yourself out of a process that is fundamentally reactive. The solution is cadence: a recurring review on a fixed schedule, with defined inputs, defined decisions, and defined owners. A capacity review process that runs whether or not there is an active alert transforms capacity planning from incident response into infrastructure stewardship.

The cadence also forces a forward-looking posture. A monthly review asks "where will we be in 60 days?" — not "where are we today?" That 60-day forward view is what gives you the lead time to act before the threshold is crossed.

---

## Monthly Capacity Review

**Purpose**: identify services approaching capacity thresholds within the next 60 days and initiate corrective action before the threshold is crossed.

**Attendees**: on-call leads (one per major service area), infrastructure lead, Director (you). Keep it small — this is an operational review, not a status meeting.

**Duration**: 60 minutes. If it takes longer, the data package is not pre-processed enough.

**Required inputs** (prepared by infrastructure lead before the meeting):
- Capacity signals dashboard showing current headroom by resource and service
- Any services that crossed a threshold since last review (what happened, was it addressed?)
- Any events on the capacity calendar in the next 60 days
- Any capacity incidents since last review with contributing factor analysis

**Decisions made in the meeting**:
- Any service with current utilization crossing 70% on any resource: **immediate capacity action required**. Assign owner, deadline within 30 days, and approval if spend is needed.
- Any events on the capacity calendar in the next 60 days: **pre-provisioning plan required**. What is being provisioned, by when, who approves the spend?
- Any service where utilization trend projects to cross 70% within 45 days: **capacity action required before next review**. Do not wait for the threshold crossing — act on the projection.

**Output — Capacity Action Log**:

| Service | Resource | Current Utilization | Projected 70% Date | Action Required | Owner | Deadline | Approval Status |
|---------|----------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------------|-------|----------|-----------------|
| DND Processor | CPU | 68% | 22 days | Add 2 node pool nodes | [SRE Lead] | [Date] | [Pending/Approved] |
| Cassandra Ring | Disk | 57% | 41 days | Plan node expansion | [DBA Lead] | [Date] | [Pending/Approved] |

The action log is the primary artifact of the monthly review. It is the accountability document — reviewed at the beginning of every subsequent monthly review.

## Quarterly Capacity Review

The quarterly review is a more comprehensive process. Where the monthly review focuses on 60-day operational horizon, the quarterly review updates the full 12-month capacity model, reviews cost trends, and produces decisions that may require VP approval for spend.

**Attendees**: all monthly review attendees, plus: VP of Platform Engineering (for the last 20 minutes, decisions requiring spend approval). The VP does not sit through the full technical discussion — bring them in for the decision segment only.

**Duration**: 90 minutes (see quarterly review playbook for detailed agenda).

**Additional inputs beyond the monthly review**:
- 12-month capacity model update (actuals vs. last quarter's projections — accuracy check)
- Infrastructure cost trend: cloud spend by service, quarter over quarter
- Demand forecast refresh: updated business growth projections from product
- Capacity incidents from last quarter with post-mortem findings

**Additional outputs**:
- Updated 12-month capacity model with new projections
- Quarterly cost trend executive summary (for the VP briefing)
- Capacity investment decisions requiring VP approval (presented in the final 20 minutes)

## Capacity Signals Dashboard — Required Panels

Your capacity signals dashboard is the primary instrument panel for these reviews. It must provide at a glance:

**Panel 1 — Headroom RAG Status**

| Service | CPU | Memory | Disk | Network | Database Connections |
|---------|-----|--------|------|---------|---------------------|
| DND Processor | 🟡 68% | 🟢 52% | 🟢 45% | 🟢 30% | 🟢 40% |
| Cassandra Cluster | — | — | 🟡 57% | 🟢 25% | — |
| API Gateway | 🟢 45% | 🟢 55% | 🟢 30% | 🟡 62% | — |

RAG thresholds: Green <70%, Yellow 70-80%, Red >80%.

**Panel 2 — 90-Day Utilization Trend by Service**

Sparkline or small multiples showing the trend over the past 90 days. Flat or declining trends are stable. Rising trends require projected "action date" calculation. Accelerating trends require immediate attention regardless of current absolute value.

**Panel 3 — Capacity Calendar**

| Event | Date | Affected Service | Traffic Multiplier | Pre-Provision By | Status |
|-------|------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------|
| Summer Sale Campaign | [Date] | DND Processor | 2.5× | [Date] | In Progress |
| New Feature Launch | [Date] | API Gateway | 1.8× | [Date] | Planned |

**Panel 4 — Projected Action Dates**

| Service | Resource | Current % | Growth Rate | Projected 70% Date | Days Remaining |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Cassandra | Disk | 57% | 1.5% / week | [Calculated Date] | 41 days |
| DND Processor | CPU | 68% | Already at threshold | Immediate | 0 days |

This panel is what converts raw utilization data into actionable urgency. A service at 55% but growing 5%/week is more urgent than a service at 65% that has been flat for 90 days.

## Escalation Thresholds

Not every capacity issue waits for the next scheduled review.

| Condition | Response | Timeline |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Any resource projected to cross 70% within 30 days | Immediate capacity action, escalate to Director | Do not wait for next review |
| Any resource at 80% now | Emergency capacity review, Director-convened | Within 48 hours |
| Any resource at 90% now | Incident — activate capacity crisis response playbook | Immediately |
| Any database disk at 65% (Cassandra) | Initiate expansion planning immediately | Same day |

These thresholds must be encoded as monitoring alerts in your observability platform (Splunk alert, Prometheus alertmanager rule) — not just as meeting discussion items.

## Capacity Incident Post-Mortem Analysis

Every capacity-related incident must include a specific analysis in its post-mortem:

1. **Was the capacity signal visible in monitoring?** If yes: when did the signal first appear? If it was visible 30 days before the incident and not acted on, that is a process failure, not a technical failure.
2. **Was the signal acted on?** If it was visible and a capacity action was initiated but not completed in time, why not? Lead time underestimated? Approval process too slow? Execution delayed?
3. **Was the signal not visible?** If the metric that would have predicted the incident was not being monitored, that is a monitoring gap — add the metric and alert.
4. **What process change prevents recurrence?** Not just "add more capacity" — what specific change to the review cadence, alert thresholds, or lead time assumptions prevents the same class of incident?

This analysis transforms individual incidents into process improvements. Over 3-4 quarters, a team that does this analysis rigorously builds a capacity planning process that is genuinely predictive rather than just reactive.

---

## Director Takeaway

Cadence is the mechanism that converts capacity planning from reactive crisis management to proactive infrastructure stewardship. Own the monthly review as a standing calendar commitment — cancel it only if there is a production incident, and reschedule it within the same week. The quarterly review is the mechanism for strategic capacity decisions and VP alignment. The escalation thresholds in your monitoring system are what catch issues between reviews. Together, these three components — monthly cadence, quarterly strategy, continuous alerting — form a complete capacity management practice. Build it once, enforce it as a team standard, and it pays dividends in reliability every quarter.
