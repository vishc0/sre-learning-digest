# Playbook: Quarterly Capacity Review

**Version**: 1.0  
**Purpose**: Quarterly review to update the 12-month capacity model, approve capacity investments, and identify strategic risks  
**Timing**: Last 2 weeks of each quarter (Q1: mid-March, Q2: mid-June, Q3: mid-September, Q4: mid-December)  
**Duration**: 90-minute meeting, plus 1 week of preparation  
**Owner**: Director of Platform Engineering / SRE Manager

---

## Roles

| Role | Who | Time Commitment |
|------|-----|----------------|
| **Facilitator** | Director (you) | Meeting lead, owns the output decisions |
| **Data Lead** | Infrastructure SRE or platform lead | 1 week of pre-work, attends full meeting |
| **Service Leads** | One per major service domain (DND Platform, API Gateway, Data Tier, etc.) | Full meeting attendance |
| **VP of Platform Engineering** | VP | Last 20 minutes only (decision segment) |

---

## Pre-Work: Data Package Preparation

The Data Lead completes all of the following **7 days before the meeting**. The meeting quality is entirely determined by the quality of the data package. A meeting with incomplete data must be rescheduled.

**Step 1 — Export 90-day utilization trends** *(Data Lead)*  
For each resource type (CPU, memory, disk, network, database connections) and each major service:
- Pull from Splunk or Prometheus: daily P95 utilization over the last 90 days
- Calculate: current value, 30-day ago value, 90-day ago value
- Calculate 30-day growth rate: `(Current - 30 days ago) / (30 days ago) × 100`
- Flag any service where the growth rate has changed significantly (positive or negative inflection)

Output format:
```
Service: DND Notification Processor
Resource: CPU
Current P95: 68%
30 days ago: 61%
90 days ago: 52%
30-day growth rate: +11.5%
Trend: Accelerating
```

**Step 2 — Demand forecast accuracy check** *(Data Lead)*  
Retrieve last quarter's demand forecast projections. Compare to actuals.
- For each service: what did we project for this quarter vs what actually happened?
- Calculate forecast error: `(Actual - Projected) / Projected × 100`
- Identify systematic bias: are forecasts consistently too high (over-provisioned) or too low (under-provisioned)?
- Identify outlier events: what caused the largest deviations from forecast?

This accuracy check is critical for improving forecasting methodology. A team that never checks its forecast accuracy never improves.

**Step 3 — Update capacity calendar** *(Data Lead in coordination with Product Manager)*  
Engage product management and marketing at least 3 days before this step to collect:
- Confirmed product launches in the next 2 quarters with expected traffic impact
- Confirmed marketing campaign windows with expected volume multipliers
- Regulatory or compliance milestones that drive user activity
- Any planned platform changes that will affect traffic patterns (migrations, retirements)

Update the capacity calendar template with this information. Flag any event with a traffic multiplier >1.5× as requiring explicit pre-provisioning planning.

**Step 4 — Calculate current headroom** *(Data Lead)*  
For each service and resource type, calculate current headroom:
- Headroom % = `(Provisioned - Current P95 Used) / Provisioned × 100`
- Apply RAG status: Green >30%, Yellow 20-30%, Red <20%
- For Cassandra disk: Green >40%, Yellow 30-40%, Red <30% (tighter thresholds — see ch05)

**Step 5 — Calculate projected action dates** *(Data Lead)*  
For each service with a positive growth rate, calculate when the 70% utilization threshold will be crossed:
- Days Until 70% = `(0.70 × Provisioned - Current Used) / Daily Growth Rate`
- Translate to a calendar date
- Flag any service where this date is within 90 days (required action before next quarterly review)
- Flag any service where this date is within 45 days (required action before end of this quarter)

**Step 6 — Prepare cost trend** *(Data Lead)*  
Pull cloud cost data from AWS Cost Explorer or equivalent:
- Infrastructure spend by service, current quarter vs last quarter vs same quarter last year
- Breakdown: compute (EC2/EKS), database (RDS/Cassandra), storage (S3/EBS), network (egress, data transfer)
- Identify top 3 cost drivers by growth rate (not just by absolute spend)
- Calculate projected annual spend at current growth rate

This cost trend feeds the VP segment of the meeting and the executive summary template.

**Step 7 — Identify capacity-related incidents from last quarter** *(Data Lead)*  
Review all P1/P2 incidents from the last quarter. Flag any where capacity was a contributing factor (disk pressure, CPU saturation, connection exhaustion, OOMKills).
- For each capacity-related incident: what capacity signal was present before the incident? How far in advance?
- Was the signal visible in monitoring? Was it acted on? Why or why not?
- This feeds the retrospective at the beginning of the meeting.

**Data Package Completeness Check** (Data Lead signs off before distributing):
- [ ] 90-day utilization trends for all services
- [ ] Forecast accuracy analysis vs last quarter projections
- [ ] Updated capacity calendar with next 2 quarters of events
- [ ] Current headroom % with RAG status for all services
- [ ] Projected action dates for all growing services
- [ ] Cost trend report with quarterly comparison
- [ ] Capacity-related incidents from last quarter with signal analysis
- [ ] Data package distributed to all attendees 48 hours before meeting

## Meeting Agenda (90 Minutes)

### 0:00 — 0:15 | Retrospective: Last Quarter's Capacity Actions

**Facilitator**: Director  
**Goal**: Accountability check on last quarter's capacity commitments

Walk through last quarter's capacity action log:
- For each action: completed on time? If yes, what headroom was recovered? If no, why was it delayed?
- For each capacity-related incident from last quarter: was the signal visible 30+ days in advance? If yes, was a capacity action initiated but not completed? Identify the process failure.
- What systematic improvement would prevent the same class of incident in this quarter?

This segment should not be punitive — it should be diagnostic. The goal is process improvement, not blame assignment.

### 0:15 — 0:45 | Service-by-Service Headroom Review

**Facilitator**: Director  
**Goal**: Identify all services requiring capacity action this quarter

Format: go through the headroom dashboard service by service. For each service:

1. Data Lead presents: current headroom by resource, 90-day trend, projected action date
2. Service Lead confirms or disputes the numbers (they know their service best)
3. Group agrees on RAG status
4. If Yellow or Red on any resource: assign a capacity action

For Yellow status (headroom 20-30%):
- Service Lead owns a capacity action plan by end of quarter
- Director approval for spend up to a defined threshold (e.g., $5K/month additional spend)

For Red status (headroom <20%):
- Immediate capacity action required — plan due within 2 weeks, not end of quarter
- VP approval may be required depending on spend level (see escalation matrix)

> Decision gate: Any service at Red status on disk (or >65% for Cassandra disk) must have a capacity action plan before this meeting ends. Do not defer a disk-critical service to the next review cycle.

### 0:45 — 1:05 | Forward-Looking: Capacity Calendar and Projected Action Dates

**Facilitator**: Director  
**Goal**: Ensure pre-provisioning plans exist for all events and approaching thresholds

For each event on the capacity calendar in the next 2 quarters:
1. What is the expected traffic multiplier?
2. What capacity pre-provisioning is required?
3. What is the pre-provisioning deadline (must be done before the event)?
4. Who owns the pre-provisioning action?

For each service where the action date is within 90 days:
1. Confirm the growth rate assumption (is it still valid, or has something changed?)
2. What capacity action is required?
3. What is the target completion date (must be before the projected 70% crossing)?
4. Who owns the action?

Output: add all items to the capacity action log with owners and deadlines.

### 1:05 — 1:20 | Decisions: Capacity Actions and Spend Approval

**Facilitator**: Director  
**Goal**: Finalize capacity action log with spend decisions

Consolidate all capacity actions identified in the previous two segments. For each action:
- Estimate cost impact: one-time provisioning cost + monthly recurring cost increase
- Classify: within Director approval authority (<$X/month) or requires VP approval (>$X/month)
- Confirm owner and deadline

Triage: actions within Director authority are approved in this meeting. Actions requiring VP authority are queued for the VP segment.

### 1:20 — 1:30 | VP Briefing

**Facilitator**: Director  
**Attendee**: VP of Platform Engineering (joins at the 1:20 mark)  
**Goal**: Present approved capacity actions and obtain VP sign-off on spend requiring executive approval

Prepare a 3-slide / 3-minute summary for the VP:
1. **Current state**: overall headroom status (RAG summary across all services). One sentence: "We are healthy in 8 of 11 service dimensions; two require action this quarter."
2. **Actions approved within Director authority**: list with cost impact. Informational — not asking for approval.
3. **Actions requiring VP approval**: present using the expected value framework (see ch08). For each: cost of investment, probability of incident without it, estimated incident cost, net expected value. Ask for explicit approval.

The VP's job in this meeting is not to review technical details — it is to approve spend decisions that are above the Director's authority and to be informed of the overall state of platform capacity.

## Output Artifacts

**1. Updated Capacity Model**  
Data Lead updates the `templates/capacity-model-template.md` with:
- New current inventory values (post-review)
- Updated demand forecast (incorporating accuracy correction from Step 2)
- Updated capacity calendar
- New capacity action log entries

Distribute to all attendees within 24 hours of the meeting.

**2. Capacity Action Log** (updated with all decisions from the meeting)

| Service | Resource | Action | Owner | Deadline | Monthly Cost Impact | Approval Status |
|---------|----------|--------|-------|----------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| DND Processor | CPU | Add 3 EKS node pool nodes | [SRE Lead] | [Date] | +$830/mo | Approved — Director |
| Cassandra | Disk | Add 2 nodes to ring | [Database Lead] | [Date] | +$1,200/mo | Approved — VP |

**3. Executive Summary**  
Director completes `templates/capacity-executive-summary-template.md` for the most significant capacity investments. Distribute to VP within 48 hours of the meeting.

## Decision Gates Summary

| Situation | Decision | Escalation |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Headroom Green, no events | No action, document | — |
| Headroom Yellow, no event in 45 days | Service Lead owns action plan this quarter | Director approval if spend within authority |
| Headroom Yellow, event in 45 days | Immediate pre-provisioning plan | Director approval or VP if above threshold |
| Headroom Red on compute | Capacity action due in 2 weeks | Director approval or VP if above threshold |
| Disk >65% on Cassandra | Capacity action plan in this meeting | VP approval likely required for multi-node expansion |
| Service at 80%+ on any resource | Capacity crisis — activate crisis response playbook | Immediate escalation |
