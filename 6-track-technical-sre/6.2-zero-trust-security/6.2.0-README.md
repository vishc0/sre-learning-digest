# Zero Trust Security — Director/VP Platform Engineering Interview Framework

## What This Framework Is

Zero Trust is not a product you buy. It is an architectural posture you design, phase, and operate. This framework gives you the vocabulary, decision models, and operational depth to speak credibly about Zero Trust at the Director/VP level — not as an implementer explaining mechanics, but as an architect explaining trade-offs and business outcomes.

This framework is built for Vishweshwar Chippa, SRE Manager targeting Director/VP Platform Engineering. It assumes production experience with EKS/Kubernetes, Vault, CyberArk, Splunk, and incident command. It extends that experience into Zero Trust architecture and leadership vocabulary.

---

## Why Zero Trust Is P1 for Director Interviews

The numbers are no longer future-state projections:

- **47% of Kubernetes production clusters** had a service mesh deployed in 2025 (CNCF Annual Survey)
- **mTLS and SPIFFE** are now standard Director-level interview topics at companies running any meaningful cloud-native footprint
- The **CISA Zero Trust Maturity Model v2.0** (2023) is the reference document federal contractors and large enterprises use — knowing it signals architectural fluency
- **Regulatory drivers** (FedRAMP High, PCI-DSS v4.0, NIST SP 800-207) now explicitly require Zero Trust architecture documentation
- Companies that had high-profile supply chain or lateral movement incidents (SolarWinds, Okta, CircleCI) are actively rebuilding their trust models — the Director who can speak to the rebuild architecture wins the role

At the Director level, the interview question is never "how does mTLS work." It is "how do you phase a Zero Trust program for a platform with 300 services and a mixed legacy/cloud-native estate, and how do you sell the investment to the CFO." This framework trains that conversation.

## Director-Level Framing: The One-Paragraph Elevator Answer

If an interviewer asks "Tell me your philosophy on Zero Trust" — this is the answer structure:

> "Zero Trust is an architectural posture, not a product. My starting point is always identity — workload identity first, human identity second — because every other control layer depends on knowing exactly who or what is making a request. I phase programs through three layers: network segmentation to reduce blast radius, identity and mTLS to authenticate every hop, then policy-as-code to enforce least privilege at the application layer. The observability layer is not last — it runs from day one, because the audit log is the proof that your controls are actually working. The hardest part of any ZT program is not the technology — it is closing the gap between 'we have the capability' and 'every workload uses it.' That gap is an organizational and operational problem, not an architecture problem, and it requires sustained engineering investment to close."

That answer demonstrates: architectural framing, operational realism, leadership awareness, and business judgment. That is what separates a Director candidate from a senior engineer in an interview.
