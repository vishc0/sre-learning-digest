# Playbook: Monthly FinOps Review

**Frequency**: Monthly, within first 5 business days after month-end  
**Duration**: 45–60 minutes  
**Facilitator**: FinOps engineer (or Platform/SRE lead)  
**Attendees**: Engineering leads (one per team), Platform/SRE Director, FinOps engineer. Finance optional. VP Engineering every quarter.

---

## Pre-Meeting Preparation (3–5 days before)

### Step 1: Pull the Cost Data

AWS billing typically finalizes 24–48 hours after month-end. Wait for the final bill before pulling data.

**Data to pull**:

```
Cost Explorer extracts:
1. Total monthly spend vs budget (by payer account)
2. Month-over-month change (current month vs prior month, absolute and %)
3. Top 10 services by spend
4. Top 5 teams by spend (by `team` tag)
5. Savings Plan coverage and utilization report
6. Reserved Instance coverage and utilization report
7. Cost Anomaly Detection alerts fired this month (with resolution status)

Kubecost extracts:
8. Total cluster cost by namespace (top 10)
9. Cluster idle cost (% and $)
10. Top 5 over-provisioned deployments (by CPU efficiency < 50%)
11. Cost per [unit] metric (calculate from CUR + business metrics)
```

### Step 2: Prepare the Dashboard Slide

One slide / document with these numbers at the top, visible without scrolling:

| Metric | This Month | Last Month | Budget | Status |
|--------|-----------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Total spend | $X | $X | $X | Green/Yellow/Red |
| M/M change | +/-X% | — | — | — |
| Savings Plan coverage | X% | X% | ≥70% | — |
| Savings Plan utilization | X% | X% | ≥90% | — |
| Tag coverage | X% | X% | ≥95% | — |
| Cluster idle cost | $X (X%) | $X | <20% | — |
| Cost per [unit] | $X | $X | Target | — |

Color coding: Green = within target. Yellow = 10–20% outside target. Red = >20% outside target.

### Step 3: Identify Discussion Items

From the data, prepare 3–5 discussion items. These are the interesting things: anomalies that were resolved, optimizations that landed (or did not), teams approaching budget limits, upcoming RI expirations.

Structure each item as: **What happened → Why it matters → What we did or are going to do.**

### Step 4: Prepare Action Item Status Update

Pull the action items from last month's review. For each:
- Completed: note result and cost impact
- In progress: note current status and expected completion
- Blocked: note what is blocking and what help is needed

## Meeting Agenda (60-minute version)

### Section 1: Month Summary (5 minutes)

Facilitator presents the dashboard. Do not read out every number — the attendees can see them. Highlight:
- Total spend and budget status
- Biggest month-over-month change (up or down)
- One headline: "This month's story is..."

**No discussion in this section** — just data. Discussion comes in the next sections.

### Section 2: Top Cost Drivers (15 minutes)

Present top 5 services and top 5 teams by spend change (not just absolute spend — the changes are more interesting).

For each item with significant change:
- "Payment team EC2 spend increased 23% — [team lead name], can you walk us through what drove that?"
- "Cassandra EBS increased $800 — this is the data archival expansion we approved last quarter. On track."

**Goal**: Every significant number has an attributed cause by the end of this section. Unattributed changes become action items.

### Section 3: Anomaly Review (10 minutes)

Present a summary of any cost anomaly alerts that fired this month:
- How many alerts?
- Which were true positives vs noise?
- What was the root cause and resolution for each true positive?
- Is there a detection-to-resolution trend (getting faster or slower)?

If no anomalies: note this. It either means the platform is stable, or the alert thresholds are too insensitive. Briefly validate thresholds.

### Section 4: Optimization Actions (15 minutes)

**Prior actions**: Quick status update — what is done, what is in progress.

**New actions**: Propose specific optimizations for the next month. Structure:
```
Optimization: Right-size notification-worker deployment (VPA recommendation)
Estimated savings: $340/month
Owner: [name]
Timeline: 2 weeks
Risk: Low (stateless, replicated)
```

The goal: leave the meeting with 2–3 concrete optimization actions with named owners and deadlines, not a list of vague ideas.

### Section 5: Commitments Review (10 minutes)

**Savings Plan status**:
- Current coverage: X% (target ≥ 70%)
- Current utilization: X% (target ≥ 90%)
- Any expiring in next 90 days? If yes, add RI analysis as next-month action.

**RI status** (if applicable):
- Utilization by instance family
- Any underutilized RIs (< 85%): flag for possible marketplace sale or convertible exchange

**New purchase decision**: If Savings Plan coverage is below 60%, propose an incremental purchase. Size it based on the last 30-day baseline usage, not peak.

### Section 6: Next Actions and Close (5 minutes)

Read back the action items from this meeting:
```
Action: [description]
Owner: [name]
Due: [date]
Expected outcome: [$ or metric impact]
```

Confirm next meeting date. End on time.

## Post-Meeting (within 24 hours)

### Step 1: Publish the Summary

Send to all attendees + Engineering Director + (if relevant) Finance:

```
FinOps Monthly Review Summary — [Month Year]

Total spend: $X vs $Y budget ([Z]% variance)
M/M change: +/-$X (+/-Y%)

Key findings:
- [Finding 1]
- [Finding 2]
- [Finding 3]

Action items:
- [Owner]: [Action] by [Date]
- [Owner]: [Action] by [Date]

Next review: [Date]
```

### Step 2: Update the Action Tracker

Log all new action items in the FinOps tracker (Jira, Confluence, or shared spreadsheet):
- Action description
- Owner
- Due date
- Expected $ impact
- Status: Open

### Step 3: Archive the Dashboard

Save the monthly dashboard snapshot to the FinOps archive. This is your trend data for quarterly and annual reviews.

---

## Quarterly Director Review (Every Third Monthly Review)

On the third month of each quarter, expand the agenda by 15 minutes for:

1. **Quarterly trend review**: 3-month cost trend, vs quarterly budget, vs same quarter last year
2. **Maturity assessment**: Where are we on the Crawl/Walk/Run scale? What moved?
3. **Roadmap alignment**: Are the planned optimizations on track? Adjust if not.
4. **VP Engineering briefing**: Prepare a 5-minute verbal summary of the quarter for the VP. Bring the one-page FinOps executive summary (see template).
