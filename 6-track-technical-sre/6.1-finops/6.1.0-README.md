# FinOps for SRE — Director-Level Framework

**Domain**: Technical SRE  
**Audience**: SRE Manager targeting Director/VP Platform Engineering  
**Prerequisite frameworks**: Kubernetes Operations, Incident Command, Observability

---

## Why This Framework Exists

A Director who cannot explain unit economics, justify cloud spend to a CFO, or build a FinOps practice from the ground up is incomplete for the role. FinOps is not accounting — it is the engineering discipline of making cloud cost visible, attributable, and optimizable. For an SRE Director, it closes the loop between reliability and economics: you cannot justify a redundant control plane, a warm standby region, or a Kubernetes upgrade investment without translating it into dollars per unit of availability.

This framework teaches you to think in cost the way you already think in latency: as a signal, not just a bill.

## Key Benchmarks (use these in interviews)

- FinOps Foundation 2024: organizations at **Run** maturity save **25–35%** vs Crawl. This is your ROI anchor for every FinOps investment conversation.
- Kubernetes right-sizing alone typically yields **15–25%** EC2 cost reduction in over-provisioned clusters.
- Reserved Instance + Savings Plan coverage at 70% of baseline compute typically yields **30–40%** discount vs on-demand.
- The "cost of a 9": each additional nine of availability (99.9% → 99.99%) typically requires 10x more redundancy investment. Knowing the dollar figure for your platform is a Director-level differentiator.

## Connecting to Your SRE Practice

FinOps connects directly to your existing SRE disciplines:

| SRE Concept | FinOps Equivalent |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Error budget | Cost budget — how much overage is acceptable before escalation |
| SLO | Cost SLO — e.g., "cost per transaction < $0.0008" |
| Capacity planning | RI/Savings Plan commitment modeling |
| Incident response | Cost anomaly response — same triage discipline |
| Observability | Cost observability — tagging as the "instrumentation" of spend |

The mental model: **tagging is to cost what instrumentation is to latency.** If it is not tagged, you cannot see it. If you cannot see it, you cannot optimize it.
