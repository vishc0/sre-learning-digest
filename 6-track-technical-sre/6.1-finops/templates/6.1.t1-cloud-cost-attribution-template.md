### Proportional Allocation Rules

For costs that cannot be attributed by tagging (data transfer aggregates, support fees, marketplace subscriptions):

| Cost Category | Allocation Method | Proportional Basis |
|--------------|------------------|------------------|
| AWS Support plan | Proportional | Each team's % of tagged EC2 spend |
| Inter-AZ data transfer | Proportional | Each team's % of EC2 spend in affected AZ |
| Shared services account | Direct tag + proportional remainder | Tag where possible; proportional for overhead |

Document the allocation method so it is auditable and consistent month over month.

## Section 7: Disputed Cost Resolution Process

When a team disputes their cost attribution ("this charge doesn't belong to us"), follow this process:

### Step 1: Log the Dispute

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Disputed amount | $[X] |
| Disputing team | [Team name] |
| Service/resource disputed | [ARN or service name] |
| Reason for dispute | [e.g., "This EC2 instance was provisioned by the platform team on our behalf"] |
| Date logged | [Date] |

### Step 2: Investigate

- Pull resource tags from CUR or AWS Console
- Check CloudTrail for who provisioned the resource (IAM principal)
- Check the namespace registry (for K8s resources) for ownership mapping
- Interview both teams if ownership is ambiguous

### Step 3: Resolve and Document

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Resolution | [e.g., "EC2 instance confirmed as platform-managed, re-attributed to platform team"] |
| Attribution change | $[X] moved from [Team A] to [Team B] |
| Tagging fix applied | [yes/no — describe] |
| Process improvement to prevent recurrence | [e.g., "Added ownership tag requirement to shared-service provisioning runbook"] |

### Step 4: Retroactive Adjustment

For disputes involving > $500 in prior months: apply retroactive adjustment to the prior month's attribution in the tracking table. Document the adjustment with a note.
